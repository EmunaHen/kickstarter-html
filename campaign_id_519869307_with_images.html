Story

What are we raising funds to do?

Tendaji is raising funds to manufacture and launch the world's first solar-powered wireless charging jacket. This jacket combines cutting-edge green engineering, fashion, and technology to keep people connected on the go without relying on conventional power sources. With its sleek design and sustainable functionality, the jacket is designed to empower users while reducing their carbon footprint.

Why do we care about it?

Tendaji was born out of a shared vision to address the global need for sustainability and convenience. As young innovators from Hampton University, we turned our Senior Electrical Engineering Design Project into a mission to make eco-friendly technology more accessible. "Tendaji" means "to make things happen" in Swahili, and this ethos drives our passion to bring groundbreaking, user-friendly products to life while supporting environmental preservation.

How do we plan to make it happen?

We’ve spent years perfecting the design and technology of the jacket, leveraging tools like CLO3D for design and Arduino for software development. With hardware and software tested and validated, the funds raised will be used for:

  • Manufacturing the first batch of jackets
  • Marketing to introduce the product to our audience
  • Finalizing logistics for delivery to our backers

Who are we?

Tendaji is led by Daryl Riley Jr., Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer, alongside a talented team of innovators: Stanton Ridley (Co-Founder & Chief Design Officer), Jordan Walls (Co-Founder & Chief Technical Officer), and Kagame Li-A-Ping (Chief Financial Officer). Our journey began during the pandemic, and despite facing challenges like layoffs, burnout, and personal losses, we’ve persevered. Our work has been recognized by Essence Magazine, Forbes, Black Ambition, and beVisioneers: the Mercedes-Benz Fellowship, and we remain committed to redefining the intersection of fashion and technology.

Risks and challenges

Manufacturing Delays Producing a new type of wearable tech comes with challenges, such as delays in sourcing sustainable materials or ensuring the highest quality standards for production. We’ve mitigated these risks by partnering with experienced manufacturers and securing contingency plans for material sourcing. Hardware and Software Integration The jacket’s wireless charging and solar-powered features rely on precise engineering. While prototypes have been tested successfully, scaling production could introduce unforeseen technical challenges. Our team of engineers and designers is prepared to troubleshoot and resolve these issues quickly. Shipping and Fulfillment Global supply chain disruptions may affect delivery timelines. To address this, we’ve established partnerships with trusted fulfillment centers and plan to maintain open communication with backers to keep them informed of progress. Funding Shortfall If the campaign falls short of its funding goal, we’ll pivot by exploring alternative fundraising options, such as strategic partnerships and venture capital. Team Capacity As a startup team, we are balancing multiple responsibilities. However, we’re supported by interns and a growing network of advisors to help us execute effectively. Our determination, combined with years of preparation, ensures we are equipped to tackle these challenges and deliver an innovative product that transforms how people interact with technology and fashion.

Environmental commitments

Long-lasting design

Tendaji jackets are designed with durability in mind, ensuring they last through multiple seasons of wear. The integration of high quality materials and advanced technology minimizes the need for frequent replacements, reducing waste.

Reusability and recyclability

Key components, including the solar cells and electronics, are designed for reusability or recycling. Tendaji is committed to offering repair services to prolong the life-cycle of each jacket and encouraging sustainable consumption.

Sustainable materials

Tendaji integrates eco-conscious materials such as: recycled polyester* and organic cotton* into the jacket's design. This reduces dependency on virgin resources and lowers the environmental impact.

Environmentally friendly factories

Manufacturing partners are selected based on their adherence to environmentally friendly practices, including energy efficient processes and waste reduction protocols.

Something else

Green engineering innovation; Tendaji solar-powered wireless charging feature promotes renewable energy use and decreases reliance on non-renewable electricity. Decarbonization - By integrating solar technology and designing an energy efficient production process, Tendaji actively contributes to reducing carbon emissions. This aligns with the broader goal of decarbonization in fashion technology.
