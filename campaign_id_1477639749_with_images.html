🧱How We Built Case For Vision

Our journey from a simple idea in 2013 to a patented, fully functional solution helping people regain independence, powered by the devices they already own.

In 2013, we started with a simple but powerful idea: 

  • What if assistive devices for people with vision loss didn’t have to be bulky, expensive, or outdated? 
  • What if we could replace proprietary single-use magnifiers and CCTVs with something people already carry every day—their smartphone or tablet?

Over the past decade, we have witnessed iPhones and iPads evolve into powerful tools equipped with stunning cameras and impressive processing power. It became clear that we could finally bring this vision to life.

That’s how Case For Vision was born.

Design Concepts From 2019

It’s a patented protective case and an intuitive app that transforms an iPhone or iPad into a powerful visual aid for reading up close and seeing clearly at a distance. With a built-in optical mirror and legs that position the camera at the right angle, users can go hands-free while reading mail, product labels, or documents. Flip the mirror up, and your device becomes a distance magnifier—ideal for classrooms, meetings, or seeing a sign across the street.

Our software is designed to be simple—no steep learning curve, no cluttered settings. Just four intuitive controls are located in the four corners of a phone’s screen. Users can zoom, pan live images, adjust contrast, freeze or save images, and even record video—all within one app. And because we leverage the power of the iPhone or iPad, users can still run their favorite accessibility apps on the same device.

The result? A reliable, portable, and affordable solution that empowers people with vision loss to read, learn, and live more independently, without needing to purchase expensive proprietary equipment.

We started with a single iPhone model, the iPhone 15 Pro. Now, we support multiple iPhones and iPads. Prices are under $300 for iPhone-compatible models and under $500 for iPad setups, with no subscriptions and a lifetime hardware warranty.

Just a peek into the future of Case For Vision 👀

This isn’t a concept. It’s real, it works, and people are already using it. Now we're looking to fund our plan to expand from iPhone and iPad to Android, refining our software, and launching new products like the CFV MagSafe and CFV Ergo. 

With your help, we can make the Case For Vision solution, a combination of hardware and software, available to visually impaired people using Android smartphones, as well as to those with age-related vision loss. 

Thank you for supporting us—and for helping others see what matters most.

🛠️ How It Works

From Close-Up Reading to Distance Viewing—It’s That Easy

✅ Step #1 – Add Case & Unfold Legs

Attach the Case For Vision in seconds—no tools, no hassle. Unfold the adjustable legs to instantly create a stable, hands-free stand. Whether you’re sitting at your desk or in a café, the lightweight design makes it easy to set up anywhere.

✅ Step #2 – Access Our FREE iOS App & Place Materials Underneath Camera

Download our FREE app "Case For Vision" from the App Store and simply place any material under the camera—books, mail, medication labels, menus, even crafts and small electronics. Pinch to zoom, tap to focus, and easily switch between reading and saving important information.

✅ Step #3 – Slide Out Lens To Telescope or Begin Distance Viewing

Effortlessly transition from reading to distance viewing. Slide out the lens to see presentation screens, classroom whiteboards, or signs across the room, without ever having to move your device. Perfect for students, professionals, and on-the-go use.

✅ Step #4 – Enjoy Hands-Free Distance Viewing

Sit back, relax, and experience true hands-free independence. Whether you’re following a lecture, attending a meeting, or reading at home, Case For Vision keeps your hands free and your focus exactly where it should be.

Risks and challenges

While we’ve made significant progress in product development and early production, like any hardware and software-based project, there are potential risks and challenges we are actively working to mitigate. 📌 Manufacturing & Supply Chain Risks We are producing high-quality molded products and accessories, and while we have successfully completed production runs for the iPhone and iPad cases, as well as the OCR Reader accessory, there is always a risk of supply chain delays or production setbacks. To minimize this, we’ve established relationships with trusted manufacturing partners and built in buffer time for delivery. 📌 Software Development Risks We are currently expanding the Case For Vision app and developing new features, including OCR text-to-speech and Android compatibility. While our app is stable and fully functional on iOS, new updates and future development may require additional time for testing and optimization. We have a dedicated team working on these updates and plan to keep all backers informed of progress. 📌 Shipping & Fulfillment Risks As a growing company, handling large-scale order fulfillment will be a new challenge. We’ve carefully planned our logistics and will use experienced fulfillment partners. Shipping fees will be collected post-campaign to ensure accurate calculations and smooth delivery. 📌 Market Expansion Challenges We’re entering a competitive space traditionally dominated by larger companies with more established distribution channels. However, we believe our unique value proposition—affordability, portability, and independence—positions us strongly for success. We’re also focused on building partnerships with organizations that serve the visually impaired to support broader adoption. How We Plan to Overcome These Challenges: - Maintaining transparent communication with our backers through regular updates. - Working with trusted manufacturing and fulfillment partners to ensure quality and timely delivery. - Gradually rolling out app updates to ensure stability before wide deployment. - Using the funds raised to support scalable production and expand our development team as needed.
