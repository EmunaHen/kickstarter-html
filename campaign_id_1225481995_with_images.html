Story

roons is a kit for building mechanical computers -- think "Minecraft redstone in real life". (This is a comparison only; we're not affiliated with Minecraft!)

Combine logic gates and memory blocks to invent your own devices — transistors, processors, and even fully programmable computers.

roons is Turing complete, meaning you can build any kind of computer circuit. But we didn't stop there -- we've made sure it's actually practical, compact, and fun to play with.

Need more room to build? Connect multiple drives together for an endlessly extendable workspace.

Want to build something new? The modular disk system lets you save your work and load a new disk in seconds.

Everything you build is tactile and physical -- you can literally hold the individual bits and bytes in your hands.

No hidden wires or electronics -- see (and touch!) every computation.

roons is now compatible with your favourite bricks, including Cogo Building System, Wange Block, roons, and Buckleburt's Big Brickz -- or even weird niche ones like Lego!

Need a special mechanism for your pattern, or just want to add some decoration? Don't wait for us -- just build it with blocks you already own!

roons’ compact design lets you take it anywhere — stick it in your pocket, put it on a tree.

roons circuits are tiny and space-efficient. An XOR gate measures just 1.6cm x 0.8cm, and you can condense a binary adder into a space the size of a postage stamp.

Our free interactive tutorials walk you through the fundamentals of computing. Learn what bits really are, and how to control them — then work up to building full-on computer components.

You don't need to know anything about computers to get started, but by the end you'll know at least some things. Impress your friends and afear your enemies with your newfound mastery of computing.

Built something cool? Upload your designs to our free online simulator to show them off to the world. Combine and remix other people's creations in unlimited combinations. (Note: the simulator is currently an alpha version, with the 1.0 release scheduled for December.)

People have built some amazing mechanical computers -- out of dominoes, paper, and even water. A couple of years ago, we got the itch to build our own. Then we thought -- why not let anyone build their own computer?

We realised there weren't really any practical, compact, powerful kits out there, so we set out to build one. After dozens of approaches we hit upon the current design, which we call a loom automaton. The loom keeps things as simple and general as possible -- we don't need lots of different gadgets and components, just different tiles to place on the loom.

After another year of iterating and polishing the design, we launched our Kickstarter, which immediately reached 100,000% of its funding goal and we all lived happily ever after. (Note: whether this part is accurate depends on you!)

Risks and challenges

There's a lot we need to get right to get roons into your hands: production, storage, packaging, shipping, regulations, and a billion other things. We've been manufacturing parts like this for 3+ years, but this is our first Kickstarter campaign. Delivering roons on this scale opens up a whole new realm of challenges. To mitigate any supply chain risk, we plan to manufacture everything in the UK. Some components are low enough quantity that we can manufacture them in-house. However, the higher-quantity components (i.e. the roons tiles) require a more flexible strategy. At low levels of backing, we'll balance production across print farms and in-house production; at higher levels, we have injection moulding partners prepared to help us achieve scale. We've also invented our own automated post-processing tools which minimise the number of 3rd parties we have to rely on. We've overcome a ton of challenges to reach this point, and we know there'll be many more ahead -- we'll keep you updated regularly on our progress, and make sure you know exactly where we are.
