Story

Bhlen: Revolutionizing Local Recommendations

Overview:

Welcome to the Bhlen Kickstarter campaign! We’re excited to showcase Bhlen (pronounced / blen /), a digital guide platform that helps hotels, cities, and event organizers share local tips and hotspots through mobile-friendly maps—no app needed. From restaurant picks to event schedules, Bhlen delivers curated experiences that guests actually use.

Whether you’re a traveler or a local, Bhlen simplifies how you explore and connect with your surroundings.

Our Mission:

Our mission is to revolutionize local exploration by delivering seamless, personalized digital guides. Bhlen empowers travelers, locals, underrepresented communities and partners in hospitality, education, and more, enabling them to enhance awareness and customer satisfaction while connecting with the best local experiences.

Our Vision:

We envision a world where discovering new places is effortless. Bhlen is on a mission to become the go-to digital guide for the world’s biggest events, from large conferences to major sporting events, revolutionizing how attendees connect with local experiences and creating unforgettable moments.

The Problem:

Bhlen was created in response to the growing need for easily accessible and relevant local information, whether in your hometown, a frequently visited area, or while traveling. Traditionally, this information is scattered across static formats—printed brochures, PDFs, or even digital lists—requiring individuals to manually search each venue or event separately to determine its location relative to where they are.

Our Solution:

Our solution eliminates that inefficiency by transforming static data into dynamic, curated digital guides. With Bhlen, venues, events, and points of interest are grouped into a single interactive guide, enhanced with geolocation features that display relevant locations in order of proximity to the user’s current location. Users can also view this information in an itinerary format, making it effortless to navigate their plans and explore their surroundings without endless searching.

Social Impact: 

Bhlen is more than a platform—it’s a tool for empowering communities. Over the years, rap icons and other entrepreneurs have made incredible efforts to create apps that uplift and highlight Black-owned businesses, paving the way for innovation and progress. At Bhlen, we’re proud to continue that legacy by expanding the vision to support all underserved communities.

Our socially inspired Digital Guides go beyond simple recommendations; they serve as a bridge, connecting people to diverse businesses, events, and resources that champion inclusion and community growth. Whether you're looking for Black-owned restaurants, LGBTQ+ safe spaces, or Hispanic heritage landmarks, Bhlen makes it easy to discover, engage, and support minority-owned businesses—right from your phone.

Bhlen Company Timeline

2014 – Bhlen Founded
Bhlen was founded with a focus on connecting users to local events and venues year-round, setting the stage for its future platform.

2015-2016 – Platform Development
Bhlen focused on creating the platform and collecting data on restaurants, bars, and local events to support the platform’s functionality.

2017-2019 – Pilot Phase and Platform Design
Bhlen piloted its platform with early partners, refining its functionality, and began building key relationships. The focus then shifted to platform design and targeting its audience in the hospitality industry.

2020-2024 – COVID-19 and Database Update
During the pandemic, Bhlen revamped its database and prepared for post-pandemic operations.

2024-2025 – Relaunch, Expansion, and Events
Bhlen relaunched with Bhlen Links, actively expanding into new markets, and began hosting major events like Black History Month and World Pride in the DMV area.

Risks and challenges

Many underrepresented businesses lack online visibility or up-to-date information, making it difficult to verify details and maintain accurate listings. Additionally, some small businesses are hesitant to adopt digital platforms, requiring extra outreach and education to encourage participation. Maintaining sustainability and engagement is another challenge, as digital guides need frequent updates. Businesses may close or change ownership, leading to outdated information. Gentrification and displacement can also impact historically underrepresented businesses, reducing their presence and making long-term community engagement more difficult. Ensuring authentic representation without tokenization requires careful collaboration with business owners. Encouraging businesses to see monetization opportunities in digital guides can also be a challenge, especially for those hesitant about advertising costs or revenue-sharing models. Lastly, driving user adoption is key, as many consumers default to mainstream platforms like Google, Yelp, or OpenTable when searching for restaurants and bars. Differentiating Bhlen’s value in supporting minority-owned businesses will require strong marketing efforts and outreach.

Something else

Reducing Paper Waste: By digitizing local recommendations, Bhlen eliminates over 500,000 printed pages annually across hotels and businesses. This significantly reduces the need for paper, transportation, and disposal, conserving natural resources.
