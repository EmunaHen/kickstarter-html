Story

The Story: Driving the Future of STEM Through Motorsports

At Virtual2Reality.tv, we believe that motorsports is more than just racing—it’s a vehicle for education, innovation, and opportunity. Our STEM in Motorsports program is designed to prepare middle and high school students for real-world careers in engineering, physics, design, marketing, and more—by letting them experience the excitement of competitive racing from the classroom.

High School Program: Real Racing, Real Roles, Real Results

We’re launching a full-scale STEM racing initiative for high schools that will run through a network of arrive-and-drive sim racing centers, along with schools that receive their own simulators. These are not just driving games—students will form full-fledged sim racing teams, taking on roles such as:

Drivers

Mechanical Engineers

Aerodynamics Specialists

Race Strategists (Mathematicians)

Team Managers

Marketing & Sponsorship Coordinators

Each school will be assigned a virtual car and a racetrack. For one month, their teams will engineer, test, and perfect their vehicle and strategy. Then, they’ll compete head-to-head online with other high schools across North America. Our long-term goal is to help these students transition into Formula SAE college racing programs and STEM-based collegiate scholarships.

Middle School Program: Where Curiosity Meets Competition

To support this pipeline of future talent, we’ve developed a middle school program centered around the Kyosho Mini-Z RC Car platform—chosen for its perfect balance of speed, drivability, and technical depth. These 1/28th scale cars offer a world of engineering learning, with adjustable:

Ride Height

Suspension Stiffness

Camber & Toe Angles

Gear Ratios

RWD vs AWD configurations

Custom liveries (paint jobs) for team identity

The racing happens on modular RCP foam tracks, which snap together like a puzzle. Kids learn more than just driving—they learn discipline, responsibility, and teamwork. Each session, they’re responsible for setting up and storing the track. If they rush the setup or don’t treat it with care? Their track gets bumpy—and their lap times will suffer. It’s a direct lesson in how precision and preparation affect performance.

In time, we’ll link these middle schools together for national time-attack challenges, where track quality, car tuning, and team effort all determine success.

Why It Matters

These programs are age-appropriate, immersive, and team-focused, providing an unforgettable way to learn about STEM through the thrill of motorsports. And thanks to our unique funding model—which includes Crowd Sourced Marketing (CSM) and Affiliate Marketing Programs—we’re able to lower costs for schools and provide real ROI opportunities for our sponsors and partners.

This campaign will help us deliver the equipment, training, and materials schools need to get started. From racing simulators and RC kits to tracks, tools, timing loops, and spare parts—we’re building the foundation for the next generation of engineers, thinkers, and drivers.

Risks and challenges

Risks and Challenges While we're confident in the success of this program, here are a few challenges we’re prepared to navigate: School Adoption Timing: School approval processes can be slow, and some districts may take longer to join. We’re addressing this by starting with schools already in discussions and hosting live demos to accelerate adoption. Shipping & Fulfillment: RC cars, tracks, and tools come from multiple vendors. We’ve pre-identified reliable suppliers and will bulk-order kits early to prevent delays. Student Engagement: Keeping students engaged long-term requires good structure. That’s why we’ve built a full curriculum with monthly racing goals, rotating team roles, and competition between schools. Track Maintenance & Care: Track wear can impact race quality. We teach students to maintain their equipment and include spare pieces in each kit for replacements. We’ve been testing these ideas for years and have the infrastructure and partners in place. Your support will help us scale responsibly and give students an unforgettable STEM experience.

Long-lasting design

How will you maximize the life of what you’re making? We’re designing this program to be sustainable, modular, and upgradeable—maximizing both longevity and educational value. Durable Equipment: The Kyosho Mini-Z cars and RCP Tracks were chosen because of their proven durability in club and competitive environments. These are not toys—they're performance-grade and built to last with proper care. Modular Components: Every kit includes spare parts, tuning tools, and easily replaceable components so students can repair and maintain their own vehicles. We teach maintenance as part of the curriculum—giving the equipment a longer life while teaching real-world responsibility. Reusable Tracks: The RCP foam track system is modular, so layouts can be changed monthly, keeping engagement high while preserving the material. Teams are trained to set up and store tracks properly, protecting the equipment and instilling team discipline. Scalable Curriculum: The program grows with the students. The same kits can be used year after year by new teams with updated goals, projects, and competitions—making it a multi-year investment for schools, not a one-time activity. Connection to High School Programs: These middle school kits are designed to feed directly into our high school sim racing programs, helping schools build long-term STEM pipelines with reusable assets. By teaching care, maintenance, and reuse, we're ensuring this investment makes an impact well beyond year one.

Reusability and recyclability

Sustainability is baked into every stage of this program—from packaging to long-term use. Minimal & Reusable Packaging: All kits will be delivered in durable, reusable storage bins rather than disposable cardboard boxes. These bins double as the club’s long-term equipment lockers, helping schools stay organized while eliminating single-use waste. Modular, Repairable Gear: Every RC car and track component is designed to be maintained, not thrown away. Students will learn how to repair, tune, and replace individual parts—preventing full replacements and extending the lifespan of every piece. Recycle-Friendly Materials: Any unavoidable packaging will be sourced from recyclable materials whenever possible, and we’re working with vendors that share our sustainability goals. Curriculum That Teaches Stewardship: A core part of the program is teaching students to care for their equipment—emphasizing the impact of waste and the importance of sustainability in engineering and racing. By combining reusable containers, teachable care practices, and repairable systems, we’re building a program that stays out of the landfill—and in the classroom—for years to come.
