Story

 3D printing opens up a world of creativity—but it also comes with hidden risks. FDM and resin printing can release fine particulates (PM), VOCs, and other airborne pollutants that build up over time in enclosed spaces. Most makers don’t realize how much their environment changes while a print is running.

Built from the ground up for makers, hobbyists, and 3D printing enthusiasts—a reliable air quality monitor tailored to the needs of modern workspaces. Here's what makes it essential gear for your bench:

  • Real-Time Air Quality Monitoring
    Instantly track particulates (PM1.0 & PM2.5), VOCs, CO₂, Humidity, Temperature and more with the professional-grade sensor unit.
  • Automatic Fan & Filter Control
    Set custom triggers that activate ventilation systems or air purifiers as air quality changes—hands-free safety.
  • Designed for FDM, Resin, and Workshop Use
    Whether you're running a high-temp extruder or handling uncured resin, this sensor is built to detect what matters!
  • Easy-to-Read Touchscreen Interface

    Print your own case, choose your filament, and mount it your way
  • Powerful Hardware,
    Features an SEN66 sensor and custom PCB

Why Choose a High-Quality Sensor?

During our testing we purchased and ran trials with a variety of off the shelf air quality monitors. You can purchase air quality monitors off amazon for around $100, however, the results will vary significantly from sensor to sensor... Additionally, we designed ours specifically for 3D printing and workshops. Off the shelf sensors often use a handful of very cheap and unreliable sensors which is what led us to creating our own. They also dont often have the sensors required for activities like 3D Printing. If you're like us and you spend a significant amount of time in your workspace, or if you have kids, pets, employees or family sharing the space, it's probably worth making sure it's safe. 

Our build is not secret - It runs on the SEN66 Sensor. The sensor unit alone is about $50-$70 just to get it on the custom PCB board. Our code will also be released to our customers as well! We took the SEN66, integrated it into our custom PCB build which includes the live monitoring, the trigger system, a touchscreen interface and desktop monitoring software.

With continuous tracking across a wide range of pollutants, you can monitor how your air quality changes throughout the day, during specific print jobs, or in response to certain materials. Whether you're printing overnight, running back-to-back batches, or working with resin, you’ll see exactly when conditions change—and how effective your countermeasures are.

Do you have a 3D Printer in your home?

Both FDM and Resin printers can affect the quality of the air in your home. If you are 3D printing in a house and not a designated workshop, we highly recommend implementing some sort of air filtration or vent system, especially if there are kids or pets present. 

Do you have multiple 3D Printers in a room without proper ventilation? 

Having multiple 3D printers running in small rooms or workshops can quickly affect the air quality, even with basic materials like PLA.

Do you have a workshop where you do any printing, curing, painting, cutting, sanding, gluing, etc, that does not have proper ventilation? 

It's not only 3D Printing that causes changes to the quality of air in your workshop!

Although we hope you consider supporting our project, even if you choose not to, we do encourage you to make sure you have sufficient air ventilation in your workplace. One of the biggest surprises for us was that in addition to 3D Printing, a lot of the other activities in our workshop also triggered the relay. 

Protect your lungs while you paint, prime, and sand. This sensor helps you stay safe during all stages of the creative process.

Whether you’re running PLA, ABS, PETG, or resin, this system gives you clear feedback on what you're breathing helping you know which types or which brands and materials are impacting your air quality.

The Relay System

One of the awesome and optional features with the 3D Print Sensor is the ability to set triggers and connect power relays. While the 3D Print Sensor can work with a variety of relays, we will link our favorite one below. 

Amazon Link (Recommended) - HERE    Alt Links Here, Here. 

If you're not familiar with how relay systems work, essentially what happens is that when the 3D Print Sensor registers air quality values over the threshold, it will trigger the relay device. The relay device we linked above is our favorite. It has 4 power outlets, one is always on, which is a good one for you to plug the sensor or your printer or machine into, as it will always be powered on. Two of the power outlets are set to normally off, which means the 3D Print Sensor will turn two of them on when the relay triggers. These are good choices for filtration devices, fans, etc. One power outlet is set to normally on, so the 3D Print Sensor will actually turn this off when triggered, we find this is a good option to use in case you want to turn off a machine if the air quality dips below a specified value (this is especially useful if you are printing in a home, home office, basement, etc).

Please note that whatever devices you connect to the relay system must be powered on by default. For example, if you have a fan connected to the relay, when the 3D Print Sensor triggers the relay to turn on the fan, it is providing power to the fan, so the fan must be set such that when power is provided, the fan turns on. (The relay does not do anything more than control the power on/off, so if you have a device that must be turned on, and then requires you to adjust some settings before it actually turns on, it is not a good option for the system). 

Ultimately, using these relays we can customize our workshop automation to suit any maker needs. 

The 3D Print Sensor can connect to up to 3 Trigger and Relay Systems! For example, if you have a workshop where you are FDM printing in one area and Resin printing in another area, you can have two different relay systems set up!

3D Print Sensor Monitoring Software 

We made a custom 3D Print Sensor Software!! Included with the file and sensor delivery will be a software download link. We have designed a custom software to go with your sensor, there is no fee or membership or anything like that, it's free forever. Using the 3D Print Sensor Software, you can wirelessly connect the sensor to your computer where it will send the air quality readings. The readings will automatically be saved in a .csv file, in live time, which can be plotted to visually see what happens to the air quality in your workshop! 

What's really cool about the monitoring software is that you can see in live time what really affects the air quality. For example, if you start up your printers, you might see some of the values start to rise, then when you open a window or maybe your relay automatically turns on a fan, you will see the values decrease. We've tested this with printing, curing, washing (IPA), painting, sanding, priming, gluing, soldering... etc!
