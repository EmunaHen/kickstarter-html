Roadmap of the project
 

1 stage: The Beginning: Arduino & Grow Tent

At first, we focused on automation: using Arduino to control light and ventilation inside a standard grow tent. It helped us test early code, lighting cycles, and temperature control. Meanwhile, we experimented with layouts — sketching and cutting dozens of cardboard mockups to define the ideal size and internal structure of the grow box.


 

2 stage: First Functional Prototype: Wood + Custom Firmware

The first real prototype was made of wood. It included our custom components and the very first version of our firmware. We completed several full growth cycles in it, which helped us understand what worked and what didn’t. Based on real plants’ feedback (literally), we revised the box size, rearranged internals, and replaced several modules.



 

3 stage: Mid-Stage Iterations

One intermediate version was made of aluminum — it looked promising but lacked the finish and thermal stability we needed. It confirmed we were moving in the right direction but also pushed us to build better.

Final stage: Master Model: Single Unit at Production Grade

Then we created our master model — built precisely to our updated CAD drawings. We hand-finished the internals and spent weeks fine-tuning element placement and mounts. After multiple reviews with manufacturers, we confirmed: every part of the design is ready for serial production.

1. Supply Chain and Manufacturing Delays We have already established preliminary agreements with our manufacturing partners in China. However, as with any new hardware product, there is always a risk of unforeseen production delays or supply chain disruptions. Mitigation: We have shortlisted multiple suppliers for critical components and maintain backup options to minimize any impact on our production timeline. 2. Quality Assurance for First Production Batch Launching the first batch always requires special attention to quality control. There is a possibility that small adjustments may be needed during the first production runs to ensure the product meets our high standards. Mitigation: We have allocated time for additional quality assurance checks before shipping and will personally oversee the validation process. 3. Global Logistics and Shipping Uncertainties Global shipping continues to face challenges, such as delays at ports or unexpected customs processes, which could impact delivery times. Mitigation: We are working with experienced logistics partners and have accounted for buffer periods in our estimated delivery dates. 4. Component Availability and Market Fluctuations Some electronic components used in EVA may become limited or fluctuate in price, which could affect production costs or schedules. Mitigation: We have proactively sourced alternative component options and flexible suppliers to adapt quickly if necessary.

Use of AI

EVA uses AI technology to assist users in growing healthier plants. Through the EVA app, users can photograph their plants; EVA then analyzes the image and provides personalized recommendations to optimize growth and maximize yields. Our AI service is integrated with OpenAI to interpret visual cues and suggest actionable advice. All training data — including plant photographs and environmental conditions — was collected and curated by our team from real plants grown in EVA under controlled settings. EVA’s AI acts as a helpful advisor, while keeping the user in full control of the growing process.
