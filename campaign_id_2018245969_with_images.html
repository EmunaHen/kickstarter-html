Story

In January 2025 we launched Bobbi as a web app (app.wearebobbi.co.uk). We did this completely on our own to prove the concept and get feedback from our community. But we don’t want to stop there. 

We want to create a NEW advanced version of the app that can be released on the App Store and Google Play Store. This version will give more than just recommendations but fully planned out workout routines for you to follow. You can track your progress and understand how your different phases impact your exercise. 

Users simply log when their period was and any specific symptoms during their cycle, and Bobbi does the rest - giving you 4 workout options (strength, cardio and two others specific to the phase). This will all be tailored to your goals, helping your workout plans get you to where you want to be. 

We will also be dropping new fitness guides based on the latest fitness trends (ermmm Hyrox anyone?), all tailored to your symptoms. 

We want to get this NEW version of the app out as soon as possible! But we can’t do it without your help. 

With this Kickstarter funding, we plan to fund a development team to help us speed up bringing the app to market (at the moment it is just Dhruvisha and some of her buddies). This will include front end and back end engineers, as well as designers. 

Once released onto the App Store and Google Play Store, Bobbi will run on a subscription model. 

A basic version will be free to everyone. We are passionate about keeping information accessible, ensuring that all women have access to science backed education on their cycle and fitness. 

For those that want the NEW full workout plans and access to guides as well as analytics and progress tracking, the advanced version will be available as a subscription of £60 a year. 

As part of this Kickstarter, you can get a subscription to the NEW advanced app, plus a year free!

We also have some other plans to make the Bobbi experience even better once we get all of the above done (we are not scared of hard work).

Risks and challenges

There is minimal risk associated with Bobbi. The Bobbi app can be delivered without funding, however funding will allow delivery to be sped up significantly. Our designs are ready and we started programming both the iOS and Android version of the app about a month ago. We will be using some funding to cover expected costs to assure all workout plans with advisors and PTs, giving users the best experience possible.
