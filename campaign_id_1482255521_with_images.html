Story

Hello my name is Brandon, and for the past two years, I've poured my heart and soul into creating the OBS Link â€“ a custom radio solution designed to integrate modern technology into the beloved 1988-1994 Chevy/GMC OBS trucks, all while preserving their iconic original interior look. I love my OBS truck, but its original radio felt like a relic from another era. The crackling static, the limitations of cassette tapes, and the complete lack of modern features like Bluetooth were a constant reminder that my classic ride deserved better. I envisioned a radio that seamlessly blended into the factory dash while offering all the tech I enjoyed in newer vehicles. This wasn't just about an upgrade it was about preserving the timeless aesthetic of the OBS interior. It began with a simple design focusing on the core functionality. My initial steps were purely conceptual. I spent hours staring at my truck's dashboard, taking measurements, and poring over diagrams of the original radio to understand its exact dimensions and mounting points. I knew the key was to create something that looked OEM not a clunky aftermarket add-on. The very first "prototype" was simply a hand-drawn sketch over a photo of the OBS dash. I mapped out where the screen would go, how big it could be while keeping the classic feel. This quickly evolved into 100s of 3d printed prototypes allowing me to visualize the fit and assess ergonomics from the driver's seat. It was a humble beginning, but these early, rough iterations were crucial for solidifying the vision of a custom OEM style touchscreen radio that truly honored the OBS look.

The first 3D printed prototype was a game-changer. Suddenly, I had a physical piece that I could snap into the dashboard. This allowed for real-world testing of the fitment confirming the measurements, the mounting hole locations for screws, and how flush it sat against the dash. More importantly, this tangible model opened up a direct channel for feedback. I shared images and even a short video of this 3D-printed prototype within various OBS TRUCK Communities on Facebook Their enthusiastic response was immediate, but a consistent piece of advice emerged: "Can the screen be bigger?"

This feedback from the OBS community was instrumental. While my initial design prioritized maintaining the factory look absolutely, owners were willing to accept a slightly larger screen if it meant a better user experience, as long as the OEM aesthetic wasn't compromised. Armed with this invaluable insight, I went back to the CAD drawing board, carefully pushing the boundaries of the screen size within the existing radio opening, ensuring it still appeared stock. The next prototype of the radio would embody this improvement, moving me closer to the ideal custom OEM-style touchscreen radio.

It was time to transition from prototypes to a real, production-ready part. This meant moving beyond home 3D printing to professional manufacturing processes that could replicate the OEM quality I was striving for. The next critical step involved investing in tooling and injection molding. This is where the digital CAD files were transformed into robust steel molds, designed to precisely form each radio bezel and component from durable, automotive-grade plastics. But the exterior was only half the story. To truly integrate modern functionality while ensuring reliability and a perfect fit, I also made a significant investment in custom motherboard design and manufacturing. This meant designing a Printed Circuit Board Assembly (PCBA) specifically for this radio, optimizing every component and connection to fit within the constrained OEM dimensions while delivering cutting-edge performance. This wasn't just another prototype it was the final product, built to withstand the daily use in OBS trucks for years to come, ready for mass production and ready to redefine the driving experience for enthusiasts everywhere.
