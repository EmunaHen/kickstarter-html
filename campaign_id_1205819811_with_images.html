Story

Background - Introducing the Hang 'n Go Range

Hi, my name is Rob McAlister. Over the last few years, I’ve been working on a solution to a simple but persistent everyday problem. How can you dry your washing outdoors even when it rains? 

I've developed three new outdoor laundry products, which I call the "Hang 'n Go" range: LaundryDome, Laundry Cave, and Laundry Hood! The range is designed to fit different needs and environments depending on space availability in your yard, balcony, patio, or garden. Whatever option you choose, they all have one thing in common: saving energy, reducing costs, and supporting a greener planet.

Introducing LaundryDome™

The benefits of drying laundry outdoors using a rotary washing line are well documented - especially when compared to using a tumble dryer. It's cheaper and more environmentally friendly. The sun's ultraviolet rays kill off germs and bacteria and leave your laundry smelling and feeling fresh. The problem is: what happens when it rains? LaundryDome™ is designed to solve this problem. 

Harnessing natural heat and airflow, this rotary drying canopy speeds up drying while protecting clothes from unexpected rain. A new type of rotary washing line umbrella, it attaches to your *existing rotary washing line to protect your washing from the rain. Using only the power of Nature, it allows you to continue drying your laundry even when it rains! What's more, it dries your washing quickly and with zero energy cost!

After several prototypes, the design is now moving to the final stage prior to production. It has a patent pending and is pretty much ready to launch. In order to take the product into production and launch it properly, though, I need to raise some initial funding. 

Based on early feedback, I propose to launch LaundryDome™  in one universal size to keep things simple and efficient for the initial production run. This size will be compatible with most square rotary clothes airers. A rib length of 160cm covers a typical square rotary airer up to 175 cm x 175 cm, making it a versatile solution for a wide range of households. I will continue to assess demand and consider expanding with additional sizes based on your feedback.

*I'm proposing to sell LaundryDome mainly as an umbrella that attaches to your existing rotary washing line. However, for people who don't currently have their own rotary washing line, I will also offer the option to include a rotary washing line. This option will be known as the LaundryDome COMBO.

Designed to reduce our impact on the planet, I believe this eco-friendly device has the potential to significantly reduce consumers' energy costs by reducing the use of energy-hungry electric tumble dryers - and consequently CO2 emissions. What's more, I believe it will also save money!

So, Just How Much Can LaundryDome™ Save You?

Well, I want to be careful not to exaggerate or make unsubstantiated claims. So, probably best if I just refer you to a couple of independent sources which explain the cost of running a tumble dryer. 

First, The Energy Trust says tumble dryers, along with washing machines and dishwashers, account for a huge 14% of households' energy usage. Some dryers can cost as much as £1.76 a cycle, which adds up pretty quickly over time. 

Also, Ideal Home Magazine says a vented tumble dryer can cost £1.34 per cycle and £159 per year on average.  

So you can do the math yourself! I think investing in a LaundryDome™ for many people should pay back very quickly. Just think how much money you could save over the years to come. 

Overview - How does it work?

LaundryDome™ attaches easily to the shaft of most rotary washing lines using our specially designed universal adjustable clamp. Its UV-stabilized, clear, vented canopy keeps your laundry dry during rain while allowing moisture to escape. It keeps the rain off your laundry, freeing up your time.

An optional, detachable side curtain (see below image) enhances drying efficiency by creating a greenhouse effect, capturing the sun’s heat to warm the air inside.  

This warm air removes moisture from your wet clothes, escaping through the overlapping canopy vents or condensing on the inner surface of the side curtain, thus speeding up the drying process.  

Key Benefits!

  • Eco-Friendly Drying: Harnesses natural sunlight and airflow, reducing reliance on energy-intensive tumble dryers.

  • Practical Protection: Shields clothes from rain, bird droppings, soot, pollen, and allergens.
  • Sustainable Design: Helps lower carbon footprints by promoting natural drying.

Other Products in the "Hang 'n Go!" Range - Laundry Cave & Laundry Hood!

A rotary washing line is not practical for everyone's situation. And so I've designed two other products in our Hang 'n Go eco-friendly laundry drying range. These use exactly the same approach to trapping the heat of the sun and creating air circulation currents.

These are the Laundry Cave and the Laundry Hood.

  • LaundryCave - is a modular drying unit perfect for compact outdoor spaces like balconies or patios. It's a self-assembly, arch-shaped structure big enough to fit a clothes horse/drying rack inside. It can be used freestanding or preferably screwed to the wall to make it stable in the wind. Made from a UV-stabilised clear fabric and fibreglass rods, it protects your laundry from the rain. It has ventilation panels in the roof and mesh door panel to allow various ventilation options.  It's also modular - if one's not big enough, you can attach a second (or third!) to increase the depth and the number of clothes airers contained within.
  • LaundryHood - is a convenient space-saving wall-mounted laundry drying system for use with clothes airers. Its clever “lift-up/drop-down” design is a boon for those with limited drying space. This retractable, wall-mounted design allows you to air dry clothes naturally, reducing condensation. It has various height settings and with ventilation panels allows you to dry your clothes, protected from the rain. 

The LaundryCave, LaundryHood and LaundryDome all work in the same way. They not only keep the rain off, but they also concentrate and capture the sun’s rays. With the increased temperature and use of ventilation, air circulates and removes water molecules from your laundry. The process is exactly the same as that used by the sun and the wind - the power of nature! Helping you save money and reduce your carbon footprint.
