How It Works – Like Magic, but Smarter
Say goodbye to lid arguments, germy flushes, and awkward reminders.
Our built-in smart sensors detect when your hand moves toward the flush button — whether from the left, right, or top — and automatically close both the toilet seat and lid before you flush.

Why? Because flushing with the lid open can release a cloud of invisible germs into the air — spreading bacteria and viruses across your bathroom.

This hands-free action doesn’t just improve hygiene — it turns your regular toilet into a modern, thoughtful smart device that protects your health, your family, and even your guests.

The Science Behind the Spray

It All Started During the Pandemic.

While the world was busy stocking up on toilet paper, we dove deep into scientific research. We quickly realized something critical: flushing without closing the lid spreads aerosols—and potentially viruses—into the air.

That sparked a simple but powerful idea:
What if the toilet lid could close itself?

We immediately began working on our solution and filed our first patent in 2022. By 2024, it was officially approved in Sweden, with patents pending across the globe.

But ideas alone aren’t enough—it takes engineering. We partnered with Omecon, a top-tier Swedish engineering firm, to develop a low-energy microcontroller and waterproof electronics able to withstand the harsh bathroom environment.

After more than a year of testing, refining, and thousands of test flushes… we finally made it.

Now, we're ready to bring No Brainer to homes everywhere—starting with yours.

Our Story – From Crisis to Creation

Our Story – From Struggle to Smart Solutions

The patented product is owned by Adam Wilewski, co-founder of No Brainer Inventions.

In 2014, I was in a serious motorcycle accident that nearly cost me my life. During my recovery, I became dependent on morphine — a quiet, creeping battle that took years to overcome. In 2017, during a break at a 12-step meeting, something unexpected happened.

I went to the bathroom and saw both the toilet seat and lid left open.
It might sound trivial — but in that moment, something clicked.
This needs to change.

That’s where the journey toward a solution — and eventually a patent — began.

Together with my partner and co-founder Jessica, who never gave up on the idea (or on me), we’ve faced failure, progress, late nights, and small wins — and kept going. When the pandemic hit, the urgency became even clearer. Studies confirmed what many feared: flushing with the lid open spreads bacteria and viruses into the air.

We began building No Brainer from scratch — at our kitchen table. We designed, failed, redesigned, tested, and learned.
