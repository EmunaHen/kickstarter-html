Story

The Sinclair ZX Spectrum Next: A Revolution, Rebooted

A machine that changed everything, reborn for a new era. The original ZX Spectrum sparked a generation of coders, gamers, and dreamers to build the future we live in. Now, the Next continues that legacy, not as a replica, but as the true next step: fully compatible, massively upgraded, and made to inspire a new wave of creativity.
 

Meet the ZX Spectrum Next Issue 3

The ZX Spectrum Next Issue 3 is the third generation of the modern Speccy, a beautifully engineered 8-bit machine built with a touch of nostalgia and focused on innovation. It follows the success of our two previous Kickstarter campaigns (delivering over 10,000 units worldwide) and introduces a larger, more powerful FPGA (Artix A7 XC7A35T-2CSG324C), support for additional platforms like the Sinclair QL and Commodore 64, and a ton more enhancements under the hood.

Whether you're a lifelong Speccy fan or just rediscovering the joy of owning a computer you can truly master, explore and make your own, the Next Issue 3 is the most powerful 8-bit computer out there..

More Than Retro: The Power of the Next

The ZX Spectrum Next is what the original might have evolved into if Sinclair had kept going: a machine to rival the likes of the Amiga or Atari ST, with a full spectrum (pun intended!) of improvements:

  • Hundreds of new games and apps, most in boxed releases for collection
  • Improved graphics: 256 colour modes, copper effects, tilemaps and hardware sprites
  • Enhanced sound: 9-channel stereo audio + dual stereo DACs
  • Built-in networking, faster storage, and modern ports such as HDMI
  • Fully compatible with classic ZX Spectrum hardware and software, no emulation in sight

Play the classics as they were meant to be played, or re-experience them with turbo modes and enhancements. Then dive into a whole new generation of Next-exclusive games like Saboteur Remastered, Head Over Heels Deluxe, and Lords of Midnight Next.

Create Your Own Classics

The ZX Spectrum Next isn't just for playing, it's built for creating. With a powerful new NextBASIC, full C support, Assembly, and complete IDEs and toolchains, it welcomes you to explore, learn, and build. Want to make your own platformer, puzzle game, or music tracker? The tools are all here for you to try, and the community ready to help to make it so.

With every system shipped, a SD card is included packed with games, apps, demos, documentation, and development tools to get started right out of the box.

Not Just a ZX Spectrum, It’s a Platform

Thanks to its new expanded FPGA, the Next Issue 3 supports more retro cores and ZX personalities than ever:

New Official Cores:

Sinclair QL/Next with switchable CPU between MC68000 and 68020. Run classic QL software with full compatibility, smoother performance, while adding vibrant colour and exciting sound capabilities. Our enhanced QL core brings the system back to life, complete with modern SD storage, Internet and legacy network support, and full integration with the Next’s own keyboard.

Commodore 64 to experience and enjoy classic SID sounds while playing iconic C64 games from tapes, cartridges and even multiple disk spanning titles! 

You can switch between cores and/or launch them directly from NextZXOS at boot, as easy as that!

Extra cores are available from third parties (like the BBC by hoglet) and more Sinclair-related cores are in the planning stage!

Personalities:

Timex as well as unofficial clones, such as the TS/TC2048, Pentagon, TK90X, TK95 and others.

Multiple Personalities: ZX80, ZX81, and all original Spectrum models.


 

The Specs

Time to get our hands dirty with the specs of the Next Issue 3. Fasten your seatbelts, this is where it gets serious:

Native ZX Spectrum Next Issue 3:

  • Processor: Z80N at 3.5MHz with turbo modes at 7MHz, 14MHz and 28MHz
  • Compatibility: Full ZX Spectrum family compatibility
  • Memory: 2MB RAM
  • Video: 2, 8, 16 & 256 colours modes (128 x 96, 256x192, 320x256, 512x192 & 640x256 high resolution modes)
  • Video Output: RGB, VGA, HDMI, 50Hz and 60Hz modes
  • Extra Hardware: Hardware sprites, zxnDMA, CTC, Copper, Enhanced ULA, Layer2 and Tilemap (Layer3)
  • Storage: 1 x SD Card slot, with DivMMC-compatible protocol, 1 x internal microSD card slot with preinstalled card (fully supported by NextZXOS)
  • Audio:  9-channel sound via three stereo AY-3-8912, plus two x 4-bit DACs
  • Joysticks:Two ports compatible with Cursor, Kempston, and Interface 2 protocols — extended to support even Megadrive joypads or configurable as serial ports
  • PS/2 port: Mouse with Kempston mode emulation and/or external keyboard
  • Special: Multiface functionality for memory access, save games, cheats and more
  • Tape support: Combined MIC and EAR port for loading and saving
  • Expansion: Original external bus expansion port (now with +3 disk interface support)
  • Accelerator Board (optional): GPU / 1GHz CPU / 512Mb RAM
  • Networking: Built-in Wi-Fi module
  • Real Time Clock: Built-in RTC with battery backup 
  • OS: NextZXOS and NextBasic, featuring an expanded command set
  • Alternative OSes: CP/M (included),SymbOS and esxDOS
  • Additional cores and official ROMs: Sinclair QL/Next and C64 with cores also launchable via NextZXOS. Community cores available
     

ZX Spectrum Next Issue 3 in Sinclair QL Core mode:

  • Processor: Switchable Motorola 68000 or 68020 CPU running at up to 44MHz
  • Video Output: VGA and HDMI support
  • Compatibility: Full Sinclair QL compatibility with MDV and FLP image support
  • Memory: 2MB of RAM
  • Storage: SD card large storage with both QXL container and FAT32 access
  • Network: WiFi network
  • Joysticks: CTL1/2 Joystick ports switchable to QL Net port (requires DIY dongle)
  • Keyboard: Full QL keyboard support with extra keys simulated via combinations
  • Co-Processor: Hermes i8049 co-processor
  • PS/2 port: PS/2 port for external keyboard and/or Qimi mode emulation
  • Real Time Clock: Full support
  • Video Modes: 4, 8, 16 (Thor XVI), 256 (Aurora compliant) and 65K colour display with dual screens for extra speed
  • Audio: Hermes/i8049 beep, 3 channel AY PSG and stereo PCM audio
  • Extras: Hardware Sprites and dual hardware timers, I2C bus
  • OS: Support for QDOS / Minerva / SMSQ/e (WIP)
     

ZX Spectrum Next Issue 3 in C64 Core mode:

  • Processor: 6510 processor running at 0.97MHZ (PAL) & 1.023MHz (NTSC) with accelerated modes in the works
  • Compatibility: Full C64 compatibility
  • Memory: 64KB RAM
  • Video: Full VIC2 support (320x200 colour mode, 16 colours, sprites and raster)
  • Video Output: RGB, VGA, HDMI - PAL and NTSC modes (switchable via hotkey)
  • Audio: SID fully implemented
  • Joysticks: Standard joysticks support
  • Storage: Support for 8K and 16K cartridges, D64 disk images (single and double sided) with multi floppy software support
  • OS: Jiffy DOS compatible
  • PS/2 port: PS/2 supporting GeosMOUSE protocol and external PS/2 keyboard
  • Keyboard: Full C64 keyboard support via combination keys
  • Tape: Tape support via Next MIC/EAR socket
     

Prepare to fall in love with a computer all over again

Our original tagline printed on every ZX Spectrum Next box remains at the heart of our mission: to deliver a machine that is straightforward, beautiful, and powerful, with everything fully documented in the included printed manual for you to master.

From the premium packaging to the accessories and the curated goodies on the SD card, every aspect of the Next is designed to delight and surprise. To get started, all you need is a TV or monitor, a video cable and… that's it. The Next supports RGB, VGA, and HDMI output, so you’re free to choose anything from a vintage CRT to a modern high-end display.

You can start your journey with your favourite Speccy game, perhaps running at a turbo mode – 3D games such as Driller or Castle Master run at full framerate on the 28MHz mode. Experience the new titles made exclusively for the machine featuring upgraded graphics and sound, and feel inspired to create your own game.

You can connect your ZX Spectrum Next to your Wi-Fi network and browse online collections of games, demos, mods and art ready to download and play with applications such as GetIt by team member David Saphier. 

Every aspect of the ZX Spectrum Next is designed to put you in control, with an open and intuitive environment that encourages you to remix, expand, or simply learn from the games and apps as you build your own. It captures the spirit of a computer that feels personal the moment it's switched on, making the Next an ideal platform for learning about computing, free from the abstraction and complexity of modern operating systems.
 

Built by the Community, Powered by You

The ZX Spectrum Next was designed as a true successor of the Speccy, down to its industrial design by the late Rick Dickinson, the original in-house Sinclair designer and his partner Phil Candy. This means it packs small touches one should expect, from a responsive keyboard replacing ye ol’ rubber keys to hardware compatibility: you can even load your original games from cassette tape if you so want, or attach that ZX Spectrum expansion board you’ve got in the attic and it will just work.

This ensures the Next has its roots firmly in the Speccy heritage, and takes it into the future with its myriad of hardware and software improvements, thanks to the community and team behind the project:

Allen Albright: The brains behind the Next core, Allen keeps expanding the functionalities of the Next with every new iteration of its cores 

Garry Lancaster: If the Next is easy to use and program, it’s thanks to Garry’s NextZXOS, literally the most powerful 8-bit OS on the planet. Garry is also the author of the CP/M bios, included in the Next.

Tim Gilberts: One of the most recognised developers of the Speccy, Tim dedicates his neurons to ensure the Next can speak to peripherals through UART drivers, Serial I/O, WiFi, Kempston Mouse support…

D. Rimron-Soutter (Xalior): The Next accelerator enabling the loading of TZX files, playing of MP3 music, MIDI and a ton of functionalities in the NextPi distro are all the work of Xalior, who maintains the accelerator.

David Saphier: Music players, terminal software, getit downloader, games programmer, NextBuild package for Boriel BASIC compiler… David is a powerhouse of a creator that makes the Next go way beyond the confines of its elegant case. 

Robin Verhagen-Guest: The Next talks to the world outside thanks to Robin’s ESP WiFi firmware. Robin keeps the computer connected and we get a ton of apps and content through those pipes!

Michael “Flash” Ware: A prolific games programmer who’s pushed the envelope by providing amazing software for the previous Kickstarters and will do so again this time around!. 

Gari Biasillo: Musician? Games Programmer? Next audio system debugger? AY drivers for NextBASIC, Z80 and C via NextDAW? He does it all and he’s good looking too!

Mike Dailly: Creator of Lemmings and GTA (you know, those classics - Ed), ported Wolf3D on the Next, made some amazing demos and author of the  #CSpect emulator that has made cross-platform debugging a breeze.

Matt Davies: When not tweaking Lords of Midnight/Next or adding stuff to his Odin assembler (nice logo btw - Ed) he’s working on a Forth interpreter. 

Lampros Potamianos: Author of the very first real Next game ever published, he’s also the greatest hacker Greece has produced. He's the go-to guy to test new features, usually by writing a game.

Theo Liontakis (Leon): A man of few words, always calm and collected, he’s our official cores’ author and noted modern QL hardware developer. When presented with the task of creating the C64 core, he said: “I dunno” and then presented a working core five days later.

Kev Brady: The methodical genius brains behind our awesome burn-in test suite that makes sure that every single one of your Nexts that comes out of the factory works great!

Phil Candy: The industrial design partner of the late Rick Dickinson, Phil continues to help us ensure the Next physical properties are up to scratch and of top notch quality.

Victor Trucco: The hardware hacker behind more gadgets than one can shake a stick at, is the creator of the TBBlue, the roots of the ZX Spectrum Next hardware.

Henrique Olifiers: A childhood friend of Victor Trucco and game developer who, a while back, started putting together the project that eventually became the ZX Spectrum Next and that now is, well, here!

Alfredo Tato: The ZX Spectrum Next box became a legendary collectible item thanks to the brilliant work of Tato, who ensures a relentless dedication to the heritage of a long line of gorgeous boxes.

Phoebus Dokos: Author of the Next manual included in the box, a book that keeps on changing as often as the team adds new features, which sure enough drives poor Phoebus, well, sort of mad.

Honourable mentions: 

Simon N Goodwin: Our resident Spectrum and QL tech guru and prolific author of books and software (and a great influence for the Next manual, NextBASIC and QL cores). Of invaluable help to make the Next an enduring platform! Don’t forget to get his 490+ page Next Tech book!

Martyn Hill: whose work on providing the Next with a ZX/QL Net interface pushes the platform forward by reviving one of the most overlooked network protocols ever invented.

Mike Cadwallader: There are few people with Mike’s patience and organisational skills. He brought KS1 and KS2 backers their Nexts and covered every aspect and every possibility with his relentless attention to detail.

Long-lasting design

The Sinclair ZX Spectrum Next is designed and built for lastability. The machine is constantly updated through firmware with new features, and every new issue is retrocompatible, ensuring the Next never gets outdated or superseded.

Reusability and recyclability

The ZX Spectrum Next case is made of ABS plastic, a recyclable material. The computer is designed to be user-maintained, meaning it's easy to open and repair, with extensive documentation and schematics available to help users to service their Nexts if necessary (although the ZX Spectrum Next is a very sturdy piece of electronics). Its open-source design allows for 3rd party expansion, maintenance and even cloning.
