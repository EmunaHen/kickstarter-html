Story

HydroSeeds 1.0 - a smart new-generation 3D-farm for microgreens right at your home 

Print it, fill the central reservoir only twice a week - and enjoy how your greens grow almost by themselves.
Each mini-pot is easily removable together with the root ball, allowing you to transplant the sprouts into larger containers, beds, or pots without stress.
Perfect for growing microgreens, salads, aromatic herbs, ornamental flowers, and even vegetables.
Sustainable. Modular. Compatible with any FDM printer.

Why HydroSeeds 1.0?

Growing from seeds should be simple and inspiring!

But what do we see in reality?
Standard plastic trays dry out quickly.
Plant roots get tangled, causing many seedlings to die during transplanting.
Plastic waste from single use only harms the environment.

HydroSeeds 1.0 solves these pain points with a 3D-printable, self-watering, and transplant-friendly design that anyone can fabricate at home.

Advantages of HydroSeeds 1.0

  • 🌱 Advanced modular design
    Each mini-pot can be easily removed and replaced. The central reservoir supplies the whole system with water, increasing autonomy and reducing the need for daily care.
  • 🧩 Ideal for 3D printing
    This system is adapted for FDM printers: printing is possible without supports, assembly — without glue and tools. The modular design allows the system to scale to any number of plants.
  • 💧 Maximum benefit — minimum effort
    Just pour in seeds, add water — and watch nature do its thing. This is a full-fledged indoor garden with your own hands that even a child can assemble and maintain.
  • 🍃 Eco-friendly and sustainable
    No single-use plastic. Only reuse, local production (your 3D printer), and healthy food without packaging and transportation. This is a contribution to ecology right from your windowsill.

Growing seeds is now easier and cleaner than ever.

How It Works

  • Print & Snap
    Download STL files, print the parts from PLA or PETG on any home 3D printer, and assemble the structure in 5 minutes - without glue, screws, or tools.
  • Fill the Reservoir
    Pour water into the central tank twice a week. Water automatically distributes through capillary channels, nourishing each module. This is a real self-watering pot - but in a modular farm.
  • Sow & Grow
    Place seeds into ventilated pots with light soil mix or coconut substrate. The built-in wick system maintains perfect moisture - plants grow quickly and evenly.
  • Pop & Plant
    When the sprout is ready to “move,” use the special tool (included in STL set) for lifting. Roots stay intact, and transplanting becomes easy and safe.

Environmental commitments

Long-lasting design

I've enhanced HydroGreen 1.0 to be even more sustainable by optimizing the design for 3D printing with durable materials like ASA. With improved durability and an optimized structure, these pots are built to last through many seasons, reducing the need for frequent reprints.

Reusability and recyclability

To minimize waste, I will provide instructions on how to recycle or repurpose any failed prints or excess filament. HydroGreen 1.0's design minimizes the need for support material, further reducing waste during the printing process.

Sustainable materials

I recommend using materials such as ASA, known for its exceptional durability and UV resistance - perfect for outdoor use. PETG is also a great option for those seeking a recyclable and easy-to-print material that offers good strength and flexibility.

Environmentally friendly factories

While I'm offering the STL files, I encourage users to choose 3D printing services or personal printers that utilize energy-efficient processes and eco-friendly or recycled filaments to further reduce environmental impact.

Sustainable Distribution

Sustainability remains a core focus of my project. By offering HydroGreen 1.0 as an STL file, we eliminate the need for mass production and shipping, significantly lowering the carbon footprint. Additionally, I will provide recommendations on optimizing print settings to minimize filament waste, with designs that require little to no support material.

Something else

I'm promoting the use of biodegradable or recyclable filament spools and providing tips on reducing energy consumption during the printing process. Together, we can make HydroGreen 1.0 not just a convenience for plant care but also a step towards a more sustainable future.
