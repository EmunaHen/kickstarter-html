Mushrooms breathe, like humans. However, they need more than just air, they need the right kind of air for each growth phase:

😶 High CO₂ when mushroom is colonizing (not visible, but growing)
🌬️ High oxygen when mushroom is pinning (coming out)

Sproushi is the only mushroom grower to tell the difference between growth phases and automatically adjust its airflow

Sproushi's fan is whisper-quiet and no louder than your average laptop (<35dB).

Mushrooms don’t like humidity, they require it. Sproushi uses an ultrasonic humidifier to get to 80%+ humidity easily, like a damp mushroom forest. Precise, fast and completely silent.

Sproushi's humidifier is wirelessly powered and waterproof, making it super easy to clean and durable.

Sproushi monitors your grow environment and alerts you if it gets too hot or cold—but we don’t actively control the temperature. Why? Because we found a better way to grow.

Most popular mushrooms thrive at normal indoor temperatures:

Lion’s Mane: 60–75°F (16–24°C)
Pink Oyster: 68–86°F (20–30°C)
Yellow Oyster: 68–81°F (20–27°C)
King Oyster: 60–75°F (16–24°C)
Chestnut: 59–72°F (15–22°C)
Reishi: 75–86°F (24–30°C)

Rather than inflate cost and complexity with active heating/cooling systems, we focused on what actually drives growth: using AI to recognize growth phases. Dynamically adapting humidity, airflow, and light—delivering consistently bigger harvests, faster.

Use of AI

This project is not raising funds to develop AI technology, and no AI-generated content was used to create this Kickstarter page. However, Sproushi product itself does make use of AI. Not for marketing, but to help users grow better food. Inside each device, a camera and sensor system works with custom-built computer vision software to recognize growth phases in mushrooms and herbs. This data is matched against an internal database of growth patterns and environmental needs (like humidity, airflow, and light cycles) to create the optimal conditions - automatically. The database powering this system was developed entirely in-house, based on the team’s own cultivation experiments and research. Everything runs in a closed system designed solely to benefit the grower.
