Story

UTRACK flips conventional thermal gear on its head: a head-mounted, flip-up design that keeps your hands free and your view clear for effortless night observation. On-board AI recognition classifies wildlife and estimates size in real time, turning raw heat into useful insight. A professional-grade 384 × 288 sensor with 12 µm pixels, 25 mK sensitivity and 1,500 m (1,640 yd) detection range delivers razor-sharp thermal detail that standard scopes can’t touch—all in one lightweight AR package.

Long-lasting design

We are maximizing U-Track's lifespan on two fronts: hardware and software. On hardware, we use high-strength, durable modified nylon for the body and have designed a hot-swappable battery system, which prevents the entire device from becoming obsolete due to battery degradation. On software, we support Over-the-Air (OTA) updates. This means U-Track is an evolving platform that will continuously improve with new features and optimizations long after you receive it, dramatically extending its useful life.

Reusability and recyclability

Product: U-Track's modular design encourages repair and upgrades over disposal. Users can individually replace or upgrade batteries and function modules, extending the life of the core device. The main structural materials we use, such as PA nylon, are recyclable. Packaging: We are committed to minimalist packaging. We will use 100% recyclable cardboard and print with eco-friendly soy-based ink to minimize our environmental footprint.

When selecting our manufacturing partners, we prioritize factories that are ISO 14001 certified for their environmental management systems. Sustainable Distribution

To reduce the carbon footprint of our global distribution, our strategy is to "consolidate shipments and reduce parcel miles." We will ship products in bulk to regional fulfillment hubs (e.g., in North America and Europe) and distribute them to local backers from there, rather than sending thousands of individual packages across the globe from a single point. Our compact packaging design also reduces shipping volume and weight, further conserving energy.
