Story

The MetaBow Dev Kit comprises a small-scale sensor board and prototype housing unit that can function as a digital interface for string instruments (violin, viola, cello, and double bass) designed to track performance data in real-time. The board incorporates advanced sensors that can be attached to a violin bow using the prototype housing unit supplied in the kit, allowing players to monitor elements such as bow speed, tilting, finger pressure, and position. The prototype housing unit provides an option for DIY makers to install the board on any violin, viola, cello or double bass bow.

Our Inspiration 

The MetaBow Dev Kit was born from the desire to bridge the gap between traditional violin playing and cutting-edge digital technology. Over the past two decades, violin bow interfaces have struggled to balance practicality with advanced functionality. Early models were often bulky, difficult to use, or interfered with the natural flow of violin performance. Our team set out to address these challenges with a focus on seamless integration into traditional instrument design while providing advanced capabilities for tracking and enhancing musical learning and performance. We believe we have achieved this goal, and we want to invite a community of experimental musicians to try our housing and sensor development kits for themselves and help us learn more about the types of products the players of the world are interested in.

Design and Development 

The development of the MetaBow Dev Kit has been a thorough and iterative process, guided by both user feedback and technological advancements. The initial goal was to maintain the integrity of the violin bow’s tactile and ergonomic qualities while incorporating essential sensor technology. This required more than 53 design iterations to perfect the balance between functionality and usability, evolving from the initial version shown on the left side of the figure below to the latest model on the right. 

By embedding a 9DoF IMU—which includes an accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer—into our MetaBoard sensor, and combining it with a precisely modelled housing prototype, the MetaBow Dev Kit can accurately track bow movements such as speed, tilt, and pressure

The Embedded Technology

When our sensor board is installed using the dev kit's housing unit, it can capture nuanced details of a performer’s technique, allowing for real-time data analysis. This information can be visualized on connected devices through an existing cross-platform SDK, enabling musicians to see and adjust their bowing techniques. The data collected includes bow tilt, speed, pressure, and other parameters that offer insights into the performer’s style and accuracy. This makes the MetaBow Dev Kit not only a solution for live performances but also an invaluable resource for students and educators in refining playing technique. The MetaBow Dev Kit includes the MetaBoard, a 9DoF IMU integrating a microphone, embedded sensor fusion computing, an IC touch sensor and a haptic feedback capacitor. 

Applications

The MetaBow Dev Kit's flexibility will make it an indispensable technique in both pedagogical settings and live performances. We believe that the MetaBow Dev Kit is the first step in opening the door to new forms of interaction between performer and technology, enabling composers and musicians to explore creative possibilities through data-driven performances.

User-Centered Design

Our user-centred design approach ensures that when installed, the sensor and housing unit don’t disrupt traditional playing techniques. For those who want to tinker with their instrument, the MetaBow Dev Kit is simple to install onto the bow and can be used without altering the player's technique.

The MetaBow Dev Kit represents a major breakthrough in music interface design. We are excited to share it with our community and can't wait to see what you do with it!

Risks and challenges

The technical and design challenges for MetaBow have already been addressed through extensive prototyping and testing. The primary risks now lie in extending our development kit out to a broader base of users to learn more about how our prototype housing design addresses the key pain point of embedded sensor design for classical string instruments and whether it is a useful add-on for musicians interested in developing their own IoT instruments.
