Fiber Laser CNC Cutting

Intelligent Flowing Cutting

Continuous cutting without interruption maximizes productivity by eliminating idle time. Single-positioning capability enables completion of the entire board cutting in one setup, significantly reducing turnaround time. 

The cutting head remains engaged without returning to the origin after each operation, allowing rapid jumps and precise positioning across the workpiece to achieve seamless, high-efficiency cutting cycles.

Common Edge Cutting VS Separate Cutting

Traditional independent cutting requires a 0.2–0.5 mm margin per edge, while common-edge cutting eliminates redundant gaps by sharing cutting edge, improving material utilization by 10%–25%. 

Shared edges need only one cut to separate adjacent parts, reducing cutting travel by 30%–50% compared to traditional methods. Of course, non-common-edge cutting is also fully supported to meet diverse processing needs.

Autonomous Obstacle Avoidance

Obstacle detection enables automatic path adjustment to avoid collisions, minimizing manual interruptions, enhancing efficiency, and ensuring operational safety.

Micro-jointing VS No Micro-jointing

Micro-joints ensure that small, delicate parts remain connected to the sheet during the cutting process, preventing them from being ejected and potentially damaging the cutting machine or causing safety issues. 

It maintains material stability during complex contour cutting, preventing displacement of thin plates. Non-micro-connection cutting is also supported for flexibility.

Automatic Edge Finder

The machine automatically detects the position of your material, making the process simple and intelligent.

Rotary Attachment

The rotary attachment enables 360° continuous rotation, expanding machining capabilities for cylindrical. 

Adjustable Lift Platform

With a 20cm (7.89in) lift capacity, it’s ready for use with the upcoming rotary axis.

Industrial Nesting Software

Advanced nesting algorithms such as automatic layout optimization, common line cutting and scrap material reuse significantly improve material utilization. These techniques are especially effective for irregular parts and can reduce raw material waste by 15% to 30%.

Electromagnetic Proportional Valve Control

The electromagnetic proportional valve dynamically adjusts pressure for piercing, cutting, and corner protection. It delivers gas on demand to reduce waste and costs. Its closed-loop feedback enables real-time calibration, improving consistency and reducing manual input. Fast and reliable, it’s essential for precise, efficient cutting.

Handheld Laser Welding

Laser Welding VS MIG Welding

Laser welding can reach speed of 200–300 cm/min, while traditional MIG welding typically operates at 30–60 cm/min, depending on the material and thickness. This means laser welding can be up to 5 times faster, making it a superior choice for applications that demand both speed and precision.

Compared to traditional MIG and TIG welding, laser welding produces cleaner, narrower seams with minimal heat-affected zones, resulting in stronger joints and a more refined finish. It's the ideal solution for users who demand both precision and aesthetics.

Multi-mode Welding
