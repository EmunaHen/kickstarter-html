Design goals

I wanted to make something I hadn't seen at DEFCON yet. I also wanted to give people something to do in between talks, workshops and other fun activities such as LineCon. While games are fun, they are more fun with friends and colorful metaphors. This required the inclusion of a mesh-network system, allowing for friends to play together at the Con. And I wanted a way for everyone to play without needing the internet, connecting to sketchy WiFi or subspace relays, which, according to Wikipedia, have yet to be invented.

To sum up my design goals:

  • Multiplayer (or single player) games
  • Wireless
  • Real-time
  • Multiple games
  • Sound
  • Color
  • Animation
  • Long battery life
  • Geeky theme

History

The original idea was an SAO to connect to the official DEFCON 33 badge, but the number of variables (battery size, board-side SAO implementation, disturbances in the Force, etc) and the fact that most official badges haven't had an SAO port dissuaded me from following this path.Â 

The idea phase went through multiple turns from ambitious projects like a 3-stage connecting board, and at one point I wanted to add a drone that you could fly. Maybe next year. After iterating through several rendering systems, I landed on LVGL. Before this project began, C++ wasn't one of the languages I knew, but I knew C#, Java, Elvish and the binary language of moisture vaporators so I went off and learned some C++ for this project, which I will continue using.Â

Enclosure

To help satisfy the 'geeky theme' requirement the enclosure needed to be iconic. The base design of the enclosure was designed in Blender, then ported to Fusion360 for a more precise design.Â 

But a good-looking design doesn't always print well. The print-in-place buttons were difficult to design for this project, because they would act strange when printed in different orientations. The nacelles also needed to be modified because the extra supports were ruining the build quality, but after those problems were fixed, the enclosure looked and felt amazing.Â 

I've prototyped SO MANY versions of the enclosure... gives me a new respect for people who design engines and other systems with a bazillion parts.Â 

I looked at a variety of classic sci-fi spacecraft and decided that shuttlecraft of some kind were the best enclosure type for this project. They're usually compact, have a big, flat surface that I can replace with a screen and often are iconic in design.

Hardware

Everyone likes cool/unique hardware! Â 
Here's the list of components:

  • LX7 Dual-Core CPU @ 240 MHz
  • 8 MB RAM
  • 16 MB flash
  • 2.4 GHz WiFi
  • Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) 5
  • 2.8", 262k color screen
  • 320x240 resolution
  • 5-point capacitive touch screen
  • Stereo audio out (with speakers!)
  • SD card slot
  • Real time clock
  • 6-axis IMU
  • GPIO interface
  • I2C and UART interfaces
  • USB-C Charging
  • BF3D2k GPU*
  • 2200 mAh rechargeable battery (10+ hours of battery life in testing - see actual results from my tests below; as with all batteries, YMMV)

*Not really

Note that some of the board's features (like SD card slot) are not accessible while in the enclosure. Feel free to disassemble (just not Number 5).

Software

The software is custom but was made possible by many (many) open-source libraries like LVGL, painlessMesh, ArduinoJson and many others. The games are all difficult to make and were in fact, a nightmare the make performant. These features will be in the final build:

Mesh Networking

Dynamic, Wi-Fi-based mesh networking that does NOT talk to the Internet (closed system).Â 

It does lets us play as long as another badge (or series of badges!) is/are nearby to provide connectivity.

Co-Op Multiplayer Games

Not going to spoil which games are going to be part of the final build, but I'm working on several multiplayer games to run as 'away missions' with a crew.

XP, Rank & Progress

As you play the games, you'll earn XP which increases your rank (and BRAGGING RIGHTS!!!!!)

Badge Mode

Sadly, not everyone at the CON will have one of these badges.Â 

The lucky badge owners need a cool way to show off their awesomeness. What better way to show that besides having the badge do that itself?

When you're not playing it will go to 'badge mode' - a dedicated display mode that visually shows off your progress to everyone around you.

Risks and challenges

The board design is done. The enclosure design is complete. I have legally avoided any tariff-related delays on the build components. The firmware is not finished, and may not be ready in time for the con (which would make me sad), but the code will be openly accessible, so if that happens you can still pick up the board flash it and yourself :) The code may also be missing some of the anticipated features (again, would make me sad) It's also possible that the boards get stolen in transit, which would make me very sad :( I plan to fulfil all orders and want to see these at DEFCON as much as you do!
