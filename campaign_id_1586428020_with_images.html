Story

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu is a mobile application designed to help you manage your training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu by providing support tools to track your progress, analyze your performance, and reference skills and techniques in an intuitive Mind Map framework.  

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a uniquely cerebral martial art that combines physical fitness and skill with intellectual challenges.  Every opponent is a puzzle.  Every roll is a test.  Every detail matters.  Brain-Jiu-Jitsu represents the cognitive aspect of the sport to help you train harder and smarter.

This is not another instructional product.  Brain-Jiu-Jitsu was built to address the most common challenges of every BJJ athlete: organization, knowledge retention, and motivation.  With a variety of features tailored to meet the needs of your individual style, Brain-Jiu-Jitsu is a platform that caters to all experience levels.  With this campaign, we aim to make this a truly community-driven product incorporating user feedback and delivering the tools you need to take your game farther.

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu is full of features to help you get the most of your time on and off the mats.  Each tool and feature addresses challenges commonly shared by all BJJ athletes - all in one convenient platform to keep you engaged and moving forward.

🧠 Interactive Mind Map

One of the most common challenges expressed by BJJ athletes - particularly beginners - is being overwhelmed by the volume of data input; countless details about positions, fundamental principles, and the individual fine adjustments needed to successfully execute a technique.

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu addresses this challenge by providing an intuitive mind map framework of techniques.  Techniques are divided into top and bottom positions represented by Top Brain and Bottom Brain.  Within each division are subdivisions of positions and skills, which contain modules of techniques.  

Each technique module provides content from the Miyachi Jiu-Jitsu Laboratory including key points, a short video demonstration of the technique, and a note taking field for users to add details specific to their execution of the technique.   The modules can be saved for quick reference in between rounds.   

✅ Competency Tracker

Embedded in the mind map is a self-assessment feature to indicate competency of every technique.  You may select Practicing, Mastered, or leave unselected (not learned) to indicate your competency level.   

The number of proficient and mastered techniques within a position or skill provides an aggregate score for that position.  This insight allows you to recognize the strongest and weakest aspects of your game and identify areas for improvement. 

📜 Logbook for Training, Competitions, and Promotions

Progress in BJJ can often feel slow and frustrating at all levels.  An often-recommended practice among BJJ athletes is to maintain a journal of their training activity.  Journaling helps you build consistency, clarify goals, codify knowledge, and personalize your journey in the sport.  

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu takes this practice further by providing a comprehensive tool for you to track not only training sessions, but competition outcomes, and belt promotions.  Tracking these details over time provides tangible proof of progress; as well as promote discipline and accountability to overcome barriers. 

📊 Performance Analysis

Many of the user input fields in Brain-Jiu-Jitsu are summarized and presented visually to help turn experience into improvement.  This performance analysis helps you identify strengths and weaknesses, set realistic goals, and track progress.  It supports continuous improvement by providing objective data for better decision-making. This leads to greater efficiency, motivation, and accountability.  Measure what matters to discover your strategic advantage by enabling more informed actions on the mats.

🤖 AI Coaching

Where many activity trackers fail is in providing meaningful insights and guidance from the data collected.  Brain-Jiu-Jitsu seeks to address this limitation through the use of AI technology by deploying a custom GPT4 chat bot trained to analyze your performance, track your data, and provide personalized feedback.

This AI coaching feature enhances training by identifying patterns, optimizing strategies, and reducing injury risk through real-time monitoring. Your AI coach can simulate match scenarios, evaluate opponents, and suggest tactical improvements. This makes training more efficient, precise, and data-driven, supporting BJJ athletes in reaching peak performance. 

Built By BJJ Athletes For BJJ Athletes

The creators of Brain-Jiu-Jitsu, Kazu and Bryan, recognized that - despite their different backgrounds and levels within the sport - they shared the same challenges with organization, consistency, and motivation.  They then set out nearly two years ago to build a tool to manage their own training and progress in the sport.  It was a project - fueled purely on passion and enthusiasm for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu - to create the app that they wanted.  However, as the project took shape and the potential of mobile app development technology realized, they set their sights higher.  According to co-founder, Bryan Kelly, "We have built the app that we wanted based on our own specifications.   Now we want to engage other athletes around the world to deliver the app that appeals to the needs of the broader BJJ community".

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu was created by Kazuhiro Miyachi, 3rd degree Black Belt and top competitor from Japan, and Bryan Kelly, a Masters level Blue Belt from California and living in Japan.  Though at very different places in their respective BJJ journeys, both expressed similar experiences with struggling to find structure within the complexities of the sport.  In sharing how each were using technology to support their own training - and the limitations of these existing tools - the concept for a dedicated BJJ training support application was born.

Kazuhiro Miyachi, Co-Creator and Lead Content Director

Kazu has been training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu for almost 20 years.  After winning the IBJJF Asian Championship in the Adult Brown Belt division, he earned his black belt in 2014. The following year, he won the IBJJF Asian Championship in the Adult Black Belt Light Featherweight division. Kazu now holds a 3rd degree black belt and continues to train and compete in Japan and around the world.  He is currently the highest-ranking Japanese Masters 1 IBJJF competitor following his most recent tournament win at the 2025 Asian Championship.

Outside of Jiu-Jitsu, Kazu is associate professor of structural engineering at a prestigious Japanese university. Interestingly, this field shares many parallels with Jiu-Jitsu—concepts like balance, force equilibrium, and load transfer are crucial in both disciplines.  He wanted to apply this analytical approach to Jiu-Jitsu by visually organizing techniques and skills to create a more efficient training method.

Bryan Kelly, Co-Creator and Strategic Partner

Bryan is an American who began training BJJ after moving to Japan with his family in 2021.  He earned his blue belt upon winning the Masters 2 Middleweight division at the De La Riva Cup in 2023 and continues to train often and compete occasionally in Kanagawa, Japan.

Use of AI

Brain-Jiu-Jitsu uses GPT4 to generate unique and insightful feedback based on the user's input. Like ChatGPT, the AI utilizes a call and response interface for users to ask questions and request analysis of their performance and challenges. The AI module has been trained to access the content of the application, but Brain-Jiu-Jitsu is not involved in the development of any new AI tools used in the project.
