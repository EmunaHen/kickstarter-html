Story

Why FoodBurp?

Cooking every day is hard. Doing it alone? Even harder.

Whether you're a student juggling classes, a busy professional, or a parent managing a household — making meals from scratch every single day is exhausting.

  • Now imagine living in a dorm or apartment building and not even knowing the person next door.
  • Imagine cooking a little extra food, but throwing it away because you didn’t know who to offer it to.
  • Imagine being hungry, but holding back from asking because it feels awkward, even shameful.

That’s the problem. Not the lack of food — but the lack of connection.

  • What if the person cooking next door could simply make one extra portion? 
  • And what if all it took was one tap to let them know you're hungry?

That’s what FoodBurp is all about.

A simple way to turn cooking into connection. A way to make sharing feel natural again.

Problems We’re Solving

1. No one enjoys saying “I’m hungry”, especially when it feels uncomfortable or embarrassing.

We even tried wearing boards around campus to break the ice and spark conversations. It worked — but let’s be honest, not everyone can walk around like this when they need a meal. With FoodBurp, you don’t have to ask. One tap, and your surroundings know. No questions. No shame. Just help, naturally.

2. Unused Meal Swipes, Untapped Potential

Many universities in the U.S. offer meal swipes — prepaid credits that students use to get food at on-campus dining halls. But here’s the problem: students often don’t use all of them.

Between busy schedules, skipped meals, or eating off-campus, thousands of students waste 2–3 meal swipes every week. That’s not just lost food — it’s hundreds of thousands of dollars thrown away each semester.

  • Even President Biden called out the growing issue of student meal plan waste — urging universities to refund unused swipe balances back to students. But the response? Most campuses gave students the option to donate their swipes. No real system. No real change.
    FoodBurp bridges that gap — and built a win-win solution.

3. Extra Food, No One to Share It With
Cooking a little extra is easy. Sharing it shouldn’t be hard. FoodBurp makes it effortless to let others know you’ve got food to offer — so nothing goes to waste and everything goes to someone who needs it.

4. Eating Alone, Feeling Disconnected
Mealtimes should be social. We turn solo dinners into shared experiences — whether it's joining a cook-together session or just eating with someone new. With FoodBurp, you don’t eat alone unless you want to.

Now it's time to know what is food burp!

We are building a peer to peer food sharing application. Anyone who is hungry with just a click of a button could tell their surroundings know that they are hungry by entering their budget that they could spend for that meal. Anyone having extra food or about to start cooking would be connected. Let’s walk you through what makes FoodBurp more than just another food app.

Making Shared Meals and Asking Meals the New Normal

  • Find Food Nearby — Whether It’s Shared, Extra, or What You Can Afford : 
    Whether you're low on cash before payday, craving something different, too tired to cook, or just curious about what your neighbor’s making — FoodBurp makes it easy to find food and connection, right where you are.
  • Cook Together, Not Alone : Cooking doesn’t have to be a solo chore. With Cook Together, you can join or host friendly group sessions — from cozy pasta nights to weekend biryani feasts. It’s more than just making food; it’s about sharing stories, laughter, and real connection. People around you can show interest, and with just a tap, we help make it happen.
  • Location-Based Sharing : FoodBurp uses your location smartly. You can choose FoodBurp uses your location smartly — but on your terms. Set how far you're willing to go, from the dorm next door to a few miles out, and instantly discover real-time meal offers nearby. Whether you're hungry, homesick, or just craving something made with care, there’s likely someone close by who’s happy to share or cook.

Conversations That Go Beyond the Plate

At the heart of FoodBurp, it's not just about the food — it’s about the people.

That’s why we made messaging simple, friendly, and secure. Once a meal is offered or accepted, you can chat directly with the other person — to coordinate pick-up, say thanks, or even just ask how their day’s going. 
Because sometimes, a simple message over food can turn strangers into neighbors — and a platform into a community that feels like home.

Every Plate Has a Story

  • Your Food Diary: Meals Worth Remembering : Every dish you cook, share, or receive tells a story. With FoodBurp’s Diary, you can capture those delicious moments — from the meal that reminded you of home to the one that made a new friend.
    Add photos, fun notes, and personal reflections. Beyond memories, your diary also helps you track calories, dietary balance, and food patterns — so by the end of the month, you’ll know not just what you ate, but how it made you feel.

    Your food journey is your story — remember it, celebrate it.
  • Food Listings - Share What’s on Your Plate : Cooked a little extra? Want to earn some pocket money or cover your grocery bill? List your meal on FoodBurp and let someone nearby enjoy it. Just snap a photo, set a price (or give it free), share your location, and add a short note. That’s it. 
    You’re not just sharing food — you’re creating moments, saving money, and maybe even meeting someone special.
    Who needs a dating app when you can cook? Your future person might just be next door — and hungry.
     

Our Traction So Far

  • FoodBurp didn’t start with an app — it started with action. 

To validate the idea, we built real communities across 38 U.S. universities. Each campus had a local community lead and a dedicated volunteer team — over 300 student volunteers in total — managing everything manually, from organizing food shares to partnering with local restaurants. All of this was coordinated through WhatsApp groups and spreadsheets.

  • The result? Over 14,000 students joined us — before the app even existed. No marketing. No incentives. Just real need, and a system that worked.


When we pitched FoodBurp at Purdue’s New Venture Challenge and won Crowd Favorite — because the solution was real, and the impact was already visible.

Beyond Campus: The Bigger Vision

Food is food — and sharing it is universal. If this works in the U.S., it will work anywhere. And we’ve already started testing that.

We’ve expanded early pilots to 5 universities each in Canada and Australia, replicating the same model with promising initial response. Our focus for now remains on international students, who often face the highest barriers when it comes to food access, affordability, and community to start with.

But FoodBurp isn’t just for students — and it’s not just for the U.S.

  • Our long-term vision is to become the go-to platform for peer-to-peer food sharing globally — whether that’s across campuses, neighborhoods, or entire cities.

As we scale, we’re laying the groundwork to:

  • Open FoodBurp to everyone — students, families, neighbors — anyone willing to share or receive a meal.
  • Expand beyond campuses, city by city, turning apartment buildings and neighborhoods into connected food-sharing hubs.
  • Empower verified home chefs to serve their communities safely, with proper training and certifications built into the platform.

We’re building the future of shared food.

Risks and challenges

We're building FoodBurp carefully to ensure a safe and reliable experience for everyone. Here are a few things to keep in mind: • Delivery features between users will take time to roll out safely. We're aiming for a launch between January and March 2026. • Our security system is strong for students now, but expanding it to non-student users will require additional verification tools, expected by early 2026. • Home chef onboarding may be slower in some states due to different food safety laws. We'll start in a few regions and expand step by step. We expect all core features to be fully in place by Jan–March 2026. Thanks to your support, we’ll get there the right way.
