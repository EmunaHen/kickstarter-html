Story

A Gallup Survey in 2023 found that almost 90% of employees of the 34m employees in the UK, are unhappy or feel dissatisfied with their role at work (there are similar statistics for many other countries).  This represents a huge number of people who consider that the demands of their job, cause them excessive stress and this position is not significantly improving. This negative situation needs to be addressed.  Therefore from these humble beginnings - activated through real life individual employee challenges, a passionate drive to encourage, enable and enthuse employees to thrive through work was created - presenting a transformational journey for individuals to maximise expectations of employers by taking the initiative to drive their own career. To do this, affordable, accessible, professional, tried and tested 'tools' are required.   

The Founder is Carol Mote (https://www.linkedin.com/in/carolmote/) who having worked in corporate life throughout her career as a leader of People & Culture aspects through organisational change, set up The Maple Kind in 2023. In 2024, Carol left her corporate role to create a platform to transfer information, experience, tips and depth of knowledge to individuals, to thrive in their own careers.  The 'inside information' shared (behind the scenes insights relating to how companies think, plan and manage people), creates the ability and momentum to overcome obstacles with guidance for individuals on how to use the information to their advantage; meeting personal ambitions, in their own way, with timing to suit their personal circumstances.

WorkVibes, creates a whole level of new engagement at work - led by the individual.

Over the past 5 years working life has significantly changed.  In most sectors and functions, employee numbers have decreased; although automation has enhanced aspects, in many cases there are less employees to undertake increased workloads. Wellbeing at work, is under pressure in many areas, however it remains key to how people turn up and contribute.  

The vision is:  new possibilities for your situation.

There are 4 key areas that WorkVibes addresses:

Area 1:  Expectations between employers and employees continues to evolve - in many cases both want more from each other and both feel under pressure.  WorkVibes clarifies employee expectations and identifies how employee expectations can be delivered, to meet individual needs and aspirations.

Area 2:  Employment Return on Investment; the requirement for employees to 'fit in' and excel continually (with limited support) is high.  WorkVibes provides a range of mechanisms and new language to enable employees to create a new and manageable approach to suit their needs.

Area 3:  Employee ownership of their role - to earn their worth, individuals need to become fully accountable for their individual performance, capability and skill development.  WorkVibes highlights areas where these factors can be positively addressed to the advantage of the individual.

Area 4:  The talent spectrum is enhanced in all aspects with an increase of pragmatic, collaborative individuals who are culturally agile and motivated to thrive at work. WorkVibes shares the ingredients for individuals to reframe their experience in order to excel, upgrade their work 'adventure' and thrive, aligning with where they want to be. 

Since 2024 The Maple Kind has produced over 1000 freely available clips on incremental activity individuals can take to improve their working life (available on Linkedin, TikTok, Instagram and Facebook).  In addition an Amazon shop, selling non digital products, and a stan.store selling digital and non digital produces were also established. 

In 2025 it became clear that the passion to transfer information to support individuals on a 24/7 basis was better served through the creation of a Mobile App, harnessing all the information (created to date and waiting to be created); ensuring ease of connectivity, reliability and speed.  Therefore Carol Mote started researching the best route to move the different platforms which The Maple Kind currently has, onto one central platform with global access; creating the framework for the new WorkVibes Mobile App. 

WorkVibes aims to launch in September 2025 in English and it will have three subscription levels from £10 to £30 per month.  WorkVibes is being created to be accessible and affordable as priorities - aware that Coaching can cost thousands of GBP (or other currency) and Mentoring can be expensive and lacking in impartiality. Through the 'Pic 'n' Mix' options, individuals can explore a wide range of aspects to positively influence/transform their working life; and then 'delve deeper' when they wish to focus on a particular element.  Although initially the Mobile App will be in English, the ambition is to increase language availability and global reach. 

This is a unique opportunity to become a positive influencer on the world of work; helping to change things for the better, supporting the return of a sense of fun and exploration around the jobs individuals undertake.  The time is right to regain a positive and relevant 'employee voice'.

Risks and challenges

Anticipated risks with mitigation to resolve, include: Delivery delays - will need to moderate marketing activity align with APP launch and utilise backup vendors where required, monitoring non-digital shipments in real-time. Data Breach - implement data encryption and conduct regular security audits. Budget Overrun - set contingency budgets and track expenses regularly. Resource Shortage - optimise resource allocation and hire additional staff on fixed term basis aligned with sales achievements. and Regulatory Changes - stay updated on regulations and involve professional services including legal advisors to ensure compliance.
