And that’s exactly what Qalzy does.

Qalzy's OpenAI-powered camera instantly recognizes raw, cooked or packaged foods and automatically logs their calories, macros, vitamins and minerals, all in just a tap.

Use of AI

Qalzy uses artificial intelligence (AI) to automatically recognize the food you place on the scale, so you don’t have to type or search for it manually. When you put something on the scale, a built-in camera takes a photo. That image is then analyzed by Qalzy’s AI, which has been trained on millions of food photos from around the world. It compares what it sees to this massive library and identifies what the food is — whether it’s an apple, a steak, a slice of pizza, or a full mixed meal. Then, it combines that visual identification with the precise weight of the food to calculate the calories and nutrients (like protein, fat, and carbs). Everything is sent straight to the app, where you can confirm or adjust it if needed.
