Risks and challenges

1. Production & Supply Chain Risks​​ The global supply chain for specialized components like carbon fiber remains vulnerable to geopolitical tensions, logistics bottlenecks, and unexpected delays. During initial mass production, quality consistency could fluctuate as assembly teams refine processes. To mitigate these risks, we’ve established ​​dual-supplier agreements​​ with carbon fiber manufacturers in China and Vietnam, securing a 3-month inventory buffer for critical parts. Our partnership with TÜV Rheinland ensures rigorous quality control through AI-powered automated inspection lines, targeting a ≥99% pass rate. ​​2. Technical & Safety Challenges​​ Operating in extreme marine environments exposes components to accelerated corrosion from high-salinity seawater. Additionally, novice riders may face balance issues during high-speed maneuvers or abrupt turns. Our engineering team has subjected core structures to ​​72-hour salt spray testing​​ and ​​3x military-grade load simulations​​, with titanium alloy connectors enhancing corrosion resistance. The integrated ​​AI Posture Correction System​​ dynamically adjusts power distribution to prevent capsizing, while complimentary training programs at 50+ partner watersports schools worldwide ensure safe onboarding. ​​3. Regulatory & Market Risks​​ Divergent regional regulations may restrict electric watercraft usage zones, while market adoption of this emerging category requires significant user education. We’ve preemptively aligned with ​​EU CE, US USCG, and China CCS compliance frameworks​​, supported by a dedicated legal team monitoring policy changes. To accelerate market penetration, we’re collaborating with top-tier influencers to launch the "FO Water Challenge" – a viral campaign combining competitive events with educational content. ​​4. Funding & Delivery Risks​​ Overwhelming crowdfunding demand could strain production capacity, and volatile shipping costs may impact profitability. Our ​​phased delivery model​​ prioritizes in-house production for the first 50 units, with excess orders managed through vetted regional OEM partners under strict quality oversight.
