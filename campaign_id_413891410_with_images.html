Story

A new wave in Space Propulsion

Space has always been a symbol of human curiosity and innovation, a frontier where dreams meet challenges. At Capsule Corporation srl, we share that spirit and want to make space exploration cleaner, smarter, and more accessible.

What is WaterCube?

WaterCube is a revolutionary propulsion system powered by something simple and abundant: water. Designed for small satellites, it’s eco-friendly, scalable, and crafted using cutting-edge 3D printing technology. And we are proud to say it truly is a Made in Italy piece of design and engineering.

But WaterCube is more than technology. It’s a mission to change how we explore space, to build a sustainable and accessible future beyond and for Earth. 

And we want you to be part of it. How? Well, we want your name to fly to orbit with us, take a look at the video hereinafter!

Why this matters

Traditional satellite propulsion systems often rely on toxic or scarce fuels, limiting their potential and increasing costs. WaterCube challenges that status quo, unlocking new opportunities for small satellite missions worldwide.

Our Team was driven by a bold question: can we rethink propulsion from the ground up, using safe and eco-friendly materials? After years of research, prototypes, and collaborations with top experts from Politecnico di Milano university and the European Space Agency, we created WaterCube: a breakthrough propulsion system that combines advanced metal 3D printing with both in-house designed and commercial components to leverage the power of water for orbital maneuvers!

The Beginning

It all started from the two Co-founders Ivan and Francesco. They were crazy enough to believe in this project and started building up a first prototype at home!

After this first little yet big success, they moved to a garage to get more room for their mad-scientist experiments! Here, the prototype was improved and thoroughly tested to verify its functionality and actual technological feasibility.

Things got serious

The two guys were actually really crazy. So, they decided to apply for an European Space Agency (ESA) grant to get enough funds to move forward with WaterCube development. And they won it! They incorporated the company in Italy as an "innovative startup" and named it Capsule Corporation srl (bonus points if you know where the name comes from!) in May 2024. Since then, they advanced the development of the WaterCube prototypes and gathered an amazing Team to help them realize the vision of sustainable and accessible propulsion for every satellite!

We completed the "Engineering Model" EM-01 prototype in October 2024, to show full functionality with flight model-like size, performance and operations. The test campaign was fully successful, and paved the way for further improvements and refinements in the next iterations.

The first recognitions

Once we completed the development of the first fully functional prototype "EM-01", we started attending aerospace trade shows and events to showcase the product and our vision for sustainable and accessible propulsion. The first feedbacks were extremely positive, as we won several startup competitions impressing the audience with our mission and vision!

We were even selected to represent the Italian startup ecosystem in the prestigious START Summit pitch competition!!!

Now, we are heading full steam (pun intended) into the flight demonstration of WaterCube and we want you to be part of it as well!!!

We just successfully completed the test campaign on the new prototype "EM-02", and are now completing the design of the flight model WaterCube that will fly to orbit in 2026 with the In-Orbit Demonstration (IOD) mission on a partner satellite.

This is where we need your help! Now, we’re preparing to build and launch our flight model into orbit for its IOD (in-orbit demonstration), a milestone that will prove WaterCube’s real capabilities in space. This campaign will fund the final development and production of the WaterCube flight model.

By backing WaterCube, you join a community pioneering a cleaner, smarter way to explore space. Together, we’ll demonstrate that innovation can be simple, sustainable, yet spectacular.

The jargon of space missions can sometimes feel like a different language: full of acronyms, tech terms, and mysterious abbreviations. But don’t worry! We’re here to make it all clear.

Whether you're a seasoned space geek or just curious about how a satellite can maneuver in orbit using water, this glossary will help you follow every step of our mission with confidence.

Below, you'll find easy-to-understand explanations of the most common terms you'll encounter as we build, test, and launch WaterCube: our water-powered propulsion system designed for CubeSats and SmallSats.

Because space is for everyone - and understanding is the first step to becoming part of it.

Alphabetic list glossary

🛠️ Acceptance Testing
The acceptance tests are the last series of tests performed on the WaterCube flight model on ground, before it is packed and shipped to the satellite platform for the final assembly and launch preparation.

🧲 Attitude Control
Not an emotional issue! It refers to controlling the satellite’s orientation in space. WaterCube can assist in attitude maneuvers like detumbling or reorienting.

🔌 Avionics Board (AVB)
The electronics that manage the energy needed to operate the thruster. In our case, it controls the valves actuation, resistors heating and timing of WaterCube's firing.

🚀 CubeSat
A class of small satellites made of 10x10x10 cm (4x4x4 in) units (called “U”). A 1U CubeSat is one unit, a 3U is three units stacked, and so on. WaterCube is designed to fit these compact satellite platforms.

🎯 Delta-V (Δv)
A measure of the change in velocity a thruster can provide. The higher the Δv, the more the spacecraft can maneuver and modify its orbit in space.

🛠️ Engineering Model (EM)
A prototype built with the same dimensions and interfaces as the final version, used for testing integration, assembly, and verification, but not for flying. It's how we make sure everything fits and works before building the flight unit. WaterCube has already seen the development of two EMs: EM-01 and EM-02. Now, we are moving forward with the flight version!

🪐 Flight Model (FM)
The final version of the system that is actually launched into space. Built and tested to meet all flight requirements, the FM is the culmination of the design, engineering, and qualification process.

♻️ Green Propulsion
Environmentally friendly propulsion systems that use non-toxic and easily storable materials. WaterCube’s use of water makes it a prime example.

🌐 Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC)
The system that keeps a spacecraft pointing the right way and going where it should. WaterCube integrates easily with standard GNC systems.

📅 In-Orbit Demonstration (IOD)
A mission that proves a technology works in space conditions. This Kickstarter campaign supports WaterCube’s IOD: our first flight in orbit!

🔁 Specific Impulse (ISP)
A measure of propulsion efficiency: the higher the ISP, the more “bang” you get for your “propellant.” WaterCube is optimized for all the typical maneuvers in LEO orbits.

🚀 Launch Campaign
The set of operations that prepare the satellite and its components for launch, including final tests, integration, shipment, and coordination with the launch provider. It’s the last step before take-off.

🌍 Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
An orbit typically between 200 and 2,000 km above Earth’s surface, where most CubeSats and Smallsats operate and where WaterCube will be tested in its IOD.

📦 Payload
The part of the satellite that performs the mission, like a camera, a telescope, or a sensor. WaterCube is therefore typically not the payload, but it helps the payload reach or maintain its target orbit.

🧳 Plug-and-Play
Means easy integration. WaterCube is designed to be modular and customizable, ready to be added to different satellite configurations without major redesign.

⚙️ Qualification Model (QM)
The Qualification Model of a space system: it is a version of the system subjected to extreme testing (thermal, vibration, vacuum) to prove the design is flight-ready. Unlike the EM, it undergoes more aggressive testing, but also stays on the ground.

🔥 Resistojet
A type of thruster where a gas or a fluid (like water) is heated with an electric resistor and expelled to produce thrust: simple, safe, and efficient for small spacecrafts propulsion applications.

🧯 Safe-to-Launch
A key requirement for anything going into space. It means the system is non-toxic, non-explosive, and safe for integration with other satellite components. WaterCube checks all these boxes.

🛰️ SmallSat
Short for "Small Satellite": a broader category that includes CubeSats and other miniaturized spacecraft, usually under 500 kg.

💧 Water-Based Propulsion
A system like WaterCube that uses demineralized water as a propellant. When heated and vaporized, it produces thrust: simple, green, and safe.

💥 Thrust
The force produced by a propulsion system to move a spacecraft. In WaterCube, thrust comes from vaporized water expelled through a nozzle.

🧪 Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
A scale from 1 to 9 used by space agencies to assess how mature a technology is: from concept (TRL 1) to flight-proven (TRL 9). WaterCube is currently at TRL6 and is moving toward TRL 8 with this mission.

📡 Telemetry
Data sent from the satellite to Earth, allowing mission operators to monitor health, status, and performance, including that of WaterCube.

🚀 Thruster
A device that generates small amounts of force to control a satellite's position or trajectory. WaterCube is a resistojet-type thruster.
