Based on Backer feedback, we identified three major pain points in existing docking solutions:
• Overloaded desktops caused by the need for separate hubs—one for charging, one for display, another for data transfer.
• Poor power coordination, where multi-port chargers fail to balance output efficiently, slowing charging when multiple devices are plugged in.
• No real-time feedback, leaving users uncertain about power delivery, display resolution, or network status.

To solve these problems, we designed a next-generation Docking Station with a built-in 160W GaN+ power supply, combining fast, intelligent charging, professional-grade expansion, and intuitive system feedback. With features like dual 4K displays, smart display readouts, and a compact design, it offers everything needed for a clean, powerful, and seamless workstation experience—whether you're working, creating, or gaming.

The docking station supports single-, dual-, three-, and four-port output combinations, adapting perfectly to a wide range of devices—from smartphones and tablets to laptops.

For single-port high-power output, the HOST/C1/C2 interfaces support the PD 3.0 protocol, with a maximum output of 100W and multiple voltage/current options (e.g., 5V3A, 9V3A). The USB-A port provides 12W (5V/ 2.4A)—ideal for headphones, smartwatches, and other low-power accessories.

Smart Multi-Port Collaboration for Efficiency

When using multiple ports, the dock intelligently balances power:

Dual-port combinations (e.g., HOST + C1) can deliver up to 160W total.

Three- and four-port combinations automatically adjust power output, ensuring efficient, high-speed charging across all devices without compromise. This makes it perfect for business travel, shared workstations, or multi-device setups.

Single-port charging: The maximum output power for each individual port.

Dual-port charging: Indicates the combined maximum power for any two ports in use.

Triple-port charging: Indicates the combined maximum power for any three ports in use.


Quad-port charging: The total available power distributed across all four ports.

Because the power supply is built directly into the docking station, charging is as simple as connecting a cable to the AC port—no bulky external chargers required. For global travelers, the dock supports international use with interchangeable plug standards. We provide four regional charging plugs: US, EU, UK, and AU, allowing you to stay powered in any country. Just swap the plug as needed—simple, convenient, and globally compatible.

The integrated intelligent screen offers real-time power tracking for up to four individual devices, plus a clear readout of total power consumption. A dedicated button lets you instantly toggle display info—viewing HDMI resolution, refresh rate, and audio output specifications at a glance. No need to dive into system settings. Get a professional, visual overview of your external displays to ensure optimal 4K quality and audio compatibility. It’s power and performance—clearly displayed, effortlessly managed.

The two HDMI 2.0 ports support ultra-clear 4K@60Hz  output with HDR10, HDCP 1.4/2.3, and 192kHz stereo audio, delivering a truly immersive experience.

Whether you're splitting screens for multitasking—editing documents on one and streaming video on the other—or enjoying smooth 4K gaming and movies, this setup transforms your desk into a high-efficiency workstation or an entertainment hub. Design Process

The design journey wasn’t without its challenges. Integrating charging (up to 160W), 4K HDMI dual video output, 10Gbps high-speed data, and dynamic digital display into a compact, thermally-efficient enclosure required intricate power management and board layout planning. Balancing the heat generated by simultaneous high-wattage output and high-bandwidth transmission pushed us to prototype multiple PCB iterations. We also needed to ensure full compatibility with a wide range of fast-charging protocols and operating systems while keeping the interface intuitive.


One of the most critical design breakthroughs was the dynamic display module. We wanted users to see real-time power status, port utilization, and display parameters — all without accessing system settings. To achieve this, we developed a custom firmware that communicates directly with key IC components and displays relevant data on a built-in screen with one-touch toggling.

After countless months of testing, tuning, and user feedback, we successfully built the first functional prototype — a compact, smart hub that replaces three devices with one. It’s more than a tool — it’s a bridge between efficiency, portability, and control for the digital lifestyle.
