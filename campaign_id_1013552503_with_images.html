Story

🎬 A Creator’s Eureka

I’m Damian Bennett, and like many of you, I’ve wrestled with the chaos of sharing everything I do online. One day, in the middle of tweaking yet another clunky bio page, I realized: there has to be a better way. I didn’t just want a list of links—I wanted a living, breathing hub that felt like me.

So I grabbed a pencil and sketched an interface on the nearest scrap of paper. The idea was simple but powerful: design in real time, see every change instantly, and never settle for a cookie-cutter layout again. That spark of inspiration lit a fire in me—I couldn’t let it go.

🔥 From Frustration to Creation

I dove in headfirst, writing code late into the night. Every line I typed was driven by a single goal: let creators spend less time wrestling with settings and more time sharing their passions. As the prototype took shape, I saw that this wasn’t just another “link page.” It was the beginning of a platform that would flex to your imagination, not force you into someone else’s mold.

🌟 What Drives Me

I believe that your online presence should be as unique as your fingerprint. Whether you’re a musician teasing a new single, an artist showcasing your portfolio, or a small business owner driving customers to your shop—your links deserve to shine. That’s why I’m pouring every ounce of energy into crafting a tool that’s intuitive, powerful, and oh-so-you.

Risks and challenges

🔒 Risks & Challenges—and How We’ll Overcome Them Bringing JustMyLinks from a spark of inspiration to a polished, reliable platform isn’t without its hurdles. Here’s where we see potential roadblocks—and exactly how we’ll tackle each one head-on. 1. Technical Complexity Risk: Building a true live-preview editor with drag-and-drop, theme customization, and instant updates can introduce bugs, performance slowdowns, or compatibility issues across browsers. Mitigation: Modular Architecture: We’ll break the editor into small, testable components so each feature gets its own quality focus. Progressive Rollout: Core features launch first; advanced custom-code editing comes later, reducing initial complexity. Automated Testing: Continuous integration with unit and end-to-end tests ensures new code never breaks existing functionality. 2. Scalability & Performance Risk: As users add more links, widgets, and custom CSS, pages could load slowly or time out under heavy traffic. Mitigation: Serverless Functions & Caching: Offload rendering to edge functions and cache static assets aggressively. Performance Budgets: We’ll enforce strict bundle-size limits and monitor real-user metrics to catch regressions early. Load Testing: Before launch, simulated traffic tests will identify bottlenecks so we can optimize database queries and CDN usage. 3. Data Privacy & Security Risk: Handling user authentication, analytics data, and custom uploads opens us to potential vulnerabilities or data leaks. Mitigation: Proven Services: We’re building on Supabase’s secure auth and storage layers, and adhering to OWASP best practices. Regular Audits: Scheduled security reviews and penetration tests will keep our defenses sharp. Encryption & Access Controls: All sensitive data is encrypted at rest and in transit, and role-based permissions prevent unauthorized access. 4. Feature Creep & Scope Creep Risk: Pressure to add more bells and whistles could delay launch or dilute the core experience. Mitigation: Lean MVP Philosophy: We’ll focus on the minimum set of features that deliver real value—everything else becomes a stretch goal. Backer Feedback Loops: Early backers get access to a private feedback channel; we’ll prioritize their requests against our roadmap. 10% Budget & Time Contingency: Reserved specifically for unexpected challenges or late-breaking refinements. 5. Competitive Landscape Risk: Established link-in-bio tools may copy standout features or undercut pricing once we launch. Mitigation: Differentiation Through UX: Our live-editing workflow and full-code customization can’t be trivially replicated. Community-Driven Growth: By engaging backers in feature decisions and celebrating their success stories, we build loyalty that’s harder to poach. Agile Iteration: Fast, iterative releases let us stay ahead—responding to user needs in days, not months.
