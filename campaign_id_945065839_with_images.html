Story

Pixo is a DIY educational kit that lets anyone—kids, teens, or curious adults—build and program their own game console from scratch.
No prior experience? No problem. Build it. Code it. Play it.

🔧 Assemble your console with real electronic components.
🧠 Learn to code with beginner-friendly lessons.
🎮 Play games you create—on a console you built.
🐒 Perfect for ages 8+, educators, and grown-up tinkerers too.

👋 Hi,

When I was a child, I was fascinated by the blinking lights of arcade machines, the sound of a new game loading, and the magic of electronics coming to life. But as I grew older, I realized something—technology often felt like a world built for a select few, a world where most of us were just users, not creators.

That never sat right with me.

Technology should be a playground for everyone. That’s what led me to create Pixo—a fun, hands-on way to dive into electronics and programming. Because I believe the best way to learn is through play, and with Pixo, I want to make sure that anyone, regardless of their background, can become a creator of technology—not just a user of it.

With your support, we can bring Pixo to the world and empower an entire generation to be creators, not just consumers of technology. 

In 2023, I launched the first 100 units of the Pixo Game Console, selling them exclusively in Uruguay as a product test to gather feedback, refine the support center, and develop the necessary tools to scale up. This allowed us to identify weak points and ensure we were ready for our global release. Now, we’re prepared to go big! 🚀

What is it?

Pixo is a learning kit designed to introduce kids, teens and whoever is interested to the world of programming and electronics in a practical and fun way.

STEP 1

The journey begins with building a gaming console, where users assemble the electronic components by following the instructions in the manual.

STEP 2

Once assembled, the console is programmed using interactive courses available on Pixo´s website. These courses teach the fundamentals of programming, allowing users to create and customize their own video games.

Currently Pixo has more than 30 excercises offering a comprehensive learning experience for various age groups:

  • For ages 8-11, we recommend mBlock, a block-based coding platform that introduces programming concepts in a fun, easy-to-understand way.
  • For ages 12 and up, users can dive deeper into C++ programming using the Arduino IDE, which offers more advanced coding challenges.

STEP 3

Finally, the games developed can be shared with an online community, fostering collaboration and idea exchange among young tech enthusiasts.

Today, we have 5 games developed by Pixo and 5 games created by members of our community—including contributions from Eduardo Veiga (CEO of Prometeo Open Banking), Agustín Suárez (CTO of Ventia), engineering students, and even a 14-year-old passionate about C++!

These are some of the games currently available, and we release a new one every month. 🎮

Product Design

Kit

The kit comes with 4 wooden pieces, which will be the body of the gaming console. We chose wood because it’s a sustainable and durable material. It can be customized with paint, stickers, or anything else you like, giving it that DIY look we’re aiming for. This not only makes it unique but also allows users to express their creativity while assembling their own console.

3 Different Designs

Custom Open-Source Board Design.

For the electronics, we created a custom design based on the Arduino Uno board. We wanted it to be an open-source board, so everyone can customize their code and, if they want, reuse the board for other electronic projects in the future. This gives users the freedom to explore, modify, and create without limitations, making the board both versatile and adaptable.

Pixo Board
The original PIXO module featuring the AVR ATmega328 microcontroller. It has 32 KB of Flash memory, 2 KB of RAM, 13 digital I/O pins (6 of which can be used as PWM channels), 5 analog inputs, and popular communication interfaces. Includes 4 GND ports and 5 voltage ports.

PIXO Button Module
The original PIXO button module includes 4 buttons and a buzzer. It has a VCC voltage input and a GND ground port.

Kit includes: Software

Courses in C++
The kit gives you access to 2 courses with 10 exercises each (a total of 20 exercises). These are the Introductory and Intermediate courses in C++, using the open-source Arduino Online platform.

Courses in Mblock-block base coding
Additionally, it includes an mBlock course with 10 exercises, which uses block-based coding. This course is generally recommended for younger children aged 8 to 11 years old.

Kit includes: Games

The kit comes with 7 pre-coded games: 5 created by the community and 2 developed by Pixo. These games are: Pong, Snake, Breakout, Rapidroll, Flappy Bird, Maze, and Tankblitz.

Additionally, there are 3 extra games available for purchase on the website. These games are: Block Building, Simon Says, and Connect 4.

About Pixo

Pixo started as an academic project in 2021 when Valentina Pereyra developed the concept of an educational gaming console as her Industrial Design thesis. What began as a university project quickly turned into a mission: to make electronics and coding accessible, fun, and hands-on for kids, teens and everyone who wants to embark on the journey!

In 2023, Pixo officially launched with the support of ANII (National Agency of Investigation & Innovation), producing its first 50 units. Since then, we’ve been on an incredible journey participating internationally in fairs like Nerdearla, and growing our community of young creators. We even started a collaboration with Holberton School, a globally recognized university, where students take their first steps in programming using the Pixo kit.

Risks and challenges

With Pixo, we have already successfully produced and delivered our first 50 kits in Uruguay, testing and refining both our product and production process. Our manufacturing partners are reliable, and our assembly and quality control have been thoroughly tested to ensure a smooth production cycle. However, one of our main challenges is expanding our logistics to ship worldwide. While we have experience delivering in Latin America, international shipping introduces new variables such as customs regulations, shipping delays, and unforeseen costs. We are actively researching and partnering with experienced logistics providers to ensure a seamless global fulfillment process. Additionally, as with any hardware product, supply chain disruptions could arise. To mitigate this, we have backup suppliers for critical components and a flexible production strategy that allows us to adapt if necessary. We are committed to transparent communication with our backers and will provide regular updates on our progress, addressing any challenges head-on to ensure that Pixo reaches you on time.

Long-lasting design

I have designed my project to be durable, using high-quality materials and components. All parts of Pixo can be replaced or repaired with local suppliers, ensuring that users can maintain and extend the lifespan of their kits without needing to discard them. Additionally, Pixo is built to be easily upgradable, allowing continuous use and adaptation over time.

Reusability and recyclability

Pixo's hardware is open-source, which means it can be repurposed for other personal projects beyond its original use. This encourages creativity and sustainability by allowing users to modify, expand, or integrate Pixo into new electronic and programming experiments.

Sustainable materials

I plan to use upcycled materials such as recycled plastics and sustainable wood for the construction of Pixo’s casing and packaging. Whenever possible, I will also prioritize suppliers who adhere to environmentally friendly production practices.
