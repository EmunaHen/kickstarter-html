‎‎‎Compatibility 

Fits More Pegs. Tunes More Instruments.

Roadie has always supported a wide range of instruments, but with a newly redesigned peg connector, Roadie 4 now fits both smaller and larger pegs more comfortably. Its rubberized texture grips tuning pegs more securely and turns smoothly with no slipping, no shaking, just steady, quiet tuning.

It works with any instrument that uses geared pegs, including electric guitars with 6, 7, 8 or 12 strings, acoustic and classical guitars, ukuleles, mandolins, banjos, bouzoukis, lap steels and much more. Whatever you play, Roadie 4 is ready to tune it with speed and precision. Bass guitars, however, have heavier strings and require more torque than Roadie 4 is designed to deliver. That’s why we created Roadie Bass, a separate tuner specifically built to handle the needs of bass instruments. It’s already available on the market, and you can order it directly through our website.

How It Works

Roadie 4 uses a built-in contact microphone and advanced audio processing to detect string vibrations, analyze pitch, and automatically turn the tuning peg with incredible precision. No focus needed. Just hold it in place and trust it to get the job done right every time.

We completely revamped the audio circuit and tuning algorithm, making Roadie 4 noticeably faster, more accurate, and more reliable than ever before.

Tune Your Instrument in 3 Simple Steps:

[New!] Auto-Detect
By default, Roadie 4 tunes from the lowest to the highest string. But with the new auto-detect feature, you can pluck any string and Roadie 4 will instantly recognize and tune it. It beeps and vibrates when the string is in tune.

What's New

Winder & Metronome

Restring, Tune, and Track. All in One.

Roadie 4 features a built-in winder with three speed settings, giving you full control over how you restring your instrument. Whether you prefer a fast wind or a slower, more controlled approach, the winder makes changing strings smoother than ever.

And beyond the dedicated winder feature, Roadie 4 also streamlines the process from within the tuner itself. When you're replacing a string, you can now wind it and immediately tune it in one smooth step. It’s a fast, fluid experience that keeps you focused on your music, not the setup.

Watch it in Action

We’ve reinvented the way you restring your instrument. Roadie 4 automates both winding and tuning in one smooth, seamless step, no more manual effort, just a fast process that gets you back to playing in seconds.

But here's where it gets even smarter: with the companion Roadie Tuner app, you can now log each string change. Ever found a string you loved but couldn’t remember the brand or gauge later? Or wished you had a reminder to change your strings before a gig or session? The app helps you track what you used, when you changed it, and when it's time to swap again, so your instrument always sounds its best.

Play in Perfect Time, With Beep or Vibration

Roadie 4’s built-in metronome has been upgraded to give you full control over tempo, time signature, and now rhythm. Set your BPM, choose your beat count, and add rhythmic subdivisions like eighth notes or triplets to match your style. With vibration mode, you can feel the rhythm instead of just hearing it, perfect for practicing quietly or playing in a loud environment.

Customization

Tune Your Way. Customize Everything.

Roadie 4 is packed with advanced features designed for musicians who like things just right. Whether you want faster tuning, ultra-precise adjustments, or custom setups tailored to your instrument and sound, Roadie 4 gives you real control. Every detail is customizable, so it works exactly the way you want it to.

Set Roadie to “Tune Up”
With the optional Tune Up setting, Roadie 4 finishes all tunings by tightening the peg and locking in your tuning even more securely. Many players swear by this method, while others prefer the default. The choice is yours.

Create Custom Tunings
Whether you're tuning to tempered or alternate tunings, Roadie 4 makes it easier than ever. You can adjust each string in cents to match your ear or specific setup. If you’ve fitted your guitar with a custom nut or bridge to compensate for intonation, you can create tunings tailored to that exact configuration. For floating bridge guitars like a Floyd Rose, follow the recommended string order (Low E, High E, B, A, G, D) to maintain equal tension across the bridge. Save your custom tuning once and access it anytime with just a tap.

Tune with a Capo
Using a capo? No problem. Just tell Roadie which fret it's on, keep the capo on, and your preset tunings will adjust automatically so Roadie tunes for the key you're playing in.

Adjust Your Reference Pitch
Not everyone tunes to A=440 Hz. Roadie 4 lets you shift the reference pitch anywhere between 420 Hz and 460 Hz, in 1 Hz increments (perfect for historical pieces, orchestral settings, or matching fixed-pitch instruments).

Fast or Higher Accuracy Tuning 
Roadie 4 comes set to "Fast Tuning" by default: quick, reliable, and accurate enough for everyday playing. But when you need extra precision, like during recording sessions, just switch to "High Accuracy" mode. It fine-tunes each note with even greater detail, giving you a perfectly polished result.

[NEW] Quick Tune: Your Shortcuts to Tuning
By popular demand from our Roadie users, we’ve introduced Quick Tune, a feature designed to get you tuning faster than ever. When enabled, your Roadie 4 will automatically open to your last tuned instrument or a favorite as soon as you power it on, so you're ready to go immediately. 

And now, Roadie 4 also comes preloaded with a few common instrument profiles, so you can start tuning right out of the box. While we still recommend creating full instrument profiles for precision and advanced features, you now have a shortcut when speed matters.

So whether you're picking up where you left off, testing a guitar in a store, or helping a friend tune in a pinch, these quick-access options save you time.

Project Timeline

Meet The Team

Back in 2013, two music-loving engineers, Hassane Slaibi and Bassam Jalgha, set out to solve a problem: musicians were spending too much time tuning and not enough time playing. So they built the first Roadie Tuner. It was smart and automatic and earned global attention, including the TechCrunch Disrupt 2014 Audience Choice Award.

Since then, we’ve kept building: Roadie 2, Roadie Bass, Roadie 3 and Roadie Coach, each one smarter, faster, and more intuitive. Along the way, bands started recording and touring with our tools, and our community kept growing.

Now it’s time for Roadie 4, our most advanced tuner yet, powered by the smartest tuning technology we’ve ever built.

Behind it all is a small, passionate team of musicians and makers who love what they do, and each other. We’re still tuning, still dreaming, and still creating tools to help you do what you love most: make music.
