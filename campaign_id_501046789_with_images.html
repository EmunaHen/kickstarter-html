Story

I’ve always loved building things.

Not just for the sake of creating, but because I believe life is about movement — momentum — the magic that happens when you take an idea, shape it, and bring it into the world. I’ve chased that feeling for as long as I can remember.

But then something started happening around me.

People I knew — friends, classmates, people who seemed totally “normal” on the outside — started saying things like:

"I feel numb."
"I don’t care anymore."
"I don’t want to be alive."

And not in a dramatic way. In a flat, quiet, matter-of-fact way. Like they didn’t even think it was worth saying out loud.

It shocked me.

Because while I’m someone who struggles with my own doubts like anyone else, I’m also someone who wakes up every day excited by the idea of more. I don’t fear death because I want it — I fear death because I might not get to do everything I want to.

And the contrast between those two worlds — the fire I feel for living and the numbness I kept hearing in others — hit me hard.

I didn’t want to just sit with that.

So I started building One More Thing.

One More Thing is a simple but powerful mental health companion built to support people through hard moments, emotional numbness, or burnout. It’s for anyone who feels stuck and doesn’t know what to do next.

The idea is simple: we give you one thing to do every day — something small but meaningful — based on how you're actually feeling. Whether you're overwhelmed, anxious, unmotivated, or lost, One More Thing helps you take one step forward.

What It Includes:

Milo (Your Support Bot) – A 24/7 AI-powered listener who adapts to your emotional tone (e.g. “Just Listen,” “I’m Not Okay,” “Give Me Hope”) and gives you calm, reassuring responses.

One More Thing Daily – A short quiz scans your emotional state and gives you one personalized task designed to improve your mindset — from self-care actions to short outdoor breaks or reflection prompts.

Future Me – You write a message to your future self, and our system decides the best moment to send it back to you — right when it could matter most.

One More Place – You drop your zip code and your mood, and we send you a nearby place to visit that fits your vibe — plus a positive message about why.

MicroThings – A button you can click when you feel frozen. It gives you quick, actionable suggestions that break you out of emotional standstills.

This platform was designed with one goal: to help people feel just a little more alive and supported — even when nothing else seems to work.

— Oliver

Risks and challenges

While One More Thing is already up and running, the real challenge is keeping it personal and sustainable as we grow. Every message costs money to send and generate, and if we scale too quickly without support, we could hit limits. We also know this project sits in a sensitive space emotionally — it’s not therapy, and we never want it to be misunderstood. That’s why we’re being intentional, transparent, and careful with every step forward. With your help, we can grow without losing what makes this special.

Use of AI

I plan to use AI-generated content in my project.

What parts of your project will use AI generated content? Please be as specific as possible.

We use AI to generate supportive messages based on emotional tone and user input — but every feature, prompt style, and tone has been designed and trained by our team to ensure it’s helpful, safe, and appropriate for mental health use. This is not a general-purpose AI tool or chatbot. It’s a custom experience built around carefully selected prompts, response categories, and behavior settings. We’ve created a framework for how the AI responds — especially during moments when users select phrases like “I’m not okay” or “Just listen.” Every message type, emotional category, quiz result, and follow-up is the result of human creativity, mental health insight, and empathy. Our team wrote the emotional scripts. We defined the moods. We chose the actions that get recommended. AI is just the delivery method and voice— we control its mind and message. We’re not building a general-purpose chatbot or open-ended AI tool. We’re building a mental health experience that delivers calm, connection, and momentum — and that’s something only humans can design with real care.

Do you have the consent of owners of the works that were (or will be) used to produce the AI generated portion of your projects? Please explain.

Yes. For AI-generated messaging, we are using OpenAI’s API under a paid license, which grants the rights to use and distribute content generated by their models commercially. For AI-generated visuals, we used original prompts and commercially licensed tools that do not rely on copyrighted or unlicensed material. All artwork has been edited and made original, and we do not use or repurpose works from specific artists without permission.

Sustainable Distribution
