Story

The Future of Your Kitchen Starts Here

Imagine a kitchen that doesn’t just store your ingredients… but knows them.

Knows how fresh they are. Knows how much is left. Knows what you can cook next — and helps you waste less, every single day.

Meet Aromi — Your Ingredient’s Smartest Companion

Aromi is a seamless ingredient management system that transforms how you store, track, and cook. It’s built for those who want a kitchen that thinks ahead, so you don’t have to.

Here’s how it works:

Smart Jars that show you exactly how much is left at a glance

Minimal NFC Tags that turn any container into a connected one

A beautifully intuitive mobile app that keeps track of your inventory, freshness, and even suggests what to cook based on what you have

Whether you’re prepping a quick meal at home or running a high-volume kitchen, Aromi helps you reduce waste, stay organized, and cook smarter — every single day.

Why We Built This

Like many households and professional kitchens, we struggled with: Forgotten ingredients buried in the pantry, Expired or unused spices and produce.
Guesswork when it came to portioning and planning meals
 

We saw an opportunity to redesign not just the jar, but the entire system that supports it.

So we asked ourselves:

What if your storage could think? What if your jars and containers could help you eat smarter, waste less, and stay connected to your kitchen like never before?

So we built Aromi — to make managing your kitchen as easy as using your phone.

The physical Aromi Jar and Aromi Tag were designed, prototyped, and engineered entirely by our team using traditional CAD tools, electronics development, and manufacturing processes.

All internal firmware, PCB design, NFC integration, and load cell calibration are proprietary and manually developed.

The Nuvolux platform and mobile application architecture were designed and coded by our technical team, with Gemini integrated only at the AI service layer.

From Sketch to Smart System: How Aromi Evolved

Every great product starts with a simple question.
For Aromi, it was:
“What if a jar could think?”

What began as a series of hand-drawn concepts quickly evolved into a full-fledged smart kitchen system — through dozens of iterations, sketches, CAD models, and working prototypes.

We started with pencil and paper — sketching out what a “smart jar” could look like.

Our initial focus was on form:

Should it be square or cylindrical?

Where should the sensors go?

How can we keep it minimal yet informative?

These early drawings explored how users would interact with the jar, how feedback would be shown (LEDs or screen), and how the tag could seamlessly integrate into any kitchen.

3D CAD Models & Technical Exploration

Once we narrowed down the design direction, we moved into 3D modeling and CAD. This is where the functionality began to take shape:

Load cell integration

Modular sensor housing

Swappable tag attachment points

NFC coil placement within a compact base

Using Fusion 360 and SolidWorks, we built a mechanical structure that could support precision electronics while keeping the glass body clean and elegant.

Risks and challenges

Risks and Challenges: Every hardware startup comes with its own set of challenges — and we’re ready to face them with transparency, experience, and contingency plans in place. Manufacturing & Supply Chain: While we’ve already sourced and tested our core components (NFC chips, E-Ink modules, load sensors), scaling production may face unforeseen delays. Global supply chain issues, customs, or tooling challenges can occasionally slow down timelines. Our Solution: We’re working with reliable vendors and pre-locked mold suppliers. We’ve included buffers in our timeline and will keep backers updated with full transparency. Fulfillment & Shipping: International logistics are complex — customs clearance, warehouse processing, or global disruptions (like COVID-related slowdowns) may impact delivery dates in some regions. Our Solution: We’re working with reliable vendors and pre-locked mold suppliers. We’ve included buffers in our timeline and will keep backers updated with full transparency. Software Integration: While our hardware is almost ready for production, our app (iOS/Android) will continue to evolve post-campaign. There may be initial feature limitations or OTA updates needed to enhance functionality. Our Solution: Core functions (pairing, tracking, alerting) will be live at launch. Advanced features (meal planning, AI health dashboard) will roll out via app updates. Backers will get early beta access and feedback channels. Scaling Unexpected Demand: If the campaign exceeds expectations, we may need to scale production faster than planned — which could cause staggered shipping. Our Solution: We’ll prioritize early bird and first-tier backers, with a clear shipping wave system and regular updates. Our Promise: We’re committed to delivering what we promised — on time and with quality. You’ll be the first to know about any bumps along the road, and we’ll never leave you in the dark.

Environmental commitments

Long-lasting design

Aromi Jars and Tags are built to last — with replaceable components, firmware-upgradable tech, and solid-state displays/sensors that extend product life.

Reusability and recyclability

The containers can be washed, reused, and repurposed. Packaging is being designed with recyclable cardboard, and the NFC tags are built for long-term use.

Sustainable materials

Our goal is to use food-grade, BPA-free plastics and explore plant-based or biodegradable alternatives.
