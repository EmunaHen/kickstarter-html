Story

Allow me to introduce myself

My name is Cynthia. Like you, I’m on an ongoing journey of growth and expansion — one filled with countless deaths and rebirths. A major turning point came when my long-term relationship with my high school sweetheart ended. With this unexpected shift came a great upheaval that sent me into a deep depression, followed by an intense cocoon stage filled with radical healing.

During that time, I grew curious about me — who I was at my core and how I truly showed up in the world. I began to dig deep into my wounds and shadows. And as I continued to heal, I explored something for the first time in a long time. I explored my creativity.

For most of my life, I didn’t consider myself creative. I felt overwhelmed by the endless possibilities and paralyzed by perfectionism. So, I started small — with a coloring book. I challenged myself not to overthink, just to color. It was extremely difficult to execute at first, but eventually I broke through and something cracked open.

With that came a new sense of purpose: to explore my creativity in any and every way it wanted to express itself — freely, unapologetically, and without limits. I made jewelry. I took dance classes. I followed my curiosity wherever it led.

Eventually, I turned those passions into a multi-passion business: Goddess on the Loose, LLC. At its heart were two things — my need to express creatively, and my desire for financial freedom.

I had to unlearn the belief that business ownership wasn’t meant for someone like me. That it had to look a certain way. That being multi-passionate was a flaw instead of a gift. I didn’t have examples to follow — and in some cases, even faced misunderstandings and doubts from other business owners. But I kept going.

Through every challenge, I got stronger. With every step, I grew more confident.

That journey — of healing, unlearning, and creating without limits — became the soil for Tree. I wanted to build a space where everyday people could turn their real-life skills, passions, and lived experiences into their own kind of business. One that honors who they are at the root — and lets them grow in every direction they choose.

Because your business doesn’t have to look like anyone else’s.
And your path doesn’t need permission.

What really drives me is:

My desire to help alleviate the collective — to help guide us out of the rat race. To release people from having to sacrifice what matters most — family, friendships, freedom, and peace — in order to make ends meet.

I know that life intimately. I lived it for years — overworked and overwhelmed, emotionally, mentally, and physically exhausted. Unable to enjoy the present moment because fear and worry about financial obligations constantly consumed my mind.

Now, I want to show others what I discovered:
You simply are a business. And by reconnecting with your natural gifts — your everyday skills and innate passions — you can create a life of financial independence. 
A life that pulls you out of survival mode and into thrival mode — heh 😅

Risks and challenges

Technical Development Delays Risk: Building a smooth, feature-rich app takes time — especially with things like multistep forms, search filters, vibe profiles, payment systems, and messaging. To mitigate this, I have already planned out the flow and MVP concept, therefore providing clarity and allowing space to grow gradually. Community Safety & Moderation Tree is built on real people, real skills, and real connections. That also means we’ll need to ensure respectful behavior, safe interactions, and boundaries — especially around service offers and messaging. I have created a clear code of conduct and terms of use policy into Tree’s foundation and intend to incorporate reporting tools and moderation systems that will evolve as the community grows. User Trust & Retention Risk: If results aren’t immediate, users might lose interest. I am approaching this by designing a platform that emphasizes personalization and self-expression, which builds emotional connection, along with the call toward self-sustained living. Ongoing feedback, community features, and evolving content will keep users engaged.
