Meet the app

It syncs seamlessly with your shoes to turn raw data into personalized and actionable insight, giving you an edge to train smarter, minimize setbacks, and perform better. It delivers the following insights, which will evolve with you:

Impact Score

Your bones, muscles, and tendons must adapt to handle it - but too much can lead to setbacks and injuries. 

Co-developed with Dr. Brent Edwards (PhD, Biomechanics), Avelo’s Impact Score leverages the best available research on musculoskeletal loading to measure the biomechanical stress of each run, and guides how much you can handle each day and week. So you can build your capacity to handle more, and push your limits without setbacks.

Resiliency Score

The Resiliency Score helps you find the sweet spot between training and recovery. Co-developed with Dr. Phil Skiba (PhD, Exercise Physiology), it goes beyond heart rate-based recovery scores by factoring in how much impact your body’s taken. 

Calculated daily by including your Impact scores, the contributors are:

Clear, personalized guidance - go hard, hold steady, or recover - so you can make better training decisions, adapt and improve

Form Radar

Form isn’t static - it shifts with fatigue, pace, terrain, environment, and time. That’s why the Form Radar doesn’t just capture your form once - it tracks every stride on every run. It learns your unique running signature and flags deviations - potential signs of inefficiency or emerging issues.  

Whether it's overstriding, late-run breakdown, increasing asymmetry, or a shifting foot strike, you'll know - so you can be proactive about it.

Efficiency Score

Efficiency is your body’s “miles per gallon”, revealing how form, fatigue, and training influence your performance over time. 

Becoming more efficient means you'll go farther and faster - with less effort.

There are two main contributors to the Efficiency score:

Propulsion: As you run, your muscles generate force to propel your body forward and upward. Avelo calculates this based on your movement and the forces acting on your body.

Heart Rate. Your heart delivers oxygen and fuel to your muscles. The faster you run, the harder it beats. A lower heart rate at the same pace means you're using energy more efficiently.

Your Efficiency score reflects how well your body converts energy into motion. A higher score means you're running more efficiently, a key factor in performance and long-term progress.

Personalized coaching 

Avelo delivers personalized guidance based on your Impact, Resiliency, Efficiency scores, and Form Radar, tailored to your goals.  

You’ll receive daily and weekly mileage targets that keep your Impact in the right zone for you, workout recommendations, and real-time audio cues to keep your form sharp.  

Plus, strength and drill recommendations tailored to your movement patterns - like calf work for forefoot strikers or hip work for heel strikers. 

Over time we’ll deliver adaptive training plans that evolve with your progress, whether you're chasing your first 5K or a marathon PR.

Pushing running science forward, together

One of our goals is to give back to the global running community by conducting what we hope will be the largest biomechanics study in history.

By capturing data from the place it matters most - the point where your body meets the ground - we unlock opportunities for entirely new insights into running performance and injury prevention.

Why does this matter? Because despite decades of research, there is still much we don’t fully understand about how runners adapt, improve, and stay healthy.

Traditional running studies are often limited:

  • Small sample sizes
  • Lab-based settings (running on a treadmill for a few minutes)
  • Short snapshots that don’t reflect real-world training

While valuable, these approaches miss the full picture of what happens during day-to-day training, across thousands of miles and a wide diversity of athletes.

We imagine a different approach:

An ongoing, community-driven study capturing data from real runners training in the real world - every run, every surface, every pace.

We believe this holds immense promise for helping us better understand the determinants of performance and injuries, so we can help more runners do what they love for life.

Opt-In, always

Participation in this research is entirely optional.

  • You will not be included without your explicit consent.
  • If you do opt in, you can opt out at any time.
  • Your individual data will always be anonymized before being included in aggregate research.

Our goal is simple: to help runners everywhere train smarter, perform better, and reduce injury risk, while respecting your privacy every step of the way.

How does this compare to foot pods and smart insoles?

  • Hassle-free. With Avelo, you don't have to insert or attach anything to your shoes, or switch devices between shoes. Perhaps most importantly - Avelos never need to be charged, lasting the lifetime of your shoes (at least 300-400 miles).
  • Unique insights. While some of the core running metrics are similar (e.g. distance, pace, duration, stride length, cadence, ground contact time), Avelo includes some metrics not captured by most foot pods such as pronation, footstrike pattern, and ground reaction force. With a sensor in both shoes, Avelo provides a measure of symmetry. And most importantly, Avelo has worked with leading scientists and coaches to create unique insights about your training load and readiness (Impact and Resiliency Scores), efficiency (Efficiency Score), and form (Form Radar). Avelo translates these insights into personalized recommendations including weekly mileage and impact targets and plans, and real-time and post-run form coaching. Getting similar insight using other products is either impossible or requires your manual interpretation. We are obsessed with improving these insights over time. We are early in our journey and as an early adopter your feedback will help shape the product.
  • Cost. Add-on products cost $300+ for both feet, and you have to buy shoes separately. Avelos have best-in-class sensors built into each shoe for less than the price of add-ons.

Does Avelo work in cold and hot environments?

  • Yes, Avelos are built to survive at temperatures below 0 deg C / 32 deg F, up to 45 deg C / 113 deg F.

Is the sensor waterproof?

  • Yes, the sensors are fully waterproof, with the highest waterproof rating available (IPX7).

What data does it measure?

The core metrics measured by the sensors include:

  • Distance
  • Pace
  • Duration
  • Cadence
  • Stride Length
  • Ground Contact Time
  • Flight Time
  • Pronation Excursion
  • Footstrike Type (forefoot, midfoot, heel)
  • Ground Reaction Force
  • Vertical Oscillation
  • Asymmetry
  • We plan to add to these metrics over time, and have done the hard work to translate these metrics into intuitive, actionable insights including an Impact score (measure of the mechanical load of each run), daily Resiliency score (helping you understand if you’re training at a level that’s safe for you, and how hard/much to train each day and week), Efficiency score (measure of how efficiently you move, the “miles per gallon” of your body), and Form Radar (designed to detect deviations in your form, which may signal fatigue or emerging issues).

Does Avelo take into account other factors when measuring training load in its Resiliency score?

  • Yes, Avelo will integrate with leading smartwatches and use measures of physiological load (e.g. heart rate) combined with Avelo’s proprietary metrics of biomechanical load to provide a holistic understanding of your training load and recovery. We also plan to incorporate more inputs (e.g. sleep, nutrition). We are launching with a Resiliency Score focused on biomechanical load and will incorporate additional measures over time. 

What does this provide that my watch doesn’t?

  • Watches are great for the basics - pace, distance, and heart rate. But running starts at your feet, and watches are measuring from the wrist. We believe it’s critical to know what’s happening where your body is interacting with the ground. Watches can’t measure:
  • Impact: The force your body absorbs with every step.
  • Form: Metrics like symmetry, pronation, footstrike type, and contact time, key to improving efficiency and monitoring your running form over time.
  • Avelo isn’t here to replace your watch - it complements it. We integrate with your GPS and heart rate data to give you a complete picture of your training, from the ground up.

Do I need to run with a watch or phone?

  • No! Avelo will record your runs even if you don’t run with a phone or smartwatch. For real-time audio coaching, running with a phone is required. We plan to develop smartwatch apps so that you can also run with a smartwatch to receive real-time audio coaching.

Can I replace the batteries?

Keep in mind that the sensors are designed to last for the life of each shoe (at least 300-400 miles), and so replacing the battery will primarily be required if moving the sensors from your old pair to a new pair.

Does it store my runs?

  • Avelo can store up to 20 hours of running on the sensor before requiring you to sync.

How will Avelo succeed when other smart shoes haven’t?

  • We’ve watched the smart shoe space for years - and we think the problem hasn’t been just about the tech or the shoe. It’s about the whole approach.
  • First, we know runners won’t wear a shoe that isn’t truly great even if the tech is amazing. That’s why we’re obsessed with getting the fundamentals right first: fit, feel, ride, durability. Our team includes some of the most experienced footwear executives in the world and our goal is simple: build a shoe you’d want to run in even if it had no tech at all.
  • Second, we’re going beyond just spitting out data. Past smart shoes gave you numbers that might be novel, but not always useful. We’ve built Avelo to turn those numbers into actionable insights: how much mechanical load your body’s taking, how resilient you are, how efficient your stride is, and how much and hard to train to stay healthy and improve.
  • Finally, we’re structured differently. Most smart shoe attempts came from big footwear companies where this was a side project. If it got hard, no big deal, they moved on. For us, this is the company. We’re a group of runners building this because we believe it should exist - and have assembled a team with skills in technology and footwear because we know it takes deep integration across footwear, hardware, software, and AI to get this right.
  • The bottom line: we’re not adding tech to a shoe. We’re building a better running experience, and our vision is to create and lead the smart footwear category.
