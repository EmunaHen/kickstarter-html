Story

From humble beginnings as a Boy Scout whose motto was “Be Prepared”, I’ve always appreciated the utility of a good multi-tool pocket knife. 

But before we get started, let me ask the very strange question...

I’m excited to introduce our 21st-century take on the classic pocket knife — one that’s much more suited to the concrete-jungles and technology of today than the trusty pocket-knives of the past. 

It’s called the Smith Blade, and if you happen to have superhuman strength, well, you can cut through almost anything with it. But where are my manners! 

The World’s First Proto-Lightsaber, or building Giant Mechs, like the Power Loader from Aliens, or simply from my varied attempts at becoming Iron Man, catching bullets with a real life Bullet Proof Suit from John Wick – or maybe coating my own skeleton in Adamantium in order to become Wolverine in real life! 

When I’m not inspiring the next generation of kids into science, technology and out-of-the-box thinking; I’m probably in my lab working on a new prototype project, or prototyping products for www.Hacksmith.store.

You see, I’m a bit of a jack of all trades, a 21st century MacGyver – and if you hadn’t guessed, it’s actually the meaning behind the Hacksmith Logo; a multi-tool and an anvil!

I wanted to create the ultimate lightweight EDC. Regular pocket knife multi-tools didn’t cut it. Traditional multi-tool pliers were all too big and heavy. 

I needed something I could always carry, no matter what – NO EXCUSES.

It’s packed with twenty-one genuinely useful tools engineered to keep you prepared for anything life throws at you. 

And it’s light. At only 95 grams (3.3oz), you won’t even notice you’re carrying it.

Whether you're a hands-on maker, or perhaps a mom or dad who is expected to know how to fix anything and everything, the Smith Blade is built to be there when it matters most. From household repairs to backwoods camping… from writing that note when nobody else has a pen, to playing family IT guy or “certified” DIY electrician swapping a light switch, it’s got you covered. 

And when the job’s done? 

Crack a cold one with the boys using the built in bottle opener. The Smith Blade has got your back.

The Features

1. A M390 BLADE that can CUT THROUGH OTHER BLADES

M390 is simply put, one of the best blade steels money can buy. Produced in Germany through a process called powder metallurgy that seems almost like science fiction, there’s a reason why it can literally cut other knives in half.

2. 15° Adjustable Protractor 

The protractor. The unsung hero of the design of the Smith Blade, that gives it its unique signature opening-sound and increases the fidget factor ten-fold.


I came up with this by accident, after I noticed the ball-detent was scribing a perfect radius on the edge of the blade. What if we just put more divots on the blade, at 15° increments? 

3. Nail File

Good files need to be hard. Our blade has insane hardness, clocking in at a 61-63 hardness Rockwell C Scale. Seriously, why hasn’t anyone done this before? 

4. Bit Retention Storage Spring

The patent-pending and most unique feature of the Smith Blade. Integrated 4mm bit storage in the front-scale of the knife!

Safe, secure, and drop-proof; it’s seriously the best.  And of course, it’s compatible with any 4mm precision screwdriver bits, especially our friend's 4mm bits over at Linus Tech Tips.

5. Tweezers

Honestly, I don’t know why tweezers aren’t in every single multi-tool out there. It’s a serious pain point, because when you need them, you need them! 

6. Wire-strippers

Wire strippers that actually work. We’ve added grooves to help guide your wire, from 12-20ga, and I’m constantly impressed at how well they work.

7. Metric & Imperial Ruler

As Canadians, we really do love the metric system… but our proximity to our American brothers and sisters means we use both. And thanks to global trade, you’ll probably run into things that are designed both in Metric and Imperial, so don’t worry, we’ve got you covered for both!

8. Reversible Belt Clip & Deep carry variation.

A pocket knife is personal. With a reversible belt clip and options for deep carry, there’s a combination for you that offers ultimate pocket-ability.

Bonus points: If you wear the best pants in the world (the Smith Pants™ coming soon…) the Smith Blade fits perfectly into the integrated tool loop and is quick-drawable for the speediest of repairs!

9. Bottle Opener

Sure, you can try and use your teeth, or the edge of a table, but a good bottle opener? It’s today’s equivalent of carrying a lighter, you never know when someone needs one!

10. Pry Bar/Staple Puller

Pop open old used paint cans with ease, or remove those pesky staples.

11. Philips 4mm Bit

As part of the standard 4mm bit load out, the trusty Philips (or crosshead) style screwdriver. 

12. Flathead 4mm Bit

While flat-heads are truly awful screws, you’re bound to encounter them more often than not. 

13. Pen 4mm Bit

But wait, what if we just had a 4mm bit that is also a pen? 

14. SIM Tool/Sewing Awl

Sometimes you need a pokey-thing. From SIM trays to emergency sewing sessions, our 2-in-1 sewing awl has got you covered.

15. Glow in the Dark Illuminator / Radioactive Tritium.

Drop your knife? The glow-in-the-dark illuminator stays lit for up to a few hours, helping you locate your blade with ease.

And the Founder’s edition? Yup, we put a vial of radioactive Tritium in it. It’ll glow for 15-20 years in complete darkness! 

Don’t worry though, the weak beta radiation can’t escape the glass vial – the glow actually comes from phosphorus inside the vial that’s being excited by the radiation!

16. Ferrocerium Fire Striker

4, 8, 15, 16, 23, 42… Well, if you find yourself stranded on an island, the Smith Blade even comes with a ferrocerium fire striker!

17. Tungsten Carbide Metal Scribe

Tungsten: one of the most dense elements in the universe, is also super hard – letting you scratch hardened steel, to even glass!

18. Emergency Glass Breaker

In a pinch, you can escape a locked car by using the tungsten-carbide to shatter tempered glass!

19. Bubble Level

I don't know about you, but making sure something is perfectly level scratches a deep OCD itch, and with one in your pocket at all times...?

20. 90-degree Bit Driver

To get into tight spots, we’ve included a 4mm bit driver on the side. Convenient!

21. Extended Ruler on Edge of Blade

Measure up to 192mm (~7.5") from end to end!

Knife Laws & Legality

At Hacksmith Tools, we take compliance seriously. We actively ensure the Smith Blade meets legal requirements in every country we ship to. For regions with stricter knife laws, such as the UK, Germany, France, and Australia, we offer a specially designed variant of the Smith Blade featuring a non-locking mechanism, a <2.99” blade, and a two-hand opening design.

This variant maintains all the same core tools and functionality while aligning with local regulations to help ensure smooth delivery through customs. And if you move somewhere else? You can buy a regular locking blade from us to upgrade your knife back to(?) normal!

If you reside in one of these countries, we strongly recommend selecting the non-locking version add-on during checkout to avoid delays, package seizures, or other issues with importation. 

We’ve done the legal homework, so you can carry your EDC with confidence.

Manufacturing in-house & Timeline

We've launched the Smith Blade on Kickstarter to raise funds in order to re-invest into H.E.R.C.'s manufacturing capabilities to allow us to better serve our audience, and customers. By manufacturing in-house we will be able to respond faster to changes, quality control, and accelerate future product development; and even offer more customization than ever before.

The main use of the money will be on a CNC production line for machining the raw titanium into Smith Blades. The average cost of a single CNC machine (16-tool, 20k rpm spindle, auto-probing) is around $50-60K USD + tooling and fixturing. 

While we're continuing to revise and improve our machine paths based on efficiency, tool-wear, and cycle times, it currently takes approximately 2.5 hours to machine the two halves of a single Smith Blade due to the complexity in the design and bit-retention. To maximize part production, we are planning on setting up lights-out-automation to ensure these machines are running 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Assuming no part failures, that means one CNC machine is capable of producing ~9.6 Smith Blades per day, running 24/7. That's approximately 300 Smith Blades per month, per machine. 

Our initial goal on Kickstarter is to raise $250,000 in order to setup a production line of five CNC machines, which would then be able to produce 1500 Smith Blades a month, which would be enough for us to produce and ship all early-bird rewards in September-October. 

This is a huge investment on our part, as it means we need to continue selling Smith Blades into the future in order to actually make any kind of financial return, and support the overhead and employees of H.E.R.C. 

But that means you can rest assured; the Smith Blade is not going anywhere. We will continue to support, produce, develop, and improve our products for Hacksmith.store -- that's our commitment to you.

If the campaign does exceedingly well, we will purchase even more CNC equipment in order to meet demand and get Smith Blades out to you guys as soon as possible. The better the campaign does, the sooner you get your rewards! And as a YouTube channel turned product business; we will be sharing more video updates than the rest of Kickstarter campaigns combined!

We can't wait to take you on this manufacturing journey with us!

Shipping, Duties, Taxes, VAT & Tariffs

Duties, Taxes, and Tariffs. No one likes them, but they're a part of international commerce and there's no way around them. So let's break them down!

Shipping estimates are provided (but not charged) with your pledge at check out. Once the campaign ends and you complete your backer survey, the final shipping rate will then be charged - you will also have an opportunity at this time to add any extras to your order! We do this to ensure the lowest rate possible for you.

Taxes & VAT are automatically charged based on your location through the Kickstarter platform. These will be charged at campaign end. Sorry, there's no way around this!

Duties and tariffs vary based on your location, and are typically charged by the shipping company at customs clearance into your country - we do not have any control over this. 

US TARIFFS:
These have been in the news a lot. They keep changing, and there is a lot of confusion around them. A tariff is charged to the consumer, you, by your own country at customs import -- just like a duty fee or tax.

Because the US keeps changing its tariff rates, we cannot give concrete information at this time, however, we will do our best to minimize the impact to you. We can do this by importing Smith Blades into American distribution networks, and pay for the tariff at the manufacturing cost instead of the retail price. This substantially lowers the tariff and removes additional processing fees normally paid by the consumer. Unfortunately, this does mean shipping to USA based customers may take longer.

Smith Blade Standard, Pro, and Founders edition should qualify for USMCA (United States Mexico Canada Agreement) and have zero tariffs or very minimal.
Smith Blade Lites shouldn’t be any more than $10 in tariffs to US customers.

Hacksmith Tools come with a 2-year limited warranty, covering any defects in materials or workmanship under normal use. For warranty claims, please contact our support team with your order number and a description of the issue. We’re here to help and stand behind the quality of Smith Blades we make.

Reusability and recyclability

Sustainability isn't just about the final product—it starts with how it's made. At Hacksmith Industries, we’ve designed the Smith Blade’s manufacturing process to be as efficient and responsible as possible. All excess titanium, steel, and other material offcuts generated during production are collected and recycled or reused in-house or through certified metal recycling partners. This closed-loop approach minimizes environmental impact and ensures valuable raw materials don’t go to waste. In addition, because the Smith Blade is modular and built to last a lifetime, individual components can be repaired, upgraded, or replaced without throwing out the entire tool—reducing long-term waste and supporting a more sustainable EDC philosophy. We're not just making a tool. We're building a product that respects the materials it's made from—and the planet it’s made on.

Sustainable materials

The Smith Blade is built to last a lifetime—and that’s no accident. We’ve chosen premium materials not just for their performance, but for their longevity and sustainability. At the core is Grade 5 Titanium (Ti-6Al-4V), a high-performance alloy known for its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio, corrosion resistance, and biocompatibility. It’s the same titanium used in aerospace, medical, and high-end watchmaking industries—making it ideal for an everyday carry tool that’s meant to withstand real-world abuse without ever feeling bulky in your pocket. For the blade, we selected M390 stainless steel, one of the most advanced blade steels in the world. Produced using powder metallurgy, M390 offers outstanding edge retention, wear resistance, and virtually unmatched corrosion resistance—even in harsh environments. It’s also a more sustainable option in the long run, as it holds an edge longer and requires infrequent sharpening cycles. By using durable, long-lasting materials, we’re minimizing waste, reducing planned obsolescence, and creating a tool that won’t just survive the years—it’ll thrive through them.

Environmentally friendly factories

All Smith Blades are proudly manufactured in-house at the Hacksmith Engineering Research Campus (H.E.R.C.) in Cambridge, Ontario, Canada. By keeping production under our roof, we maintain full control over quality, precision, and craftsmanship—ensuring every tool meets the highest standards. The Smith Blade Lite is currently manufactured with trusted partners in China to allow for broader accessibility and affordability. However, once production is fully optimized, our goal is to transition that model to local manufacturing as well. Want to know if your Smith Blade was made in Canada? Look for the engraved maple leaf by the tweezers—a small mark of Canadian pride that represents local craftsmanship, accountability, and innovation.

Sustainable Distribution

To reduce unnecessary emissions and streamline delivery, all Smith Blade orders are shipped from our headquarters in Canada or through our U.S. distribution partners, depending on your location. By keeping fulfillment regionalized, we minimize long-haul international shipping and reduce our carbon footprint. This also helps us get your order to you faster and more efficiently—without relying on wasteful third-party logistics chains. It’s a smarter, more sustainable way to ship—because how a product gets to you matters just as much as how it’s made.
