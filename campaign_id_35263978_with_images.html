Rate My Landlord


“Because Renters Deserve the Truth!”

Created by: Evan Guy

There’s no simple way to check if a landlord is responsive, honest, or even safe to deal with. You’re expected to sign a lease, often worth tens of thousands of dollars, based on little more than a gut feeling.

Rate My Landlord changes that.

It’s a web and mobile app where renters can rate, review, and warn others; just like they would for a restaurant, Uber driver, or Airbnb host. It’s anonymous, secure, and long overdue.


TL;DR / Summary

I’m building an app called Rate My Landlord, a mobile and web platform where renters can rate and review their landlords. It’s inspired by how we already rate Uber drivers, professors, and restaurants—but right now, there’s no reliable way to check if a landlord is honest, responsive, or even safe to rent from.

This app will change that.

Renters will be able to leave star ratings, write reviews, upload photos (like repairs that weren’t fixed), and link to Zillow listings to give context. Users can search landlords by name or area code, and everything can be done anonymously.

The project is fully in progress. I’m building it myself using Flutter (so it works on iOS, Android, and web) and Firebase (for secure login, database, and image storage). I’ve already written key parts of the code, including review submission, area code filtering, and photo upload features. The design is modern, clean, and intentionally simple, made to be accessible for renters of all ages.

This is a lean, focused, high-impact app that fills a very real gap. Renters are already talking about their landlord experiences. It's time they had a proper place to do it. If you’ve ever had a bad landlord, you know how much a tool like this could help. What's It For?

  • Search for landlords by name or area code
  • Leave 1-5 star reviews for landlords or tenants alike
  • Upload photos of the unit or issues
  • Link to the property’s Zillow listing
  • Read other tenants’ experiences
  • Use the app anonymously or join the community with a free profile
  • Choose from clean, modern UI themes

It will work across Android, iOS, and web, all from one codebase.

Why does this Matter?

There’s no real way to research a landlord before you move in.

People rely on word of mouth, Reddit threads, or just luck. And when things go wrong, there’s no way to warn the next person.

We rate restaurants, drivers, and even professors. But not landlords? That doesn’t make sense.

This is a tool that renters clearly need, want and frankly surprisingly does not exist yet!

Why This Matters

Because where you live affects everything: your safety, your mental health, your finances, your sense of control over your life. And yet, renters are expected to make one of the biggest decisions of their year, sometimes their lives based on zero information about the person who owns the property.

You don’t know if your landlord is going to fix things. You don’t know if they’ll respect your privacy. You don’t know if you’re walking into a nightmare. All you see is clean photos, staged furniture, and maybe a smiling leasing agent.

That’s the reality for millions of people. And when something goes wrong and it often does there’s no real way to hold anyone accountable or warn the next renter.

This app matters because it gives people a voice where they’ve never had one. It gives renters a way to protect each other.

It puts pressure on landlords to do better. It takes what’s always been a private struggle and makes it visible.

Rate My Landlord is about trust. It’s about the truth. It’s about giving renters a tool they’ve needed forever and building a little bit of balance into a system that’s been one-sided for far too long.

If you’ve ever rented a place and felt powerless, disrespected, or trapped, then you already know why this matters. This is how we start fixing that.

Since I’m building this solo while working full-time, I’ve created a realistic, focused timeline that lets me move steadily without sacrificing quality. I’m committing 6–12 hours a week, and I’m aiming for a public launch in early 2026 with a fully polished app for both web and mobile.

Here’s the breakdown:

  • Planning & Design (Weeks 1–3)

I’ll lock in the final feature set, finalize UI designs, secure the domain and name, and set up branding and social handles.

  • Development Setup (Weeks 4–7)

This phase is about getting the project structured, integrating Firebase, and wiring up basic user authentication and navigation across platforms.

  • Core Feature Development (Weeks 8–12)

I’ll build out the main functionality: landlord search, area code filtering, review submission, ratings, and display—all tied securely into Firebase.

  • Media + Theming (Weeks 13–16) 

This is where the app gets polished. I’ll integrate photo uploads, add visual themes (dark mode, etc.), and clean up the UI for a consistent cross-platform experience.

  • Testing & Beta Feedback (Weeks 17–19)

I’ll do thorough internal testing, then invite a small beta group for real-world feedback. I’ll use that to patch bugs and tighten up the UX.

  • Launch Prep (Weeks 20–26)

This includes app store submissions, legal documents (TOS, privacy policy), and preparing the website and social channels for launch. I’ll also do light outreach and early marketing.

I’ve paced this to be sustainable but focused, so I can deliver a well-built, trustworthy product without rushing. The app is scoped realistically and designed to go live in Q1 2026 with full web and mobile support.

Use of AI

Our app leverages AI at every step, from development to user experience. During development, we use AI coding assistants like GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT to help write code faster, catch bugs early, and streamline testing. This means we can build Rate My Landlord more efficiently and with higher quality. In the app itself, AI-powered natural language processing analyzes every review, detecting the sentiment behind each comment and automatically filtering out spam or fake reviews. This intelligent system even delivers personalized search results, so each renter gets the most relevant and trustworthy landlord information tailored to their needs.
