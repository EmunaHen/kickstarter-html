Taking the right medication at the right time is vital—but it’s not always easy. Missed doses can lead to complications, hospital visits, and loss of independence, especially for seniors and those managing chronic conditions. Medication non-adherence is a growing concern for clinicians, healthcare systems, and families alike. Whether managing long-term illness or recovering from a temporary condition, medication adherence is key to better health.

We built the EMTHINGSSmart Medication Organizer to simplify medication management - eliminating the hassle of traditional pill boxes and confusion with a seamless and effortless technology that works for you, not against you.

Anyone who values independence deserves a simple, reliable way to stay on top of their medications. Whether you're managing a chronic condition or supporting a loved one, missing a dose can have poor consequences.

The development of our Smart Medication Organizer began with a clear mission: to simplify medication management and improve health outcomes. From the outset, we focused on creating a design that was simple, seamless, and non-intimidating—especially for users who might be overwhelmed by complex technology. Most existing smart solutions were bulky, expensive, and still required users to manually sort and organize their medications. We set out to eliminate that friction entirely by designing a system that accommodates medications in their original bottles, while also incorporating a built-in mechanism to keep family and caregivers informed offering added encouragement and support.

Our journey began with a very rough concept sketch on a piece of paper in late 2023. 

After several months of development and multiple iterations of electronics logic, PCBs design, software development, and enclosure modeling, we built our first fully functional prototype housed in a 3D-printed enclosure during August 2024.

After testing and refinement for several months, we finalized the design, software, and completed tooling for injection-molded enclosures in January 2025.

Parallel to the device development, we developed a companion app for both Android and iOS using the React Native platform, with a strong focus on accessibility and ease of use—particularly for elderly users. Since March 2025, we’ve been testing beta units with real users, and the feedback we've gathered has allowed us to further fine-tune both the hardware and software in preparation for mass production by September 2025.
