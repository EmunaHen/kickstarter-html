Clothes are three-dimensional. Seams, sleeves, collars, and cuffs all come in different shapes. Traditional irons struggle to treat these surfaces evenly.

Einsen’s patented 3D ironing system solves this by using separate plates at multiple levels inside the machine, to turn the 3D structure of your garment into a 2D outcome. This architecture transforms the natural 3D shape of garments into a treatable format – allowing Einsen to treat all surfaces simultaneously.

Here’s what happens:

When you insert your garment into the auto-feeding mechanism, the sleeves are redirected to the lower channel.

Einsen then flattens and presses them separately, with controlled pressure while applying superheated steam.

The result: all surfaces are treated at once, whilst ensuring garments are free from bunching. A marvel of engineering, exclusive to Einsen.

Einsen produces steam that reaches up to 200°C (392°F). But unlike ordinary steamers, it’s dry, superheated steam. This enables multi layer penetration, releasing even the most stubborn wrinkles without leaving garments damp.

This results in no additional drying time, humidity stains and improved fabric preservation.

Einsen’s spring-mounted pressing system adapts pressure in real time. This helps Einsen maintain just the right amount of pressure – never too little, never too much – for a professional-level press across various parts of the fabric.

Einsen’s auto-feeding system uses internal rollers to guide garments through the machine with calibrated control. Once inserted, the hanger is automatically driven forward, sleeves are rerouted to a lower track, and the fabric is kept wrinkle-free throughout. Einsen manages the entire journey automatically.

Following my experience in Japan I searched for an iron that was automated - frustrated with the research and lack of options in the market, I started Einsen. Since its inception, our early prototypes could successfully move garments between two points, or remove wrinkles, but not both. Through these failures we were confronted with a realization for why automation in ironing does not exist. By understanding the barriers to automation, we began developing the core technology to enable automation. After 100’s of iterations and 1000’s of models we developed and filed a patent for our 3D technology. Through engineering optimization and the continued evolution of our technology, we’ve refined the product. Prototype III reflects 4 years of development, streamlined into one machine.

                                   From early testings of the technology of Einsen 

Einsen’s internal architecture is built on a proprietary multilevel system that translates complex three-dimensional garments into treatable surfaces. Using a combination of top-mounted and lower plates, the system simultaneously presses these complex areas such as sleeves and cuffs in a single pass. Integrated roller systems drive the garment forward while the sleeves are redirected onto a separate lower track. This ensures each surface receives dedicated treatment without overlap or fabric distortion. Dynamic, spring-mounted pressure adapts to fabric thickness and garment irregularities such as buttons, while superheated dry steam penetrates deep into fibers for precise, dry results. This system is protected under patent application WO2021243413A1.
