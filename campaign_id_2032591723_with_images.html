Story

Some circuits just don't cooperate! Whether its datasheets with key details hidden in a haystack, 100s of reference pages but no reference code, every conceivable detail available except for ECAD symbols, footprints or 3D models.

Let's face it, hardware development is hard. The industry has come far in simplifying development, but not far enough. Luckily, Quickboards will change things, and you get to be a part of it!

We’re launching the Quickboards Schematic Library—a curated collection of proven, modular circuit building blocks you can copy-paste directly into your own ECAD software.

Skip component search, Skip symbol/footprint/model creation, Skip interpreting datasheet circuit diagrams - browse our library for pre-built schematics, download and paste into your project.

A complete 12-cell lithium-ion Battery Management System (BMS) can be assembled by combining a few modular Quickboards blocks.

Start by adding a Power Management Block built around the LTC6811 IC, responsible for cell voltage monitoring and passive balancing. Pair this with a Processor Block featuring a PIC24F microcontroller, which communicates with the LTC6811 via SPI. The microcontroller reads voltage data from each cell and sends commands to initiate cell balancing as needed.

Next, add a Power Management Block equipped with eight N-Channel MOSFETs. Two of these are used to drive mechanical relays—one for controlling the battery relay and the other for the charge relay.

for current and temperature measurement, integrate current sensor blocks to monitor both battery discharge and charging currents, and include a temperature sensing block with eight analog temperature sensors to track cell temperatures accurately. Then add an ADC block to collect the various sensor voltages.

To complete the system, include a Power Management Block containing several buck converters to power all the sub circuits.

A fully programmable low-pass filter system can be created by combining several Quickboards building blocks designed for precision analog and digital signal processing.

Start with an Analog Input Block that includes four differential input buffers. These buffers condition the incoming analog signals and pass them to the next stage with high signal integrity.

Next, connect an ADC Block to sample the differential inputs. This block digitizes the analog signals and sends the data to a Processor Block featuring the dsPIC33 DSP via SPI. The dsPIC33 performs digital filtering on the sampled data in real time by applying a FFT, subtracting or attenuating the particular frequencies of choice, then applying an inverse FFT to reconstruct the time-domain signal.

Once processed, the filtered signals are transmitted through SPI to two DAC Blocks, which convert the digital data back into analog signals.

Finally, the analog output from the DACs is routed into an Analog Output Block containing eight precision active low-pass filter circuits. These filters provide final analog smoothing and bandwidth control, making the system suitable for applications requiring clean, programmable analog output.

Risks and challenges

Our main challenge is expanding the library while maintaining quality and reliability of designs. With your support, we’ll be able to expand our team of industry experts to ensure success. Our secondary challenge is navigating component stock issues, which can impact module availability and timelines. To address this, we’ve prioritized components based on availability and production status, ensuring resilience and minimizing the risk of delays. In the event of out-of-stock components, we will communicate promptly with backers regarding potential delays, including any required redesigns

Long-lasting design

One of the key factors in ensuring the longevity of Quickboards is the careful selection of integrated circuits (ICs) that have been in production for years and are widely used in the electronics industry. This approach guarantees long term demand for the particular design, proven reliability and industry support.

Reusability and recyclability

Quickboards are modular building blocks designed for reuse across multiple prototype designs, reducing electronic waste.
