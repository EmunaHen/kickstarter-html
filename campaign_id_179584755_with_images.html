Story

My daughter Nevaeh was diagnosed with severe autism when she was little. 

What followed were years of meetings where I watched her confidence crumble. Meltdowns after school. Nights where I posted through tears, trying to make sense of it all—fighting for my daughter to be seen, protected, and believed.

What is an IEP?

An IEP (Individualized Education Plan) is a federally mandated process that is supposed to guarantee a child with a learning difference or disability gets the support they need in school. Instead, it's often 30 pages of confusing notes, overwhelming meetings, and parents left to figure it out alone.

Most families navigate this in complete isolation—thinking they're the only ones struggling, the only ones confused, the only ones fighting for their kid.

One parent put it perfectly: "IEP meeting tomorrow? Rum tonight."

That's not a joke. That's survival.

And Right Now, It's Getting Worse

The federal office that investigates civil rights violations for students with learning differences or disabilities just lost two-thirds of its staff. Fewer investigations. Slower responses. More silence.

You all heard RFK Jr. float the idea of an autism registry—then try to backpedal. Every crisis becomes an excuse to profile our kids, push them out, blame them first.

But where there is friction, there is also: opportunity.

I Built Expert IEP

That calling led me to build Expert IEP—a mobile app that turns your phone into an IEP advocate.

Our AI model checks the IEP for missing services, vague goals, and legal red flags — then helps families co-create a version that’s clearer, compliant, and more likely to be used by schools.

Think of it as having an IEP expert in your pocket.

Here's How It Works in Practice:

📚 Meet Adam. Adam is a middle school student with autism. His original IEP goal? "Adam will be a better reader by the end of the year."

Through our platform, that goal became: "Adam will read grade-level passages aloud with 85% accuracy and appropriate pacing by May 2025."

Then his family shared something his school didn't know: Adam wants to be a public speaker.

So we co-created this version: "Adam will read grade-level passages aloud with 85% accuracy, appropriate pacing, and clear verbal delivery for classroom presentations by May 2025."

The result? Instead of pulling Adam from class for speech, his SLP now joins his Gen Ed class for 20 minutes a week—giving him strategies in real time. Same services. Same resources. More impact, less disruption.

Risks and challenges

We’re building something bold: a platform that gives families the tools, language, and clarity to navigate special education with confidence. That kind of shift—especially in a system as complex as special education—comes with real considerations. Here’s what we’ve thought through: 1. Families Need Time to Build Trust We know that many families have had difficult experiences in school systems. That makes trust something we earn over time, not assume. We’ve designed our onboarding to be human-centered and culturally responsive, and we’re working with trusted partners to ensure families feel seen and supported from day one. 2. Special Education Is Complex and Varies by Region The IEP process is shaped by federal law (IDEA), but implementation can differ by state or district. Our platform is designed to guide families through their documents—not to give legal advice. We’ve worked closely with special education experts, advocates, and legal consultants to keep our content accurate, respectful, and within scope. 3. Responsible Growth Takes Time We’ve supported over 1,200 families already, and this campaign will help us reach 1,000 more. To ensure every family gets the attention they deserve, we’re scaling in phases, expanding our team carefully, and prioritizing quality of care over speed. 4. Hoodie Fulfillment May Take Longer Some backer tiers include limited-edition hoodies. We’re proud of the design and want them to be perfect. However, depending on final quantities and sourcing, delivery may take longer than digital rewards. We’ll keep you updated with honest timelines and communicate any delays clearly. 5. Privacy and Security Are Non-Negotiable Families trust us with sensitive information. All data on our platform is encrypted, never sold, and always under user control. We follow FERPA-aligned best practices and conduct regular security reviews. We’ve designed Expert IEP with data dignity in mind from day one. 6. This Is Our First Kickstarter We’re not juggling other crowdfunding campaigns—this is our only one. That means all our attention is focused on delivering what we’ve promised. And we take that seriously. We’ve done the work. We’ve built the infrastructure. We’ve mapped the path. Now we’re inviting you to build it with us—knowing we’ve prepared for the risks, so families can finally move forward with power.
