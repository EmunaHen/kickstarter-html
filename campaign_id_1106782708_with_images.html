Risks and challenges

Although we firmly believe in Invited’s success, we acknowledge these potential challenges and how we will address them: Development delays: The automatic invitation module and provider dashboard require tight frontend-backend integration; we will mitigate this risk with weekly sprints, continuous testing, and incremental feature releases. Template quality control: Designing 20 premium templates may introduce visual inconsistencies; we will conduct internal design reviews and usability tests with real users before launch. B2B provider adoption: The learning curve for florists or planners could be a barrier; we will offer step-by-step tutorials, live webinars, and personalized support during the initial rollout. Infrastructure scalability: As the number of couples and events grows, hosting requirements will increase; we have configured AWS Vapor and Vercel to scale automatically and set aside a budget buffer to add resources if needed. Third-party dependency (transactional email): If our mailing provider experiences downtime, RSVP confirmations could be affected; we will configure a fallback service (Mailgun/SendGrid) and monitor deliverability 24/7.

Not applicable, as we do not use AI to generate images, audio, or video—only for writing texts and suggesting code corrections. All AI outputs come from publicly available models (OpenAI) based on our own prompts and do not incorporate third-party content without license; additionally, our human team reviews and refines each result before publication.

Environmental commitments

Visit our Environmental Resources Center to learn how Kickstarter encourages sustainable practices.

Long-lasting design

Invited’s digital invitations do not expire or deteriorate over time. Once created, they remain accessible indefinitely in the cloud and can be reused for future editions or related events, maximizing their useful life.

Sustainable Distribution

Invitations are sent digitally, completely eliminating the transport, packaging, and emissions associated with postal mail or courier. This is, de facto, the most sustainable delivery method.

Something else

“Zero paper, zero printing emissions” As reinforcement to the points above, we emphasize that Invited eliminates the need to print or transport paper invitations, thereby avoiding waste and emissions.
