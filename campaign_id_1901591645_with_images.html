Story

We’re raising $50,000 to launch the MVP (Minimum Viable Product) of Discreet Guardian, a mobile app designed to enhance personal safety through discreet and customizable features. Our goal is to achieve 10,000 app downloads and $4,000 in Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) within six months of launch. The funds will be used to develop and test a seamless user experience with intuitive features like silent SOS alerts, location tracking, discreet audio recording, and fake calls to help users exit uncomfortable situations. We’ll also invest in marketing and user acquisition, leveraging high-engagement platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and safety-focused blogs to reach our target audience. Additionally, we plan to launch a pilot program to engage early adopters, gather real-world feedback, and refine the app. Finally, we’ll allocate resources to ensure operational security, maintain data privacy, and comply with industry standards to build trust with our users.

We care deeply about this mission because current safety apps often lack discretion and personalization, leaving many users without a solution that respects their independence while enhancing their safety. Discreet Guardian fills this gap by offering a discreet, culturally aware, and highly customizable safety solution that empowers users without drawing unwanted attention.

I’m Ugonna Emuka, a design engineer and the founder of Discreet Guardian. While I don’t have a background in product development, I’ve assembled a talented app development team to bring this vision to life. I’m driven by a deep commitment to creating innovative solutions that address real-world safety concerns. My engineering background equips me with the problem-solving mindset needed to identify gaps in safety technology and develop a solution that truly meets users’ needs. With your support, we can empower individuals to take control of their safety, one discreet action at a time.

Risks and challenges

Launching Discreet Guardian comes with several potential challenges, but we have clear strategies to overcome them. User adoption and market penetration may be difficult in a crowded safety app market, but we plan to differentiate our app through targeted marketing campaigns on platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and safety-focused blogs while leveraging influencer partnerships to build trust. Ensuring app security and data privacy is a top priority, and we will implement end-to-end encryption, conduct regular security audits, and comply with privacy regulations such as GDPR and CCPA. Technical development delays could impact our launch timeline, but our phased development plan, agile methodology, and built-in timeline buffers will keep us on track. User retention and engagement may be challenging, but we will enhance retention through personalized safety settings, push notifications, and a freemium model that encourages continued use. Regulatory and compliance risks will be managed by collaborating with legal experts to ensure ongoing compliance with data privacy and safety laws. As our user base grows, scalability and infrastructure management will be addressed through a cloud-based infrastructure with regular stress testing to maintain optimal performance. Finally, to ensure financial sustainability, we will rely on a freemium model with premium in-app features that drive recurring revenue, securing long-term growth and success. Through these strategies, we are confident that Discreet Guardian can effectively meet its goals and empower users to take control of their safety.

Something else

Discreet Guardian is committed to minimizing its environmental impact, even as we plan to incorporate physical safety devices and printed materials in the future. When we do expand into physical products, we will prioritize eco-friendly materials and sustainable manufacturing processes to ensure that our devices are durable, energy-efficient, and recyclable. Printed materials will be produced using recycled paper and environmentally friendly inks to reduce waste and lower our carbon footprint. For our digital operations, we will continue to use cloud-based infrastructure hosted on platforms that prioritize energy efficiency and carbon neutrality. Additionally, we plan to offer repair and recycling programs for our devices to extend their lifespan and minimize electronic waste.
