Story

Cybersecurity skills are in higher demand than ever – there are over 3.5 million unfilled cybersecurity jobs worldwide – yet learning cybersecurity and ethical hacking can be intimidating for newcomers. Traditional courses are often dry, overwhelming, or divorced from real-world applications. Many aspiring cybersecurity professionals give up after wading through dense textbooks and isolated tutorials, only to feel lost when facing real-world problems. Backdoors.ai is here to change that.

Backdoors.ai is a gamified, AI-assisted cybersecurity learning platform that teaches you by doing. Instead of passive lectures, you’ll solve hands-on hacking challenges that simulate real security scenarios. And you’re never alone – a built-in AI mentor provides guidance and explanations at every step. Learning is faster, smarter, and way more engaging with this approach. Whether you have zero experience or already know the basics, Backdoors.ai meets you at your level and helps you grow from novice to pro in a supportive, interactive environment.

Our founder, Connor Fitzgerald, knows the pain points of learning cybersecurity firsthand. He spent countless hours with dry textbooks and disconnected tutorials, only to end up overwhelmed by theory and missing practical skills that employers value. Connor envisioned Backdoors.ai as the solution he wished existed – a platform that combines real-world hacking scenarios with just-in-time AI guidance. By learning through actual challenges (not just quizzes or videos) and getting help exactly when you need it, you can be challenged but never stuck. Backdoors.ai’s mission is to ensure that the best learning happens when you’re “challenged, supported, and never left behind”.

Right now, Backdoors.ai is in active development, with a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) about 2 months from completion. We’ve built the core platform and designed many of the initial challenges. This Kickstarter will allow us to polish the user experience, cover final infrastructure costs, and grow an early community of users who will shape the platform with their feedback. By backing this project, you’re not just funding software – you’re joining a mission to make cybersecurity education practical, fun, and accessible to all. We’ve set a small funding goal and a 30-day campaign to welcome our first cohort of “alpha” users. With your help, we can finish strong and launch a platform that empowers the next generation of ethical hackers and cyber security professionals.

Risks and challenges

Every project has risks, and we want to be upfront about the challenges we face and how we’ll address them: Technical Development & Delays: Backdoors.ai involves complex technology – an interactive web platform, real-time challenge environments, and an AI integration. There’s always a risk that development hurdles (bugs, integration issues, etc.) could slow us down. The good news is we’ve already built most of the core platform, and our team’s expertise in software development reduces this risk. We’ve planned buffer time in the timeline for testing and fixes. If we do hit an unexpected snag that delays the beta or launch, we will communicate transparently with backers and work diligently to resolve it. Thus far, progress has been steady, and we’re confident in our 2-month MVP timeline. Content Creation & Variety: A learning platform is only as good as its content. We need to continuously create high-quality hacking challenges and educational material. As a small team, there’s a risk of content not keeping up with user demand or covering all topics. We’ve mitigated this by carefully planning the initial content scope – focusing on a breadth of fundamental topics for launch. Post-launch, we intend to release new challenges regularly. If the project really takes off (fingers crossed!), we’ll scale by bringing in additional content creators or partnering with security experts. We might also leverage community contributions for challenge ideas (with proper vetting) to keep the content pipeline flowing. Competition & Market Adoption: The cybersecurity training space isn’t new – there are other platforms and resources out there. It’s a challenge to attract and retain users if they have alternatives. However, we strongly believe our AI mentorship and newbie-friendly approach set us apart. Our goal isn’t to compete head-on with big players but to carve a niche helping beginners who might be intimidated elsewhere. We also think the market demand is huge (remember those 3.5 million unfilled jobs!), so there’s room for innovative approaches. Our risk is that we need to effectively get the word out. As a tiny team, marketing is a challenge in itself. We’ll rely on Kickstarter to kickstart (pun intended) our community, and then user word-of-mouth – if you love Backdoors.ai, tell your friends or your company’s junior analysts! Early traction will be critical, but our grassroots strategy is to let the product speak for itself. Small Team & Resource Constraints: With just two developers, we’re juggling a lot of roles. There’s a risk in having a small team – if one of us gets sick or something unexpected happens, progress could slow down. We mitigate this by maintaining good documentation, dividing responsibilities clearly, and having contingency plans for critical tasks. We’re also keeping the project scope focused to what two people can reasonably deliver. Financially, we’re running a lean operation (hence the small funding goal), but that means we depend on careful budgeting. If costs run higher than expected (say, higher server usage or AI costs due to many users), we might need to seek additional funding or scale gradually. We’ll never compromise on delivering the core promise to backers, though – we’d rather cap user sign-ups or throttle non-essential features than let our supporters down. Despite these risks, we are very confident in Backdoors.ai’s success. Both Connor and Daniel have built software projects before and understand the pitfalls. We’ve deliberately done a lot of groundwork before coming to Kickstarter to minimize uncertainty. Our approach is iterative: launch a solid MVP, then improve continuously with your feedback. We will keep all backers informed with frequent updates throughout development. If any challenges arise, you’ll hear from us right away along with our plan to address them.

Use of AI

The Backdoors.ai mentor is powered by a large language model (LLM), currently prototyped using OpenAI’s GPT-4, to deliver real-time guidance, hints, and explanations during cybersecurity challenges. Rather than training a new model, we use existing AI trained on broad internet data and supplement it with our own curated content—such as original challenge explanations and publicly available cybersecurity definitions. All educational material used is either created by us or sourced from open, properly licensed resources. We do not use any proprietary or copyrighted material without consent, and any third-party data used in the future will be fully disclosed and credited. The AI is strictly educational, and we maintain a human-in-the-loop approach to monitor accuracy, refine responses, and ensure it never assists with anything unethical or outside the learning scope.
