Story

Rangers Forge is developing their own 1/12 Spandex Savior! (dubbed “Sparky” for the moment) It’s been in the concept stages since the end of 2024 when the first images were shown of future line-up.

Now we need YOUR help to make it a reality!!

Beyond expectations the project got fully funded within 12 hours!!!
A massive thank you to all the backers so far!

The spark has been lit!!!!

Once the campaign is finished expecting updated and progress rapports here on Kickstarter.


Due to the overwhelming response to this project, we decided to reach out to resin manufacture Resione. 
As their resin has been the main source of all out prints for the past 2 - 3 years we were interested in working together and so were THEY!

They’ve graciously decided to send over some bottles to help make the sparky project a reality.

The cost? On the release material for the Sparky project there will be an “Made with Resione” stamp.

Big thank you to Ke Feng and Resione’s Sales team for this collaboration

Sparky is a fully articulated 3D printed Action figure in the legally distinct style of the Power Rangers Lighting Collection.

The goal with sparky is to continue the collection! And get as many teams complete as possible.

Every printed figure would come with their respective rangers Helmet, Side-arm and an extra set of hands.

A female variant has also been discussed with the designer and will be made, working prototype title “Sparkette”

The groundworks for the base body have already been tested and prototyped. However professional assistance is required to get the best possible figure in your hands!

Rangers Forge has been in contact with multiple professional 3rd party designers to make this a reality and we’ve landed on the super talented Seba Dom (@ArtOfSebaDom) as our design partner.

With their impressive portfolio we’re assured the best possible result will come to reality with this collaboration.  

Rangers Forge is a one-man operation working on a small profit. As the goal was and always will be to make as affordable as possible items. This means most of the revenue goes right back into the store.

To make Sparky a reality, we need a bit more...

The current goal is set to cover the cost of the designers modelling fee and nothing more. 

Production of the backer rewards will be non-profitable.

And that’s alright with me.

Kickstarters are known to have some risks. To avoid any doubt or potential loss of funds the following is guaranteed:

If the project fails and/or the final product is not to your liking, you're free to request a refund through either Kickstarter or Rangers Forge directly.
