Story

Spinny is the upgrade your hands didn’t know they needed. Designed to give your fingers a break, it offers a natural, ergonomic grip that eliminates pinky strain, awkward claw-holds, and the daily fear of dropping your phone on your face. 

With its always-ready design, you don’t need to twist, flip, or fumble - just grab and go. Whether you’re scrolling, texting, walking, or lying down, Spinny locks into your hand like it was built for it.

Spinny makes your phone fun - even when you’re not scrolling. 

Whether you’re spinning it between calls, fidgeting during a break, or just enjoying the smooth, tactile motion, Spinny gives your hands something to do without diving into another doomscroll. 

It’s a simple, satisfying way to interact with your phone that’s playful, grounding, and screen-free.

Compatible with pretty much any phone, from any time in history, as long as there's a magnet on the back, or you use our adhesive adapter.

Spinny is a sleek, ultra-light phone grip designed in Milan. It attaches magnetically to any phone - MagSafe or via our adapter - and provides a secure, comfortable hold. But it’s more than a grip; it’s a tool for creators. 

With smooth 360° rotation, it opens up new possibilities for transitions, angles, and motion shots. 

Spinny adds functionality without bulk, whether you’re filming, scrolling, or just carrying your phone.

We set out to fix what phone design overlooked: your hands. Phones are sleek, but holding them? Not so much. 

Spinny is our answer—a minimalist, ergonomic grip that feels like an extension of your hand. It’s about comfort, control, and a bit of fun. Because interacting with your phone should feel as good as the tech inside it.

Spinny transforms your phone into a precision tool for capturing motion. Whether you’re vlogging, filming product shots, or capturing smooth pans, its rotating grip gives you dynamic angles and stable control - without bulky rigs or tripods. 

It’s like a mini gimbal that lives on the back of your phone.

Spinny delivers maximum utility in a minimal form. It’s sleek, flat, and designed to blend seamlessly with your device. No flaps, no wobble, no unnecessary extras—just smooth, functional rotation that adds value without clutter.

Born from a desire to improve the way we interact with our phones, Spinny was developed by a team of designers and engineers in Milan. After months of prototyping and testing, we’ve created a product that combines form, function, and fun. 

Spinny is the result of countless iterations, real-world testing, and obsessive refinement. From the beginning, the goal was to create a grip that feels like an extension of your hand - effortless, secure, and reliable.

Every curve, click, and material choice was carefully considered, from the premium-grade components to the high-friction rotating mechanism that delivers smooth, confident movement. Built to last, Spinny doesn’t just look good - it performs flawlessly, day after day.

Spinny introduces a new layer of interaction with your phone. The rotating disc offers tactile feedback that’s satisfying, intuitive, and surprisingly addictive. It’s the kind of subtle innovation that redefines how you hold, use, and think about your tech.

We're a group of young Italian designers, in partnership with e-Pol, a company with 10+ years of experience in phone accessories.

We want to innovate - just not with AI sh*t - but with human creativity, intention, simplicity… and a little bit of silly.

Ivan Crivicich is an Italian designer born in 2001, with a lifelong passion for creativity that began with wood carving and evolved into 3D design. Fascinated by the limitless possibilities of digital creation, he started working as a freelance 3D artist at a young age. His interest in collaborative, purpose-driven design led him to found VISIWO, a visual communication studio, in 2023. There, he connected with e-Pol and co-created Spinny—his first real product design project. For Ivan, Spinny isn’t just a product; it’s a human creativity experiment—and just the beginning of what’s to come.
