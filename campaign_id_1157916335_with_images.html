What do you shoot ?

Consider HDR at least if you shoot digital landscape, night photography, macro, water, glass, cars and whatever glossy subject, wildlife, sky, artworks, paintings, ceramics and whatever high-white-color gradients subject, and also if you shoot Black/White.

What means Dynamic Range and
what means High Dynamic Range

By "HDR" we mean video signals sent to displays from PCs playing image files containing extended brightness (>SDR) and 10–12-bit color depth, avoiding color banding.
This is not the traditional photo merging process producing a single image exposed both for lights and shadows.

HDR facts

For more than 10 years Full-frame cameras captured RAW with ~14EV dynamic range using 12–14-bit. Taking into consideration noise, the effective dynamic range was -and still is 11~12 EV.
The best photo printing technology can show only 8EV of dynamic range, the same can do the best SDR professional monitor and also the best edited JPEG SDR.
Since 2024 HDR file formats are supported by the main web browsers and by main digital developing software, and now also some social media platform can show also HDR images.

What are HDR photos ?

*Note: All images on Kickstarter (like almost all websites) are reprocessed with SDR (Standard Dynamic Range), resulting in only 8-bit JPEGs. Image labeled "HDR" here is simulation downgraded to SDR (and SDR is further downgraded).*

As said, HDR still images are files containing extended brightness (>SDR) and 10–12-bit color depth (avoid color banding), resulting in up to +4EV over SDR peak brightness.

To better understand HDR, which is born in the motion pictures environment, you can read Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_dynamic_range) 
or a video motion pictures specialized website like VMI (https://vmi.tv/blog/learn-help/hdr_survival_guide) - tanks VMI for the image shown above.

HDR photography already exists, as does the ability to view HDR images in some software—this is a non-issue. If you use Apple and lower-quality HDR HEIC files, everything works smoothly (but the HDR quality is the lowest HDR available).
Using Microsoft Windows environment, we shall use HDR file formats other than HEIC, that web browsers can already display pulled from a server—and that's even on Apple MACs.
You can read and view further on this site: https://gregbenzphotography.com/hdr/.

However, you can only view HDR images manually one-by-one, or view a slideshow within advertising frames or only at reduced 2K resolution.
To view or display HDR photos with the best possible HDR quality, fullscreen in 4k, you have to rely on one of the only two HDR-capable digital developing software: Adobe Lightroom or Zoner Studio.

If you want a structured slideshow (multi-album, varied transitions, scheduling, etc.), no device or software currently meets this need.

Shooting HDR Photos

Since almost 10 years Full-frame cameras capture RAW with ~14EV dynamic range using 12–14-bit.
Taking into consideration noise, the effective dynamic range is still 11~12 EV: more than enough for HDR developing and publishing.

We suggest to go this way: Shoot RAW → Develop HDR → Export AVIF.

Now it is possible to develop HDR only using these software, that you have to pay for:
- Adobe Lightroom (costly subscription)
- Zoner Studio (≈1/3 Lightroom’s cost)

Why avoid HEIC (today)?

HEIC (default on iPhones since iOS 11) loses HDR data on non-Apple displays (converted to SDR). Sony, Canon, and Nikon adopted HEIC but face cross-platform issues.

Critically, today HEIC files shooted in camera and also exported from editing software have a relative low peak brightness (+1 /+2EV over SDR), limited by:
- iPhone’s narrow dynamic range vs. full-frame cameras
- Apple’s early market dominance
- HDR displays were dimmer than today's, when HEIC was launched.

Future implementation sould imporve HEIC HDR quality, but until today these are the facts.
Most photographers have found HEIC’s complexity unjustified versus SDR JPEG (I too!).
This small quality improvement and the impossibility of sharing online HDR HEIC files explain the quite unanimous opinion expressed in the last years in chats and websites: it was better to stay with JPEG.
But things are changing, already changed in 2024 allowing high quality AVIF files to be published online and shared on some social networks!

Why choose AVIF ?

AVIF format allows brightness up to +4EV over SDR. It also encodes separate HDR/SDR color spaces, auto-adapting to playback systems.
Experts recommend AVIF for compressed HDR viewing.

Why use Editing software ?

HDR could expands dynamic range by +4EV beyond SDR. Manual editing (Adobe Lightroom, Zoner Studio) is essential to optimize image brightness for the brightness of the target display. Since late 2023, Adobe and Zoner added HDR development with luminance monitoring.

Color space is also important, therefore we suggest to export AVIF files with dual color profile:
- HDR (Rec.2020)
- SDR (sRGB)

HDR-Compatible Hardware

Your PC:

  • PHOTONoBOOK runs on Windows 11/10 (Intel/AMD only; ARM unsupported).
  • Modern PC GPUs support HDR graphics at OS level.
  • In Windows 11: Settings > Display > HDR Settings verifies capabilities.
    macOS version in development.

Your Display:

  • HDR TVs/monitors (HDR10, Dolby Vision, HLG) now feature peak brightness >1.000 nits (+3EV vs. SDR’s 100 nits), 10–12-bit color, and rich blacks.
  • OLED/microLED/LED tech enables HDR on all screen sizes.
  • Many displays are factory-calibrated for accurate HDR colors.

- SDR max: 8-bit (16M color shades)
- HDR: 10–12-bit (1.000M–68.000M color shades)

Where to find reliable HDR display recommendations?

Some displays cannot show a wide color gamut, but most of them can—although not all are well-calibrated by the manufacturer.
As a general rule, we can say that the more expensive displays from major brands are usually better, but it’s always best to read reviews before making a choice.
To our knowledge, the only website offering objective, instrument-based testing (rather than just opinions) is RTINGS.com.
