Risks and challenges

Bluvia H1 relies on geospatial machine learning models and environmental data sources, which means data gaps or inaccuracies in public infrastructure records or industrial zoning could affect prediction quality. Additionally, field validation of heavy metal concentrations takes time and resources, which could impact timelines for testing and calibration. Budget constraints may also limit early-stage hardware deployment or user incentive programs. We are mitigating these risks by using scalable, modular model architecture; verifying predictions against SEM-EDS baseline data; and forming partnerships with public health organizations to ensure local pilot success and data availability. In-fact part of the investment generated through Kickstarter will be used to fill the gaps in data and expand our current training set of data.
