The Story Behind Portrayals XR

Healing the Inner Child. Rewriting the Future.

We didn’t set out to build another tech company. We set out to heal ourselves.

Portrayals XR was born from a deeply personal place, a place many of us have visited but few know how to leave. That space where trauma lives just beneath the surface. Where anxiety, shame, or emotional shutdown sneak into our relationships, our parenting, our careers… and we don’t know why.

For us, it was the moment we realized that traditional tools weren’t enough. Talk therapy helped, but only scratched the surface. Self-help books opened our minds, but didn’t release the pain trapped in our bodies. We needed something more immersive. More experiential. More personal.

We needed a way to step inside the story of our younger selves.
To feel seen, held, and heard, maybe for the first time.

So we created Portrayals XR, a platform that combines AI, neuroscience, virtual reality, mobile tech, and emotional healing into a groundbreaking experience designed to reconnect you with the parts of yourself that have been forgotten, suppressed, or misunderstood.

Using VR-guided environments, immersive soundscapes, and trauma-informed design, Portrayals XR creates safe, sensory-rich moments where you can:

  • Revisit your childhood bedroom.
  • Hear the voice you needed to hear.
  • Say what was never said.
  • Feel what you were never allowed to feel.
  • All in your own time, in your own space, privately, safely, and powerfully.

This is healing from the inside out, not just managing symptoms, but rewiring the subconscious mind. And the best part? It’s scalable, accessible, and stigma-free. You don’t need a diagnosis or a therapist’s permission to start your healing journey.

You just need to start.

Risks and challenges

Risks and Challenges As with any emerging technology project, there are real challenges we want to be upfront about — and show you how we are prepared to meet them. 1. Development Timelines We are building immersive healing environments across both VR and mobile platforms, which requires coordination between developers, designers, and therapists. While we have built foundational prototypes, delays in final development, testing, or platform certification sometimes have the potential to impact delivery timelines. We have built in buffers and partnered with experienced teams to stay on track, and we’ll keep our backers updated every step of the way. 2. Budgeting for Quality Creating emotionally safe, visually rich, and therapeutically sound environments takes resources. We are allocating funds toward voice talent, trauma-informed advisors, sound therapy creation, and Unity/VR development. Stretch goals will allow for broader compatibility and even deeper experience layers. We are keeping our scope lean at launch to ensure quality over quantity. 3. Audience Education VR and emotional healing are still a new combination for many. There may be skepticism or hesitation from audiences unfamiliar with this approach. That’s why part of our rollout includes education, testimonials, and real-world case stories to build trust and awareness. 4. Tech Access While not everyone owns a VR headset, we’ve designed Portrayals XR to be accessible on mobile and tablet devices as well. This increases reach and lowers barriers while still delivering an immersive experience. Backers will get early access to both options. 5. Regulatory and Ethical Responsibility Because we are creating tools that support emotional healing, we take user safety seriously. We are not a replacement for therapy. We are working alongside licensed professionals to ensure our content supports—but never replaces—clinical care when needed. We believe in what we’re building because we’ve lived the need for it. With your support, we’ll bring this to life responsibly, powerfully, and transparently.

Use of AI

I plan to use AI-generated content in my project.

What parts of your project will use AI generated content? Please be as specific as possible.

We used AI tools (ChatGPT and DALL·E, both developed by OpenAI) to assist in creating visual assets and written descriptions for our Kickstarter campaign. Specifically, AI was used to generate: Conceptual images to represent healing experiences in virtual reality Visual mockups of scenes such as inner child reconnection, grief resolution, and future self visualizations Marketing copy, including our campaign description and video script, refined by our team These AI-generated assets helped us visually communicate our vision, but the actual VR content itself—such as audio, scripts, designs, and guided experiences—is being created by our team of coaches, creatives, and developers.

Do you have the consent of owners of the works that were (or will be) used to produce the AI generated portion of your projects? Please explain.

Yes. All AI-generated content in our project was created using tools developed by OpenAI (ChatGPT and DALL·E), which are trained on publicly available data and do not use copyrighted works without proper licensing. We did not upload or reference any copyrighted images, texts, or third-party content in the creation process. All prompts and creative direction were original and generated by our team specifically for this campaign.

I am incorporating AI in my project in another way.

Our project is not raising funds for AI tools themselves, nor are we using content that is solely generated by AI. However, we are incorporating AI in an innovative and responsible way—by training it to act as a supportive, trauma-informed healing practitioner within our immersive platform. This AI is not replacing human therapists but is designed to help guide users through emotional regulation, reflective exercises, and somatic prompts in real time. All AI interactions are developed under the guidance of experienced practitioners and thoroughly reviewed to ensure safety, empathy, and alignment with our healing methodology.
