LifeShip is a community-powered space movement to preserve Earth, continue humanity’s story, and spread life to the stars. We believe in our shared responsibility to extend life beyond.

Founded by Ben Haldeman, LifeShip now includes tens of thousands of participants across four successful space missions. Ben is a spacecraft architect who previously built dozens of big telescopes to find new exoplanets, designed and launched hundreds of satellites to help Earth at Planet Labs, and now leads LifeShip’s community space mission. Risks and challenges

Spaceflight is inherently risky. Potential issues include: Launch Delays – rocket launch dates can often slip weeks or months. We are optimistic about this launching in early 2026 but are realistic that space missions are complex and delays occur in space exploration. The mission could be cancelled or for some unforeseen reason our payload could not be included on it. LifeShip has successfully delivered four payloads for space already and we do not see any reason why this would occur. Space missions are risky and not all launches, spacecraft deployments, and missions are successful. We are flying on the most successful rocket ever built and do have high confidence this will make it to Deep Space. The spacecraft with LifeShip Pyramid could leave Earth's orbit and go into Deep Space without successfully landing on the asteroid. If this happens then we will still consider the mission a huge success—a monument in orbit around the sun that could last for billions of years. If for any reason the collections do not make it to deep space then LifeShip will make a best effort to fly content again on one of our future missions to the Moon or to another destination.
