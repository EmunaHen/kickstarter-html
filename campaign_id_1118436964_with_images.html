The Single-handed Concept

I wanted to design the shaker so that it would be fast, simple and save the bartender from having to do extra work. The idea had to be for single-handed use, which has never been done before.  It needed to eliminate a step.  It needed to make shaking and serving easier.  One of the biggest pain points for any bartender is having to separate shaker tins or the frozen cap at the top.  I also knew the design had to be something even beginner bartenders could easily use.  

Built-in Strainer 

Having a built-in strainer ensures no ice cubes will ever end up in your cocktails.  The built-in strainer can also hold back other ingredients.  With a built-in strainer, you eliminate having to purchase and utilize another bartending tool.  This also means one less thing to rinse, clean or put away.  Sometimes an actual strainer can be difficult to find.
 

Convenience: It eliminates the need for a separate straining tool, simplifying the cocktail-making process and allowing for a quicker pour.

Consistency: A built-in strainer helps ensure that only the desired liquid (the cocktail) flows into the glass, while ice and other ingredients remain in the shaker.

Less Mess: It reduces spillage and makes for a cleaner pouring experience, minimizing the mess often associated with shaking and straining cocktails.

Portability: With a strainer integrated into the shaker, it's more practical for mobile use, such as at parties or during travel, as there's one less item to carry.

Space-saving: It takes up less space in your bar or kitchen, particularly useful in smaller setups where storage is a concern.

Overall, a built-in strainer enhances the efficiency and ease of preparing cocktails, making it a valuable feature for both novice and experienced mixologists.

The Pull Sled.  Spring-loaded with an Easy Pull-back

The pull-back mechanism utilizes a spring, which is useful in allowing the bartender to control the size of the opening.  If the bartender were pouring shots or shooters, they may only pull back slightly. But when pouring a martini, a bartender can pull back all the way, allowing maximum flow.  The spring allows the mechanism to close after being let go, which will save the bartender the step of having to close the opening themselves.  

Flow Patterns

We've tested out several styles and sizes of openings to develop the best and most accurate flow pattern.  The sizing also assists with accurate pouring for shooters or shots.

One Directional Pour

You may observe that bartenders may sway the shaker back and forth to get all the liquids out.  With the Speed tin we've designed it so that all the liquid inside would flow out in one direction from top to bottom.  We designed a patented internal pitch making sure no liquid gets trapped within the shaker.

How it All Started

I've been a bartender for over 15 years, working some of the busiest high volume locations in the Jersey Shore and NYC area.  Dealing with high volume, I've always tried to create efficiencies around the bar to improve customer satisfaction and increase sales.

It all started with an idea during a busy shift in the summer of 2022. I was 4 customers deep all around the bar.  I lined up a row of shots for one of my regulars, while simultaneously shaking a set of Boston shakers with one hand and my finger placed on top of the smaller tin. Then, it was as if the world suddenly moved in slow motion for a few moments, as I stared at the shaker and thought to myself, "wow, how cool would it be if there were a way to just pour out directly from the top without having to separate the tins?!" Staring at my shaker in my hand, I thought, "there just might be some promise in inventing this."

Long-lasting design

Every aspect of Speedtin’s design was considered to meet and in most cases exceed the expected service life of the tools used in commercial food preparation and bar service. We have spent months prototyping and testing our product in some of the busiest settings to ensure that it can survive drops, bangs, cleaning, and general rough treatment of a tool intended to produce hundreds of drinks per shift. Specifically to maximizing the life of Speetin are as follows: The major components that comprise the body and moving components are made of 304 stainless steel with a substantially thicker gauge than existing bar tins. All components subject to wear and tear such the gaskets and springs are easily serviced and replaced to extend the life of the Speedtin. The grips are made to be easily removed and replaced with different styles to better suit different users' serving style.

Reusability and recyclability

As mentioned the grips are replaceable to better suit other users. As we expect Speedtin to outlast the tenure of most bartenders With exception to gaskets which constitute a very small amount of material, all major components of Speedtin are easily separated and recycled.
