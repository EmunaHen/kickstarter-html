Story

🚗 From CB Radio to the Cloud: The Voice of the Open Road

This project was born from a simple yet powerful idea: to bring the serendipity and real-time humanity of CB radio into the modern, mobile world.

For over a decade, I’ve wanted to build a digital space where travelers could hear from—and talk to—others nearby. Not through likes, filters, or endless scrolls, but through authentic, voice-based conversation.

Imagine driving across the state, and instead of silence or yet another playlist, you hear:

"Watch out—there’s a huge backup near Exit 43."
"Clean bathroom at the next gas stop!"
"Hey, is that you in the blue van? You rock!"

This app brings this vision to life.

🔧 Not My First Ride: What Led to This

This isn’t my first time building voice and location-based apps.

🗺️ FigWig

I was the founder of FigWig—a location-based, anonymous social platform that competed with Yik Yak. I developed anti-troll strategies and a novel hierarchical geo-fencing system that kept conversations context-aware but safe.

The toughest challenge? Letting people speak anonymously while still maintaining a civil, welcoming environment. FigWig taught me how to build trust without profiles, and how to moderate without silencing genuine expression. That philosophy is foundational to this project.

📂 Metago / ASTRO File Manager (Android)

As a former ScrumMaster at Metago (makers of the ASTRO File Manager), I led feature planning, QA, UX, and product coordination—everything short of direct coding. Working with a widely adopted Android app gave me firsthand experience in scaling mobile products and refining user journeys.

🧩 Other Apps I’ve Brought to Market

Minecraft download app for Android—a gamifying Minecraft model download app. We made money when people downloaded and "un-zipped" custom Minecraft maps. 

A joint collaboration with Intel that brought AirDrop-style functionality across platforms: Android, Apple, and PC. The goal? Seamless local file transfer, no matter the device.

Risks and challenges

Drive to Connect is a software project, which means our biggest risks lie in development scope, testing timelines, and cross-device performance. While we're building a lean MVP, voice-first technology—especially across iOS and Android—can present unpredictable technical challenges. That’s why we’ve budgeted for both development and testing, and we’re working with experienced developers who understand mobile audio systems. Another challenge is community: since the app depends on people using it while traveling, we know adoption matters. That’s why we’re launching with a strong pre-built audience and community support baked into our campaign. If delays or roadblocks happen, we’ll be transparent, honest, and communicative. Backers will receive regular updates, and early supporters will play a role in shaping decisions through beta access and feedback sessions.

Use of AI

Yes, this project has used and may use AI tools in support of development and moderation. Specifically: We’ve used AI-assisted writing tools (e.g. ChatGPT) to help draft portions of our campaign copy and planning documents. We’ve used AI-generated images for early concept art and visual brainstorming. We may incorporate AI-powered content moderation tools in the final product to help ensure community safety, detect harmful speech, and support user wellbeing. All creative direction, product decisions, and final outputs are managed and reviewed by humans. Our goal is to use AI responsibly, in ways that enhance safety and clarity—never to replace human creativity or connection.
