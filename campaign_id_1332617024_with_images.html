Story

(A social‑first mental‑wellness app that transforms a 3‑second mood check‑in into real‑time support, evidence‑based micro‑activities, and meaningful community connections.)

1. Our Story & the Problem We’re Solving

More than 1 in 5 people report feeling seriously lonely on any given week. While hundreds of wellness apps promise “community,” most still keep you scrolling alone. Meanwhile, privacy scandals and inconsistent clinical standards make many users wary of sharing their most personal data online.

MoodLink was born from one simple insight: if we could turn an emotion into an invitation, our phones could become the fastest bridge between how we feel now and the human help we need next.

2. What Exactly Is MoodLink?

Core layers & why they matter

• Color‑Swipe Check‑In — Drag your thumb across a living gradient, pick today’s color‑feel, add (optional) voice note. Result: 3‑second habit → 30‑day emotional graph.

• AI Feel‑Fuse Feed — GPT‑powered model sums up your entries and pushes a micro‑action (60‑sec breath drill, quick stretch, or “nudge a friend” card). Result: Converts awareness into scientifically grounded action.

• Circle‑Match — Smart algorithm groups 3–5 peers in complementary mood ranges for live text / audio rooms. Result: Peer support proven to improve outcomes & retention.

• Clinician‑Verified Journeys — Short, leveled paths (e.g., “Navigate Sunday Scaries”) written by psychologists. Result: Structure without replacing therapy.

• Privacy‑First Vault — End‑to‑end encrypted; zero ads; GDPR‑ready audit log; local delete‑any‑time. Result: Trust by design, not promise.

Stretch Goal (€110 k) → AR Mood‑Maps: see opt‑in circles in your vicinity via soft‑focus overlay.

3. Why Now?

The global mental‑health app market was ≈ $7.5 billion in 2024 and is projected to triple by 2032 at 14–18 % CAGR.

Kickstarter backers already support transparent, impact‑driven health‑tech; MoodLink fills the clinically‑literate, community‑first, privacy‑obsessed gap.

Risks and challenges

Risks & Challenges Clinical efficacy • Why it matters: MoodLink offers mental-wellness guidance; ineffective or unverified advice could erode trust—or worse, harm users. • How we’ll address it: • All “Journeys” are written or reviewed by a panel of four licensed psychologists. • An IRB-approved usability study runs during closed beta; we’ll publish the results before v1.0. • Any content that might be construed as medical advice is clearly flagged with “Wellness only – not a substitute for therapy.” Data privacy & security • Why it matters: Users share highly personal mood data. • How we’ll address it: • End-to-end encryption for entries and chats. • Annual third-party penetration test plus GDPR audit log. • No ads and zero data resale; our revenue comes from subscriptions only. AI hallucinations • Why it matters: GPT summaries could generate misleading suggestions. • How we’ll address it: • Fine-tuned models on a vetted corpus; high-risk outputs blocked by moderation filters. • Prompts that mention self-harm escalate to human moderators and crisis-line resources. Timeline slips • Why it matters: AI and mobile projects often overrun. • How we’ll address it: • Two-week sprints with a public burndown board in Discord. • Buffer baked in: we promise a Jan 2026 launch, but our internal goal is Nov 2025. Platform rule changes • Why it matters: Apple or Google policy shifts could delay store approvals. • How we’ll address it: Dual-track builds (TestFlight + APK sideload) so backers can still access new versions if one store is held up. Stretch-goal creep • Why it matters: Extra features (Watch widget, AR Mood-Maps) can pull focus from core release. • How we’ll address it: v1 scope is frozen; stretch-goal code lives in separate branches and ships only after the main launch. Shipping risk • All rewards are digital, so there are no supply-chain delays or customs fees to worry about. ⸻ We’ll post monthly video updates, share sprint demos in real time, and flag any setback the moment it appears—along with our revised plan. If something ever becomes impossible, backers will vote on the best alternative use of that budget or receive proportional refunds.

Use of AI

How MoodLink will use AI and why we’re seeking funding 1. Building core AI features (funding request) • We will fine-tune an open-weight large language model (LLM) plus a lightweight on-device classifier to: • summarise daily mood entries (“AI Feel-Fuse Feed”) • suggest micro-actions (breathing drill, 2-min stretch, “nudge a friend”) • Kickstarter funds cover GPU training time, inference credits, and data-privacy audits. 2. AI-generated in-app content • The LLM produces short, original text: calming prompts, evidence-based tips, and friendly summaries of a user’s 30-day emotional graph. • All outputs are post-processed through content-safety filters; anything medical, diagnostic, or self-harm related is escalated to a human moderator and crisis-line resources. • We never present AI text as professional medical advice; every suggestion carries a “wellness only” disclaimer. 3. Additional AI use-cases • Circle-Match algorithm: a clustering model groups 3–5 peers with complementary moods for live text/audio rooms. • Privacy-First Vault: on-device AI redacts personally identifying info before optional cloud sync. • Dynamic theme engine: a small GAN variant generates gradient backgrounds that reflect aggregated community mood (purely cosmetic). 4. Data & IP assurances • Training uses only: • public-domain or CC-BY mental-health corpora • licensed clinical-psycho-education materials • first-party user data collected with explicit opt-in (de-identified). • No copyrighted or proprietary text is ingested without permission. • Users can export or delete their mood data at any time (GDPR compliant). 5. Risk mitigation & transparency • Monthly model-performance audits shared in public updates. • External penetration test + bias evaluation before each major release. • If an AI component under-performs or becomes unsafe, we will disable it and notify backers immediately, offering a roadmap revision or partial refund vote. In short, we’re seeking funding to develop, fine-tune, and run the AI that powers MoodLink’s mood summaries, micro-actions, and peer-matching, while meeting Kickstarter’s AI policy for safety, data rights, and transparency.
