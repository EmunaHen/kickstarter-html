Story


The Story Behind Rawr.


Rawr. was born out of a simple frustration: most queer apps felt too limited, too outdated, or just riddled with ads. I wanted something different, something that celebrated who we are, without filters or shame.

The idea started as a sketch during a late-night moment filled with coffee, memes, and a desire to create a space where queer men could connect without judgment, subscriptions, or pop-up ads chaos. Just real-time discovery, real conversations, and a vibe that’s unapologetically us.

I didn’t have a big team. Just a vision, a laptop, and the drive to learn everything I didn’t know. I began with simple screens login, sign-up, profile cards and built from there. Slowly, the pieces came together: live location tracking, media-rich chat, and profile customization that actually matters.

Rawr. is still growing. It’s queer, it’s chaotic, it’s intentional, and it’s ours.

And it’s just getting started.

Risks and challenges

Risks and Challenges 1. Technical Complexity & Scaling Building a real-time, location-based app with messaging, media sharing, and security features is technically demanding especially for a solo developer. How I’ll handle it: I’m taking a modular approach, starting with core features first. I’m using scalable backend services and modern frameworks to ensure performance grows with the user base. For advanced parts, I’ll bring in help when needed. 2. User Safety & Moderation Apps in the queer space often face harassment, spam, and fake accounts. Plan: I’m implementing tools for reporting, blocking, and content filtering from day one. Future updates will include behavior-based trust ratings and AI-supported moderation. 3. Privacy & Data Protection Handling sensitive data like location and photos in queer spaces means privacy must be rock-solid. Solution: All data will be encrypted, and the app will follow GDPR principles. The privacy policy is transparent, and only essential data will be collected. 4. Age Verification & Authenticity Ensuring that all users are over 18 and real is critical, especially with adult content or kink-friendly spaces. Approach: The app includes a date-of-birth check during sign-up. I plan to integrate ID or selfie verification to confirm age and identity for added trust and safety. 5. App Store Approval & Platform Bias LGBTQ+ and kink-positive apps sometimes face stricter moderation or bias from app store platforms. Strategy: RAWR. will follow all Apple and Google content policies carefully. NSFW features will be age-gated, optional, and clearly separated to meet requirements. 6. Building and Retaining a Community A great app needs people and growing a user base from scratch is a big challenge. Plan: I’ll launch in queer-friendly online communities first, use targeted social media campaigns, and create shareable content. Listening to user feedback will shape future updates.
