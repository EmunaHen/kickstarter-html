Story

Introducing our first product—the BG-1 Indexing Bevel Gauge. This isn’t just another bevel gauge; it’s a leap forward in layout tool technology. Whether you’re setting up a complex miter or refining a simple angle, the BG-1 is engineered to deliver consistent, accurate, and repeatable results every single time.

What Makes the BG-1 Special?

  1. Patent-Pending Indexing Mechanism
    Effortlessly set common angles with positive locks from 15° to 165° in precise 15° increments—including 22.5°, 67.5°, 112.5°, and 157.5°. No more guesswork or chasing down protractors.
  2. Independent Blade & Angle Locking
    Independent locking mechanisms allow you to adjust the blade length or angle separately, giving you complete control to extend, retract, or set without disrupting your settings. Total control —no compromises.
  3. Retractable Kickstand
    Keep the blade flat against your work surface for stability and enhanced control. It’s like having an extra hand to hold everything in place, it ensures your bevel gauge stays exactly where you need it.
  4. Angle Indicator Markings & Vernier Scale
    Adjust angles precisely with the dial face featuring 1° increments and dial them in down to 0.1° with our built-in Vernier scale. Confidently set any angle with unmatched precision for your most demanding projects.
  5. Premium Materials
    Crafted from aerospace-grade 6061-T651 aluminum, 304 stainless steel components, and a 440C Stainless Steel blade, the BG-1 is built to last for generations.

Traditional bevel gauges rely on friction locks and external tools—like protractors or sine blocks—to set and layout angles. As woodworking enthusiasts, we found that process clunky and time-consuming, especially when dealing with a laying out bunch of miter cuts. Inspired by the dividing heads used in rotary tables and indexing detents used in motorsports, we reimagined the bevel gauge to include a patent-pending positive locking indexing mechanism right inside the tool.

The result? Faster setup, more accurate angles, and less downtime. By fusing motorsports engineering and workshop know-how, we’ve elevated a simple but essential tool into something that feels premium, purposeful, and precise.

The BG-1 is simple to use whether you're setting the BG-1 to a positive locking indexed angle, or any angle. 

Setting the BG-1 to a Positive Locking Indexed Angle

Effortlessly set common angles with positive locks from 15° to 165° in precise 15° increments—including 22.5°, 67.5°, 112.5°, and 157.5°.

  1. Unlock the cam lock on the rear of the BG-1.
  2. Pull down the detent release button.
  3. Rotate the blade angle to an indexed position.
  4. Release/push the button up to lock into the positive lock of the indexed angle.
  5. Lock the cam lock on the rear of the BG-1 to lock the angle in place.

Setting the BG-1 to Any Angle

The only difference when setting an angle that isn't indexed, is that you don't push the positive locking button back into place. 

  1. Unlock the cam lock on the rear of the BG-1.
  2. Pull down the detent button.
  3. Rotate the blade angle to the desired angle.
  4. Lock the cam lock on the rear of the BG-1 to lock the angle in place.

Adjust Blade Length

  • Turn the blade lock knob counterclockwise.
  • Move the blade to the desired length.
  • Turn the blade lock knob clockwise.

Open the Kickstand

  • Open the kickstand either direction using the "switch" lever.

The journey from concept to creation has been exciting and meticulous. We’ve taken every step with care to ensure the BG-1 is well-engineered and crafted.

The Idea

Traditional bevel gauges often require a friction lock and additional tools to set specific angles. We were determined to eliminate this constant back-and-forth. Drawing inspiration from dividing heads on rotary tables and detent mechanisms used in motorsports, we envisioned a bevel gauge that indexes the most common angles directly—reducing setup time and frustration.

Sketches

From the initial idea, we began sketching out the features we wanted to include in the BG-1. Notably, our patent-pending design features an indexing mechanism that uses a detent wheel and pin to lock in the most common angles used by woodworkers.

3D Modeling

We created detailed 3D models, allowing us to visualize the BG-1 in full detail and indexing mechanism functionality. This step was crucial for optimizing handle ergonomics to the indexing mechanism geometry, ensuring that every element of the design was optimized for both form and function.

3D Printing

Using 3D printing technology, we created multiple prototypes for physical testing and functional validation. User feedback was invaluable, guiding us to make further improvements. Each round of feedback helped refine the indexing mechanism, ergonomics, and user experience.

Test, Revise, Iterate

We tested dozens of detent wheel designs, adjusting each notch’s shape for precision and fine-tuning the actuator button until it felt natural and intuitive. After rigorous testing, prototyping, and countless refinements, we’re confident that our final design redefines the traditional bevel gauge.

Production Prototype

Once we were satisfied with the final design, we manufactured prototypes from the actual production materials—aerospace-grade 6061-T651 aluminum, 304 stainless steel, and a 440C stainless steel blade.  This phase allowed us to inspect quality standards across multiple manufacturers and ensure our product meets—and exceeds—Quantis Tool Works’ exacting requirements.

During this phase, we meticulously refined the indexing and detent mechanism to deliver unwavering precision and eliminate any slack in the angle-setting process, ensuring every adjustment feels rock-solid.

Final Testing

Once we had real pre-production samples of the final design, the samples were sent out to hobbyists and professionals in the industry to provide additional feedback and validate the final design was ready for launch.
