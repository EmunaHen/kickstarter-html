Story

Industrial Quality Robotics for Hobbyists, Makers and Professionals. 

The 401Bot

For the first time, professional quality robotics are accessible for all makers. The 401Bot is a full 6 Axis Robotic Arm capable of creating functional products, services and automations.

The New Makerspace

The 401Bot Makerspace allows users to create, upload, download and share robot programs in the same way we currently do with 3D printing files.

Integration of 3D Printing and Robotics

Makers now have the ability to create your own programs, jigs, end effectors, tools, grippers, automation sequences and so much more. No experience required.

Empowering the Maker

The 401Bot creates an entire new maker category. Bring your ideas to life, accelerate your workflow, grow or launch your business, be part of the future.

We've Made Industrial Robotics Easy.

Over the last year and a half, our software team has been working hard to create our own 401Bot software. The 401Bot control software has modes that make it accessible to everyone from complete beginners all the way to seasoned robotics engineers. Simply move the 401Bot and save waypoints, then export as a 401Bot program. We've also integrated the 401Bot with RoboDK, unlocking function for intense industrial and commercial applications.

Below is a full demo video showing both software options for controlling the 401Bot!

The 401Bot is capable of everything from folding your laundry and automating your coffee machine (yes, seriously) to industrial pick and pack applications for your business.

Over the last couple of years, we've seen a rapid integration of artificial intelligence and automation. The way we do things is changing and we want the maker community to be a part of it. 

As Hobbyists and Enthusiasts, our real world impact has been limited by the machines available to us. Until now, most of what we could create has been primary prototypes, trinkets, gadgets, gizmos and personal use objects. With the 401Bot, we're flipping all that upside down and showing we can create practical, functional and real world products, services and automations.

We engineered the 401Bot with the goal of providing professional quality robotics at a price that hobbyists and enthusiasts can access. We were able to do this though multiple iterations of prototyping, our patent pending design and the integration of 3D printed parts with custom electronics.

Reach And Payload Visualized

The most common tech specs we get asked about are the reach and payload so we made this awesome animation! The reach from center to end effector is 521mm and from center to standard gripper center is 643mm. The payload capacity is 2kg. 

Repeatability Testing

Our current pre-production model of the 401Bot is achieving a repeatability of 0.15mm. We expect the performance of the repeatability to increase on the production unit as we have a new J1 motor control system. 

** Please note that all testing has been done with our pre-production 401Bot unit. The results may be slightly different on the production model. Though we only expect the specs to increase in performance, we believe it's important to note this. We will post full demo testing videos with the production unit once it's here!  

Company Backstory (Optional Read!) - Our design company here in NYC was tasked to build an assembly line for a client, which required picking and packing small electronics. After building the system we realized it could probably be operated by a robotic arm, so we did a deep dive... which is when we discovered the lack of options for affordable industrial quality robotic arms. This immediately kickstarted our R&D journey to build our own. Fast forward a couple of years... and today we're thrilled to show you the 401Bot.

Affordability - Traditional Pre-Built 6 Axis Robotic Arms are extremely expensive. They often start around $10,000-$20,000. In fact, even a simple end effector/gripper can often start at several thousand dollars.

Integration of 3D Printing - The rapid innovation in the 3D printing industry has made quality 3D printing extremely affordable. 3D Printing + 401Bot = Unlimited Capabilities. We can now design and 3D print everything from parts for the 401Bot itself to jigs, end effectors, and other automation tools like conveyer belts, sorters, grippers... you name it. With 3D printing and the 401Bot you can automate almost any task. 

The 401Bot Marketplace - The 401Bot Marketplace will allow users to create, share, upload and download programs for the 401Bot. In the same way we currently do with 3D printing files, we can now do the same for robot programs. 

Patented Design and Easy Build - The 401Bot is easy to build. It starts with our patent pending design, includes a custom wiring harness and custom electronics, and the 401Bot is controlled using our custom software. It's truly plug and play industrial quality robotics. 

Community - Our goal is always to empower the maker community. Together, we can design and create products, services and automations that will be used in industries around the world. The 401Bot is here to enable entrepreneurs, creators and hobbyists.

3D Printing + 401Bot = Unlimited Capabilities 

The 401Bot is your 3D Printer's perfect companion. Not only can the 401Bot automate your 3D printer... but you can now use your 3D printer to print functional parts that work with the 401Bot.

With 3D printing and robotics, we can automate almost any task... Here are two fun examples!

Example 1 - Bed Removal Jig

Take for example the 3D Printer Bed Removal program we made. In the past, creating an automation like this would be extremely expensive. However, we were able to simply 3D print a Jig that the 401Bot can use to grab the build plates. This same concept can be applied to almost any automation task. (Ps. you probably don't need a humanoid robot to do that task!)

Example 2 - Paint Can Jig Example! 

To illustrate this even further, we wanted to make a Jig that would allow us to use the 401Bot for painting. Well, a traditional painting end effector/gripper for a robotic arm is extremely expensive... but with some basic design and a 3D Print, we were up and running in no time. The best part... with the 401Bot Makerspace, you can now simply download the paint Jig and use it on your 401Bot too! 

The 401Bot is capable of industrial robotic applications which is why we have already integrated the 401Bot with RoboDK. The integration of RoboDK allows those users looking to push the limits of robotics by creating advanced simulations, environments and controls for the 401Bot. 

There are a few popular industrial robot control software packages out there. We will work with the backers after the launch to determine if we should integrate the 401Bot with any others!

Hardware Development

Our current 401Bot is extremely close to being ready for production, we only have one hardware change remaining, the J1 or the base of the robot will change shape a little bit, allowing for a more precise motor and a hole through the base for the custom wiring harness to run through. This will not only improve the performance but also hide all the cabling! 

Software Development

The current 401Bot software is robust enough to create, save, and run programs, however, we are currently in the process of greatly upgrading it. The updates will allow users to control the robot in a few new ways and also allow the implementation of things like third party electronics, cameras, sensors, etc. We are making it so that the software has control options for everyone from complete beginners to industry experts.

Tariffs 

Our company is based in the USA and in order to mitigate the impact of any potential tariffs we have a few measures in place. First, we have an office in Canada that can be used in the event that there are any major changes to our import tariffs (this is the tariff we would need to pay to get the 401Bots from overseas to our office here in the USA). Second, we have a backup production facility located outside of China; this mitigates the impact of any USA - China specific tariffs. As of July 22, 2025 (the launch day for this Kickstarter), we do not anticipate any major trade or tariff issues. The ongoing tariff dispute between China and the USA has mostly come to a resolution. We have been shipping a ton of prototypes and parts back and forth for the last year and have been managing the situation accordingly. Backers will be responsible for any import costs/tariffs to their respective countries, we will do our best to keep this as low as possible.
