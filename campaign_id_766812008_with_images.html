Story

FollowFit is a high-end electric wagon equipped with cutting-edge AI vision technology, combining convenience, functionality, intelligence, and comfort. It supports three flexible modes: AI-powered follow, manual pull assist, and remote control, catering to every adventurer's needs. Designed to enhance transportation, storage, and entertainment during camping, FollowFit meets diverse requirements in various scenarios.

Smart Follow Mode

Adopt Al visual tracking technology to accurately identify and track users, without the need for additional receivers / remote control and frequent operations, achieving hands-free operation.

Pull Assist Mode

Allows for easy navigation of narrow paths or precise control, retaining the traditional camping experience.

Remote Control Mode

Provides 360° flexible remote control, mastering complex terrain from a distance.

Equipped with low latency response, FollowFit processes data at millisecond speeds, ensuring stable following without lag and guaranteeing safety and reliability. 

Its advanced vision recognition system automatically avoids obstacles like rocks and trees, adapts to complex outdoor terrain, and can automatically brake in emergencies. This technology allows users to travel light and focus on enjoying nature rather than battling equipment.

FollowFit is built to withstand the rigors of outdoor exploration. Its all-terrain adaptability features anti-slip tank universal wheels that easily handle muddy, rocky, grassy, and other complex terrains. 

The high-load chassis can statically carry 100 kg. With a large capacity battery supporting all-day use and a USB port for temporary phone/device charging, FollowFit ensures you stay powered up. The low-noise motor preserves the natural tranquility, offering a silent operation that enhances the immersive outdoor experience.

Creation Journey of FollowFit

Dear FollowFit fans,

I am Dr. Cheng Liu from the FollowFit team. Since we founded the FollowFit Group and released Followfit E-wagon project, we have continued to receive enthusiastic attention and feedback from everyone. Every day we receive emails from all over the world expressing their love for the project, asking when it will be available, and describing how he/she will own and use it.

We are touched by your support, and it is also our greatest motivation.

Today, I'd like to share with you the development journey of FollowFit, how we designed and built it, and how the staff overcame the challenge to bring it to you. Yes, it's unlike anything you've seen before, it's real, and we did it!

1.  Leo’s wish

I have always loved the outdoors. As a father, I often take my six-year-old son, Leo, on camping trips with our traditional wagon. Every time we go out, Leo loves to sit in the wagon and ask me to pull him around. However, he’s still too young to pull the cart by himself. One day, out of the blue, Leo said to me, “Daddy, wouldn’t it be fun if the wagon could follow me around like a puppy?” That simple wish sparked an idea.

Children’s stories remind us that technology should help people, not replace them. In everyday life, it’s often the simplest, most practical features that bring us real joy and convenience. Inspired by this, we decided to turn Leo’s dream into reality.

Figure 1: Leo is helping to test the prototype, together with his portable fan.

2.  Just following? How hard can it be!

“Come on, it’s just a cart that can follow you—not a Boston Dynamics robot. How long could it take? Two months? Maybe three?” That’s what I said to my team in the beginning.

But as life often shows us, things rarely go as smoothly as planned. We soon discovered that the UWB technology commonly used for auto-follow features wasn’t as effective—or as enjoyable—as we’d hoped. As a form of radio technology, UWB comes with some fundamental limitations:

  • An additional signal receiver must be carried

For the cart to detect your approximate location, you have to carry a UWB receiver with you at all times. In reality, the cart isn’t actually following you—it’s following the receiver in your hand. If you were to toss the receiver into a lake, the cart would head straight for the water without a second thought. So, you’d better keep an eye on it!

  • Sensitive to metal interference

UWB signals also have a very weak ability to penetrate metal. In areas with lots of vehicles or metal structures, the signal can easily fail or become much less accurate. If the UWB device gets close to a metal object—like a car door or a metal shelf—the signal can even be completely interrupted.

  • Multipath effects cause positioning drift

In complex environments, like warehouses with shelves or crowded shopping malls, UWB signals can be reflected by surrounding objects, creating what’s known as multipath interference. This interference leads to errors in distance measurement and positioning, causing the cart to suddenly “jump” to the wrong location as it tries to follow its target.

  • Electromagnetic interference

The distortion rate of UWB signals near high-voltage power stations and MRI equipment can exceed 60%, leading to confusion and errors in following commands. In fact, because UWB signals are close to the noise level, any high-power, multi-frequency interference can disrupt them.

That’s why we shifted our focus to AI vision. Since AI vision technology is already capable of recognizing pedestrians in autonomous driving, we realized it could be used for following tasks as well. Unlike UWB, AI vision offers several key advantages:

  • Complete immunity to signal interference

Whether you're in a metallic environment, a high-power electromagnetic environment, or a complex multipath environment, computer vision won't be interfered with. We don't have to worry about signal interruptions or jumps anymore.

  • Signal receivers are no longer required

The camera resembles an eye, which allows the cart to recognize and follow its owner like a puppy. We don’t need an additional signal receiver anymore.

  • Greater technical potential

In addition to human body, AI vision can also recognize obstacles such as trees and stones. As a result, the AI vision technology has even greater potential to enable obstacle avoidance to further improve intelligence and safety.

Next, it’s time for our hardware and software engineers to show up.

In terms of hardware, we have carried out a lot of self-developed, mainly the design and development of PCBA boards. It includes the most core high-performance AI computing module, as well as various other control and driver boards. All PCBA boards have gone through more than three generations of iterations to ensure stable performance.

Figure 2: The self-developed AI core board

Figure 3: The other control and driver boards

In terms of software, we use the most popular YOLO algorithm to ensure that our model can run on the edge AI chip with lightweight and achieve centimeter-level high-precision ranging. Despite the good foundation, the development and optimization of the FollowFit AI algorithm took more than 8 months, with no less than 20 iterations and updates, and finally succeeded in improving the accuracy of human recognition from 63% to 95%.

Figure 4: AI vision-based human recognition and accurate ranging function

At the same time, all FollowFit staff participated in the collection of AI datasets, totaling more than 8,000 pieces, to ensure that our AI model is well trained to accurately recognize a wide variety of different human bodies.

Figure 5: Collection of human body datasets

3.  Prototype machine

After the main software algorithms and hardware PCBA were developed, we started to build the first prototype. It is based on a traditional hand-pulled wagon. As you can see, its motor control board and battery are all exposed, and it is covered with test traces.

Figure 6: The Prototype machine

Based on the prototype machine, we completed the testing and selection of other key components. We decided to configure and design the FollowFit to the highest specifications.

Different from ordinary brushed motors, we chose a high-performance brushless hub motor. Its power conversion efficiency is higher, which can save more than 35% of electricity; Quiet operation, no physical wear and tear, and a service life of up to 7-10 years. It’s spark-free and generate low heat, making it safer and more reliable.

More robust off-road wheels are necessary. In this way, FollowFit can better adapt to various terrains, and has better passability and carrying capacity.

In terms of batteries, we selected a better ternary lithium battery pack. Its energy density is more than 5 times higher than that of ordinary lead-acid batteries, and its capacity is 5~10 times that of ordinary electric campers, thus ensuring the endurance of FollowFit.

In addition, we have equipped it with a set of automotive-grade ultrasonic radars to ensure that FollowFit is safe even in extreme cases of vision failure.

Although the cost of these parts is higher, they bring more performance and experience to FollowFit, so we still think it’s worth it.

4.  APP? I really don't want another one

With so many features, the next question was: how should we operate it? An app seemed like the obvious choice, just like many other smart devices today.

But honestly, I didn’t want to have to take out my phone, connect to a wireless device, and navigate a complicated app every time I went outdoors. That would take the fun out of the experience. We wanted FollowFit to be a reliable companion, not just another gadget full of unnecessary features.

At the same time, we knew the physical buttons on the wagon itself needed to be as simple as possible—so simple that even my grandmother could use them with ease.

5.  Appearance Matters

With the AI algorithms and core components in place, the rough appearance of our prototype no longer did justice to its powerful inner workings. So, we partnered with a professional design team—winners of both the Red Dot Award and the IDEA Award—to work on the industrial design of FollowFit. Together, we developed the following design concepts:

a) In terms of visual language, we wanted people to immediately sense the advanced technology, while still feeling the familiar charm of a traditional hand-pulled wagon. We also made sure to retain the practical folding feature.

b) Drawing inspiration from automotive design, we introduced the concept of a “front battery compartment.” This allows us to neatly conceal the battery, motor controller, and other key components, making the wagon both sleek and well-protected.

c) We added a front-facing LED searchlight, providing illumination so FollowFit can operate even in low-light conditions. It also doubles as a convenient outdoor camping light.

d) To enhance interaction, we incorporated a multi-segment RGB ambient light. Its color can change to indicate the device’s working status, making communication between FollowFit and the user more intuitive and engaging.

Figure 7: Exterior and structural design

"It doesn't look bad!" After the design drawings came out, everyone said.

6.  Life in the factory

Designing is one thing—bringing it to life is another.

To faithfully reproduce every detail from our design drawings, all metal parts had to be precisely machined, while every plastic component was first verified through 3D printing. Only after rigorous testing did we move on to molding, ensuring product consistency and laying a solid foundation for future mass production.

Figure 8: 3D printed plastic parts

Time was of the essence, so we moved directly into the factory.

We examined every detail—from the choice of metal materials to the color of plastic parts, from shrinkage structures to battery mounting methods, from the height of the handle to the fabric of the storage bag. Since April 2025, we have upgraded and refined our prototypes month after month to meet our expectations. To date, the FollowFit engineering prototype has reached its fourth iteration.
