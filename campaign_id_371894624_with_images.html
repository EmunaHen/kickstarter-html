Story

The humanoid robot FideLIS was born in 2022, the idea originated from a school project. It started as a personal challenge, the prototype evolved into a perfect device for the Sign Language translation. Its hand quickly adapt to the arm movement to respond to communication needs.

FideLIS' hands use the Italian Sign Language to fluently interact with whoever might use it. This extraordinary progress is an important technological evolution and it also sets a significant tool for the inclusion of deaf people. We try to create a world where accessability is guaranteeded to everyone! 

Our current team is composed of Riccardo Pedone (CEO), Alessia Tescaro (CIO), Domenico Tevere (COO), and Yuri Michelotti (CTO). We are four ambitious and dedicated young professionals driven by the goal of making healthcare—and beyond—truly accessible for the deaf community.

Our project, FideLIS, is a humanoid robot capable of interpreting Italian Sign Language (LIS). It is designed to support hospitals and healthcare facilities by breaking down communication barriers and promoting greater independence for deaf individuals.

We are currently collaborating with the Innovative Companies Incubator of the Polytechnic University of Turin (I3P), one of the top incubators in Europe and globally. FideLIS has already been recognized and appreciated at several competitions and industry events, confirming the value and potential of our initiative.                                                        

Why support a project like FideLIS?


Supporting FideLIS means contributing to a more inclusive future where technology breaks down communication barriers and makes healthcare truly accessible for deaf individuals. Risks and challenges

Uno dei principali rischi di un progetto come FideLIS riguarda l’accettazione da parte degli utenti finali, in particolare delle persone sorde. Nonostante l’intento inclusivo, la tecnologia potrebbe non rispondere perfettamente alle diverse esigenze comunicative e culturali della comunità sorda, rischiando di risultare poco efficace o addirittura non utilizzata. Per mitigare questo rischio, abbiamo attivato fin da subito una collaborazione diretta con associazioni che rappresentano persone sorde e con disabilità, come Lega del Filo d’Oro, Fedora e Listen. Queste partnership ci permettono di raccogliere feedback costante e aggiornato dagli utenti reali, adattando lo sviluppo di FideLIS alle loro esigenze specifiche. Inoltre, prevediamo cicli regolari di test sul campo e co-progettazione partecipata per garantire l’efficacia e l’accettazione del prodotto finale. --- One of the main risks for a project like FideLIS is user acceptance, particularly among the deaf community. Despite its inclusive purpose, the technology might not fully meet the diverse communicative and cultural needs of deaf individuals, potentially leading to limited adoption or ineffective. To mitigate this risk, we have established early and ongoing collaborations with key associations representing people with hearing and other disabilities, such as Lega del Filo d’Oro, Fedora, and Listen. These partnerships allow us to gather continuous, real-world feedback and ensure that FideLIS is developed in alignment with users’ actual needs. We also plan to implement regular field testing and participatory co-design sessions to enhance usability, relevance, and user acceptance.

Long-lasting design

Il materiale che utilizzeremo è uno dei più resistenti per la stampa 3D attualmente disponibili sul mercato: il PETG (polietilene tereftalato glicolato). Grazie alle sue eccellenti proprietà meccaniche, il PETG garantisce un’elevata resistenza a sollecitazioni, urti e calore, rendendolo ideale per applicazioni in ambienti complessi come quelli sanitari. Oltre alla sua robustezza, il PETG rappresenta anche una scelta sostenibile: è riciclabile e produce basse emissioni di composti organici volatili (VOC) durante la stampa, contribuendo a un processo produttivo più sicuro e rispettoso dell’ambiente. --- The material we will use is one of the most durable 3D printing materials available on the market: PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol). Known for its excellent mechanical properties, PETG offers high resistance to stress, impact, and heat, making it ideal for demanding applications such as healthcare environments. In addition to its strength and durability, PETG is also a more sustainable choice. It is recyclable and emits low levels of VOCs (volatile organic compounds) during printing, contributing to a safer and more environmentally friendly production process.

Sustainable materials

FideLIS is entirely made by 3D printing with PETG filament, this approach guarantees a greater sustainability and durability of the product, thanks to the ecological and resistant properties of the material. --- Realizzato interamente tramite stampa 3D con PETG, questo approccio garantisce una maggiore sostenibilità e durabilità del prodotto, grazie alle proprietà ecologiche e resistenti del materiale.

Something else

Un pratica che implementeremo è la creazione just in time, ovvero un sistema di produzione e approvvigionamento in cui i materiali e i componenti vengono realizzati o forniti esattamente nel momento in cui sono necessari, riducendo così gli sprechi, i costi di magazzino e aumentando l’efficienza complessiva del processo produttivo. --- One practice we will implement is just-in-time production, a system where materials and components are produced or delivered exactly when needed. This approach minimizes waste, reduces inventory costs, and improves overall production efficiency.
