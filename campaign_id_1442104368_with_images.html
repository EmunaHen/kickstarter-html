Story

There are plenty of pour over kettles on the market, but most only offer the illusion of choice. 

Beneath the surface, they’re just basic kettles dressed in a gooseneck, wobbly plastic knob and abstract claims about control and precision. Even the premium models feel dated, with slight improvements wrapped in decade-old tech, priced like they are breakthroughs.

Until now.

Palatti Enso is a complete rethinking of what a performance electric kettle should be. From the interchangeable spouts to the intuitive touch interface, from the magnetic knob to its potential for complete customization, every detail has been reimagined. Not just to impress with pure looks, but to perform in your quest.

When comparing the countless pour-over kettles on the market, the spout design stands out as the key functional differentiator. After carefully analyzing and measuring mainstream kettles, we discovered that spout performance can be objectively characterized by the influence of tilt angle and flow rate.

Kettles that users generally find easier to control typically have a lower maximum flow rate. For instance, the Brand F uses a flow restrictor inside its spout, limiting its maximum flow to around 16 g/s. With these types of kettles, adjusting the tilt angle between roughly 20 to 50 degrees results in a flow rate range from about 1 to 16 g/s.

By repeatedly refining every detail of our Still spout, we’ve enhanced stability at ultra-low flow rates of 1–3 g/s. We designed dozens of spout variations, each with distinct curvature angles, and conducted thousands of tests to ensure a consistent, more linear relationship between kettle tilt and water flow.

The result: the Still spout offers exceptional control, maintaining a stable, linear flow from 1–16 g/s across a wider tilt-angle range of 17°–50°.

In contrast, kettles known for versatility, such as the Brand B, often feature higher maximum flow rates, typically around 25 g/s, with classics like the Brand H even reaching more than 40 g/s. Higher flow rates usually mean flow changes more rapidly with small adjustments in tilt.

Our Flex spout underwent the same dozens of prototypes and thousands of trials, to achieve a smoother, more predictable increase in flow. Within a tilt-angle range of 25°–50°, the Flex spout delivers a nearly linear flow from 2–26 g/s, providing you with a wider range of expression and intuitive pouring control.

We created the chart below to help you clearly visualize how tilt angle affects flow rate of various mainstream kettles and ours. However, the curves only reflect one dimension of spout performance. Choosing a spout ultimately comes down to personal preference.

Palatti Enso comes with two spouts as standard, the Still and the Flex, allowing you to explore your own pouring style. You can also swap the seal with or without the built-in restrictor to unlock two additional flow profiles, enabling either a much faster flow rate of over 50 g/s or a slower, more controlled pour.

We're also developing additional spouts: an ultra high flow prototype (up to 100 g/s, ideal for cupping or more general use) and a customizable brass spout, so pros can tweak the shape and fine tune the tip of spout to match their own style. If you have more ideas, let us know.

Enso’s spout features a self-aligning design, fitting in vertically everytime. A simple quarter-turn is all it takes to lock the spout securely. The entire process takes less than one second.

All connectors are CNC-machined directly from 304 stainless steel, guaranteeing precise alignment and reliability. This simple and reliable assembly has successfully completed 10,000 high and low temperature cycles without any loss of seal integrity, far exceeding international safety requirements for electric kettles.

All silicone seals are made from high-quality, heat-resistant, food-grade silicone to ensure long-term durability. We include a few extras in the package for easy replacement, and additional parts will also be available at palatti.com and our retail channels. One seal option can be paired with the Still spout to function as a flow restrictor, while the other seal pairs with the Flex spout to allow unrestricted flow.

Flow quality matters as much as flow rate. There is a widely accepted theory by Jonathan Gagné about breakup length, that defines how far a continuous water column falls before surface tension splits it into droplets. A longer breakup length signals a steadier stream that stirs the coffee bed more effectively and gives brewers greater control.

Fine-tuning the shape alone is not enough. Manufacturing precision, especially the smoothness of the spout’s inner wall, is equally decisive. Our partner factory is China’s leading kettle manufactuerer. For almost thirty years, they have focused solely on kettles and now run a high automation level that is rare in the small appliances industry, which is the key to ensuring quality and consistency of the spouts. Thanks to this focus on details, our spouts achieve longer breakup lengths than mainstream pour over kettles.

Yes, the entire control knob is removable. It attaches magnetically to the tempered glass touchscreen for control. There are no hard-to-clean corners, and no battery inside. You can think of it as a rotating, passive stylus designed for seamless interaction.

Enso’s control knob is CNC-machined from solid stainless steel and built around a large high-precision bearing. It offers an incredibly smooth, stable spin, unlike any loose and flimsy plastic knobs you may be used to. It’s not just a control tool, but also a surprisingly satisfying fidget piece.

The temperature can be set anywhere from 40°C to 100°C (104°F to 212°F), adjustable to any specific degree within that range.

*The interchangeable spouts concept, locking mechanism, and magnetic control knob system are all patented.

Most electric kettles make it hard to tell whether they are heating, finished, or inactive, which often leaves users confused or causes safety risks. Enso solves this with a prominent yet gentle LED ring that clearly shows the kettle’s status, even from several meters away.

The LED ring is designed to indicate water temperature and working status. It changes automatically from light blue gradually to yellow and to red as the water heats up, and turns white when the kettle is lifted.

When heating, the light gently pulses. When holding temperature or idle, it stays solid.

The tempered glass touchscreen makes all information and interactions simple and intuitive. Even without watching the demo below, you'll quickly figure it out with just a few taps.

If you're in a high-altitude area, Enso will automatically enter High Altitude Mode once it detects the local boiling point. From then on, the target temperature will be limited to that maximum. To exit this mode, simply press and hold the mountain icon on the screen.

Enso is designed to be a pure and focused pour-over kettle. No app, no timers, no scheduling, and no settings to fuss with.

All kettles on the market rely on painted finishes. But paint naturally has limitations in durability. They inevitably scratch and peel over time.

Palatti Enso is the world’s first pour-over kettle finished entirely with PVD coating. Unlike traditional paint, PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) forms a dense, wear-resistant metallic layer that is deposited atom by atom in a vacuum environment. This metallic layer integrates with the surface of stainless steel body at the atomic level, creating a highly durable surface that never peels or chips.

PVD is typically reserved for industries with demanding performance standards, such as luxury watches, surgical instruments, high-end tableware and aerospace components. In the coffee world, it’s used to coat premium grinder burrs.

The kettle body is made of 304 stainless steel, and there is no PVD coating on the interior. The PVD process involves placing the entire component into a vacuum chamber, where ionized particles are deposited using arc discharge. Because of the spout's complex shape, it's not possible to use masking fixtures to shield certain areas while being coated inside the vaccum chamber, so the inner surface of the spouts is also coated with the PVD layer. 

That said, we want to emphasize that PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) coating is considered food-safe due to its non-toxic nature and the absence of harmful chemicals in the process. They are chemically and biologically inert to food safety standard. Our kettle will also go through FDA and other relevant food safety tests.

Beyond echoing the design language and the naming of Enso, the size, thickness, angle, and position of our handle are all based on force analysis and extensive prototyping and user testing.

Through visting and observing baristas, we found that the most physically demanding moment is not just maintaining the flow of water, but the quick lift at the end of the pour. When using kettles with poorly designed handles, baristas often experience strain from the base of the thumb to the upper wrist after extended use. This fatigue is caused by the constant force required from the thumb to maintain control, as well as the effort needed to lift and stop pouring.

Our design aims to minimize the effort required to balance the kettle during pouring and to reduce the need for active force from the thumb. Just like the proper grip in golf, the thumb should mainly help with direction rather than apply pressure.

The top-narrow, bottom-wide circular handle and the outward angle at the connection point bring the grip area closer to the kettle’s center of gravity. This shortens the resistance arm and extends the effort arm when lifting back.

At the same time, the ring-shaped design also helps prevent accidental contact with the hot kettle body, reducing the risk of burns during use.

Long-lasting design

We’ve spent over two years refining every detail of this kettle, aiming to create the best kettle on the planet for the next decade. In both design and technology, it goes far beyond anything currently available on the market. The kettle is primarily made from stainless steel with a PVD surface treatment that avoids invitable aging issues like paint peeling. The whole kettle and all major components have been tested far beyond industrial standard safety requirements. Throughout the design process, we intentionally reduced unnecessary complexity, leaving out unessential features like app connectivity. Instead, we focused on what truly matters to a kettle: lasting quality, performance, and simplicity.
