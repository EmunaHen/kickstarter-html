Story

Take your padel experience to the next level with the 153040.WIN The Advanced Scoreboard — the innovative solution designed to streamline scoring, enhance gameplay, and elevate your focus. Forget distracting score disputes and relying on memory to track points. This smart, net-mounted scoreboard puts you back in control with precision, portability, and convenience.

Whether you're a seasoned player or just getting into the game, the 153040.WIN Advanced Scoreboard helps you maximize your potential and keep your eyes firmly on the ball.

Its lightweight design ensures effortless portability, while the stylish and practical protective case means you can carry it with ease and confidence. Whether you're heading to your local padel court or traveling to a tournament.

Bright LED digits make it easy for every player to follow the match in real time. No more asking, “What’s the score?” — it’s right there for everyone.

Mounts directly to the padel court net with secure hooks. No need for walls or extra stands — just hook, switch on, and play. Compact and travel-ready.

How many times have you asked that? At the start of each new game, the Scorekeeper's blue light indicator clearly shows whose turn it is to serve. No more confusion, just smooth, uninterrupted play.

A dedicated button on the wristband lets you check the results of previous matches and then seamlessly return to the current game. Stay informed and focused without missing a beat.

With every point scored, simply press a button on the dedicated wristband to update the score. One player updates the score for everyone, ensuring a smooth and efficient game experience.

Your personal controller, always within reach.

The wristband command is securely stored in a built-in compartment on the scoreboard. The wristband bracelet is easily removable from the command and is machine washable, ensuring it stays clean and hygienic between uses. For optimal hygiene, we recommend keeping the wristband bracelet as a personal item.

The P Programming button, on the back of the scoreboard, allows you to select the type of Padel game, such as with or without Killer Point, and other modes.

The internal batteries are rechargeable via a USB-C port. Enjoy up to 8 hours of playtime when playing indoors, where the LED brightness is automatically minimized.

Starting from November 2024 we have involved some Beta Testers in Italy to try our advanced scoreboard during their Padel matches. Here is what they say:

It all started with a poorly played match. As often happens, I had signed up for this competitive match without knowing any of the players I would be playing with. After winning the first set, during the second set, there was a dispute over the score.

My partner and I were sure we were right, but due to the insistence of one of the opponents, we decided to let it go, for the sake of peace, and accepted what the opponents claimed. Unfortunately, we were truly convinced we were right and got nervous. From that moment on, we started playing poorly and lost the next two sets.

Once I got home, reflecting on what had happened, I told myself that this wouldn’t have happened if we had a scoreboard that could be updated remotely and visible to everyone.

So, I started browsing the Internet to find a device that did what I had in mind. I found several, but none met all my desires:

Well, disappointed by the search results, I decided to create what I needed myself. And here is the patents and the result: 153040.WIN

PROTOTYPE PHASE 0

In January 2024, I had a working device ready and took it to a first Padel match. It was a success! The device did exactly what I was looking for.

  • It was hung outside the field, near the goal.
  • It updated the score wirelessly with a wristband worn during the game.
  • It was visible by all players.
  • It told you who should serve at the start of each match.

People started asking where to buy the device, and I was sorry not to be able to say something.

PROTOTYPE PHASE 1

At that point, I started to consider producing the device, and I made a more industrialized prototype—Prototype #1.

It was still full of wires and manual cabling but not like the first one, which was handmade piece by piece.

PROTOTYPE PHASE 2 and 3

Then I needed to reduce the wiring and created Prototype #2.

It was also time to find a name for the device.
Since it displays points in a padel match (15, 30, 40, win), the most natural name was:
15 30 40 dot WIN — which is also the website address: 153040.win

At the same time, while using it, other needs emerged:

  • Different ways to count points with advantages
  • Choose between:
    – 2 advantages and a Golden Point
    – Indefinite advantages until one wins with two consecutive points

PROTOTYPE PHASE 4

So I built Prototype #4, improving memory and user programmability.

But the world is not only about hardware and software.
You also need mechanical design to hold everything, and mechanics has its constraints.

That’s why we built Prototype #5, which was used to build the first batch of devices for beta testers.
