Story

Introducing the Vividia G-810N BlueEye Grabber!

  Tired of losing small items in hard-to-reach areas? Meet the G-810N, the ultimate foreign object retrieval tool. This innovative device not only helps you fish out items from tight spaces with an integrated grabber claw, but also captures crystal-clear pictures and video to help navigate your retrieval. Whether it’s a ring that fell down your sink or a tiny screw in a dark corner, the G-810N combines precision and convenience into one sleek design. It also features a removable magnet ring, making the capture of metallic objects even quicker. Learn more about this innovative product below:

Applications 

  This product is, in essence, a semi-rigid probe equipped with a camera, grabber claw, and bright LEDs. It can also be known as a visual grabber, visual retrieval tool, camera assisted retrieval tool, visual pick up tool, or grabber borescope. This unique combination of features makes it suitable for retrieving objects from a variety of areas, such as:

  • Automobile, Aircraft, & Marine Engines
  • Vehicle Compartments
  • Drywall
  • Industrial Machinery
  • HVAC Systems
  • Plumbing & Piping
  • Electrical Panels
  • Telecommunications Equipment
  • Behind Furniture/Tight Spaces
  • And More!

About Oasis Scientific

Risks and challenges

In an ever-changing economic landscape, we can acknowledge the risk of potential supply chain issues. However, we have 15+ years of experience in sourcing, manufacturing, and delivering quality inspection equipment to customers. We do not see any potential risks for our project outside of general market risks any company in the inspection industry is exposed to. There were several challenges during the development process that we had to figure out ways to overcome. These included: -Getting the diameter of the probe small enough to fit in tight spaces -Building grabber claws with the strength to lift objects -Enabling product compatibility with iOS devices (iPhone & iPad) -Creating a quality, cost effective product Through careful planning, research, and trial and error, we now have a final working prototype and are ready to deliver quality units directly to the hands of consumers.
