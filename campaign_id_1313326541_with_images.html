Story

This project is about making a low cost Raspberry Pi compute module carrier that fits neatly into home or small business network racks. 

Rapid Analysis is an Australian company with a mission to launch a cheap, well documented platform for small business and personal clustered computing. But what, exactly, does that mean? It means you can run all the cool open source software out there for free on your own home or small business server. 

Who is this for?

Right now, we are launching our Xerxes Pi carrier board aimed at home lab enthusiasts and technical professionals looking for a low cost way to run their docker containers and other server based software. However, in the future, we plan to launch software that will make running your own home lab much simpler for less technical users.

Key Features

A lot of deep thinking went into the thermal design of the heatsinks, and their integration into the surrounding enclosure. The Raspberry Pi CM5 gets quite hot, and then when you add a Hailo M.2 AI accelerator or other peripheral, the heat really begins to stack. For this reason, we adopted a heavily ventilated design slanted at an angle where it would not need supports during 3D printing. We also found a 3D printable filament that could be used as a heatsink in low temperatures, which we found to be perfect for Raspberry Pis (see our videos section for more details). 

  • Modular Compatibility: Seamlessly integrates with multiple Raspberry Pi compatible Compute Modules, giving you unparalleled flexibility.
  • Ultra-Compact Design: At just 40mm x 120mm, its fractional Nano ITX form factor is perfect for tight spaces without compromising performance.
  • Enhanced I/O Options: Designed with diverse use cases in mind, offering robust connectivity for industrial, DIY, and home automation projects.

Whether you’re prototyping the next big tech innovation or leveling up your existing projects, this fractional Nano ITX powerhouse is here to redefine what’s possible with Raspberry Pi Compute Modules.

Technical Specifications

The size of the board is 120mm x 40mm, or one third the size of a Nano ITX board. This allows it to fit inside a 1U enclosure vertically. 

The back side of the board has pin headers for power, ground, I2C, and SPI connections. M.2 E Key and micro SD is also on the back side of the board. 

The Xerxes Pi has been tested using the Raspberry Pi CM4 & CM5, Banana Pi CM4 & CM5, Radxa CM5, Orange Pi CM4 & CM5.

Power Over Ethernet (POE)

POE is a technology that allows network cables to carry electrical power along with data. This means that the Xerxes Pi can receive both power and network connectivity through a single Ethernet cable, eliminating the need for separate power supplies and simplifying installation. POE is commonly used in commercial and industrial settings where centralised power management and flexible device placement are important.

We have decided to de-couple the POE chips from the main board and use a mountable "shield" board instead. We did this to 1) keep costs down, because some of these chips can be expensive and not everyone wants POE 2) allow for different power voltages, for example, a 14W and 30W version for higher power throughput, 3) expose POE, SPI, and I2C pins for future development of companion boards.

Additionally, POE can be provided via an ethernet POE splitter, for users who would prefer this configuration. 

Also, full specifications for the POE, SPI, and I2C pins will be available for engineers who want to develop their own companion boards or accessories.

Shipping, Tariffs, and Manufacturing

We have the capability to manufacture small volumes (less than 500 a month) of boards  here in Sydney, Australia and are currently working to get "Australian Made" certification. This will mean minimal tariffs for our USA customers. 

If we have a large volume order exceeding 500+ units we may manufacture those orders with an external manufacturing partner.
