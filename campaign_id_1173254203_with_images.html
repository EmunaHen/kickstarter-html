Story

Why Your Flat Keyboard is not ideal 

Most mechanical keyboards are manufactured flat because it’s cheaper. Flat rows force your fingers to reach up and down, over‑extending your knuckles and stressing tendons. After hours of work or gaming, that strain adds up, slower typing, sore fingers, even long‑term RSI.

MX Tilting Kit fixes that in seconds. Snap the adapters onto your existing MX‑style switches, pop your keycaps back on, and voilà: a smooth 15°/25° key‑well that brings every row closer to your fingertips.

Mix‑and‑match pieces to sculpt your perfect curve—or leave some rows flat for a custom feel.

        How we got here

Our mission has always been simple: make typing more enjoyable and healthier. After the community‑loved Choc Tilting Kit, we heard one request over and over—“Bring this to MX switches!” MX is still the most popular switch standard, yet ergonomic options are scarce. Guided by your feedback, countless prototypes, and thousands of test keystrokes, we’re now ready to launch MX Tilting Kit, stronger and sleeker. 

Yes, this is the third version of these tilter. We had released a first iteration in 2024, made with clear resin. While it was well received, the resin was too brittle for this application so we refunded people that had issues and pulled the product off the shelves. 
After months of refinements and testing, we finally are able to reliable use an expert resin (if you resin 3D print, we advise agains't trying it if you are a beginner). 

Old MX tilters

New Stronger MX Tilters
Now the tilters are 3d printed with a stronger engineering resin: Siraya Tech Blu Nylon Mecha. This tough engineering resin has nylon particles, ideal for this application that requires a tight friction fit. 

Scratch-Resistant Surface - Blu Nylon Mecha 3d print resin combines the toughness of Blu Nylon with the surface scratch resistance of Fast Mecha, making it ideal for mechanical parts with movement, such as joints, ensuring smooth operation without grinding/powdering.

        Handle with Care

 Even after reinforcing the design, our tilters are still resin‑printed; resin is inherently more brittle than injection‑molded plastic.


Installation Checklist
1- Test‑fit first. Try a single key to confirm your keycap stems and switch housings fall within normal MX tolerances and work with your tilters.
2- Go slow. Seat each tilter gradually and evenly. If you feel resistance, stop and realign, never force it.

Known Risk: Stem Fracture
During prototyping a handful of testers cracked MX stems inside their keycaps by pressing too hard. We’ve thickened stress points and optimized curing to reduce this, but a small risk remains until we move to injection molding.

Usage Guidelines
- Not rage‑proof. Excessive force or key‑slamming (looking at you, salty gamers) can fracture the parts.
- Travel smart. Remove the tilters before throwing your board into a bag or suitcase to avoid shock damage.

Installing the MX Tilting Kit is quick and easy. Just attach it to your compatible keyboard, and you're instantly set for a more personalized and comfortable typing experience. Whether you're an experienced mechanical keyboard enthusiast or new to customization, the  is designed to be intuitive and easy to set up, no technical expertise is required.

        Keyboard Layouts

This MX Tilting Kit will work on most keyboards. But we mainly recommend them for split keyboards and 60% or less. While it will work with larger keyboards such as 75% and up, we might not recommend it, since the added row of the function keycap. 
This tilter kit does not work with a vertical stabilizer. In other words, ISO Enter keycaps and Numpads are likely not to work in most cases.

We initially designed these tilters for split keyboards and then by popular demand we tried them on standard 60% and 75% keyboards. Therefore the function row is not ideal and for that reason we actually recommend this kit for smaller builds that do not have a function row. But still making that option available for those who want it is better than nothing. 

We did experimented with making an extra tall tilter for that function row but the wobble + the odd pressing angle made the feeling not ideal (the height would cause the tilter to lean so much and on some switches you could feel it scratching as you pressed down). Hence in the short term we think that it is best to stay with a kit that has 3 parts, it is the simpler compromise. 

        Keycap Profiles

These tilters are compatible with all MX keycap profiles, allowing you to experiment with your existing sets. Yes, OEM and Cherry profiles will work.  
That being said, the added tilt can cause some 'stepping' to occur between rows, for that reason, we recommend to use none-sculpted keycaps that have a large flat top. This will result in a smoother curve between row.  XDA  is an ideal profile for this, DSA is also a great option. 

They do not touch!
Since pictures where used, we have a perspective view instead of orthographic, therefore while it might appear that some keycaps are interfering (touching), this is not actually the case. All keycaps (yes, even FMX) are not touching and fully working.

        Switch Compatibility


Standard Cherry MX Switches
These tilters fit most standard Cherry MX-style switches. When it comes to BOX switches, some are compatible, but not all of them. When comparing BOX switches,even if they look the same, there are variances between brands, hence compatibility needs to be validated. 
Unfortunately, these will not work with most MX low-profile switches since most of them are tight-box switches. 
Shrinking our design to make them compatible with all switches would lead to thin tilters that wouldn't be as strong and sturdy, hence we made the compromise of making them strong at the cost of sacrificing some switch compatibility. 

BOX Switches
Tilters fit the following BOX switches, however be aware that for closed off box switches, adding the 1mm spacer is fine but removing it can be challenging at best and impossible at times.

Switch Wobble 
The tilters fit snugly onto the switches and into the keycaps. However, that added height creates a moment arm on the switch, accentuating switch wobble. This is a function of switches, therefore, your experience will vary depending on your switches. 

Click and Thock took the time to make an interesting wobble comparison: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OFmsC1j9t-E&t=288s  

        Stabilizers

When it comes to using this kit on stabilizer, it is as simple as adding one tilter per stem, hence 3 tilters for that 1 modifier keycap. 

       ISO Enter keycaps

Not recommended: Some looser stabilizers will allow you to make it work but in a lot of cases our titlers will not work with an ISO Enter keycap. 
To make it work (for this picture) we used one 15 degree tilter and applied it to the top stabilizer, while leaving the switch and second stabilizer stem bare. 

        Learning curve

While there is some getting used to required when you will install your tilters, since the keycaps don't really change location but rather it's the lowering of finger travel, it's very easy and fast to get used to this and start enjoying the benefits.

        Add-Ons

One drawback of this MX tilter kit is that it adds height to your keyboard. Using medium switches is a great way to counteract this drawback.

Tecsee MX medium switches have the same pinout as standard Cherry MX switches, making it compatible with most hot-swap boards. Yet they offer a similar height to MX low profile switches. This "medium profile switch" therefore allows you to convert a Standfard MX keyboard into a low profile keyboard. 
If you do end up using medium switches, then you will also need to use low profile keycaps.
