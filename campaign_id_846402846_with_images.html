Story

We believe robotics should be fun, accessible, and educational. That's why we created OpenWheely — a smart, AI-powered robotic car that gives you the tools to learn, experiment, and innovate with ease.

What started as a small project to teach embedded systems quickly evolved into a fully functional 4WD robotic platform — one that can drive upside down, follow sound and light, stream live video, and even operate in a swarm.

Combined with a powerful ESP32-based controller, OpenWheely is perfect for students, educators, developers, gamers, and anyone passionate about future tech. Whether you're into AI, automation, gaming, or simply building cool stuff, OpenWheely has something for you. 

OpenWheely is a 4-wheel-drive smart robot car powered by the ESP32-S3 Wroom-1, equipped with rich sensor input, wireless control options, and AI-ready processing. 

OpenWheely is designed with ultimate flexibility in mind — you’re not limited to just one way of control. Whether you're on your phone, using our custom-built joystick, working from your laptop, or even reaching for a TV remote, OpenWheely adapts to your environment and preferences. Use your mobile device as a remote! With our web-based or app-based control interface, you can steer the car, adjust speed, view the live camera feed, and even activate different modes like sound-follow or light detection — all from your pocket.

Take full command with the custom-built joystick controller. Featuring dual analog sticks, an onboard LCD for live video streaming, and programmable buttons, this is the most immersive way to control OpenWheely. It's not just a controller — it's a dev board, a gamepad, and a robotics dashboard in your hands.

For developers and tinkerers, OpenWheely is fully accessible via your computer. Use keyboard keys or custom scripts to send commands, visualize sensor data, or even train and deploy lightweight AI models directly to the car over Wi-Fi.

Sometimes, simple is powerful. With built-in IR receiver support, you can control OpenWheely using a regular TV remote — ideal for classrooms, workshops, or quick demonstrations. Assign basic commands like forward, stop, or mode switch to remote buttons and go!

OpenWheely keeps going no matter what. Thanks to its smart sensor logic and symmetrical 4WD design, it continues driving even when flipped upside down — perfect for rugged terrain and unexpected turns.

Using onboard IR and distance sensors, OpenWheely detects obstacles and intelligently avoids collisions. Great for autonomous navigation or demoing reactive AI behavior.

OpenWheely can track a light source or respond to sounds like a clap. This mode is perfect for interactive robotics projects or creative room lighting interactions.

With dual IR sensors on each side, OpenWheely detects table edges or drop zones with 360° coverage and stops safely before disaster strikes — ideal for desktop demos or elevated courses.

Classic line-following robotics, enhanced with precise 4-channel IR sensing. OpenWheely navigates black-and-white line paths with smooth, stable movement.

With built-in support for swarm behavior, OpenWheely units can communicate, coordinate, and move as a unified team. Program multiple cars to form patterns, respond to shared commands, or complete group objectives — all without a central controller.

Perfect for AI experiments, robotics education, and showcasing decentralized intelligence, Swarm Mode lets you explore collective behavior in a hands-on, visual way that’s both fun and future-ready.

Stream live video directly from OpenWheely’s onboard 2MP camera to the joystick controller’s screen — or your phone or laptop. It’s your own FPV (first-person view) robot experience, anytime, anywhere.

OpenWheely responds to sound cues like claps or snaps, making it easy to trigger actions without any physical contact. Start, stop, or switch modes with simple sounds — a fun and hands-free way to interact with your robot.
