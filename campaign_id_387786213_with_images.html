Story

We made you the most playable and interactive modulated fuzz pedal.¬†This is the most exciting 'toy' we've ever created. You'll find yourself playing for hours, exploring its special features that generate unique bleeps and bloops.

Fuzz Fields is an analog fuzz core with 3 modulation voices, all working magically together. Nothing is static here. The Fuzz is constantly in motion, causing it to scream, swell, saturate, and flutter. It is your playing dynamics that bring control of this futuristic device to your fingers.

Challenge

We deliver rich sound and innovative features. Traditional effect pedals rely on fixed knob settings to manipulate sound and often focus on single effects, limiting musical inspiration. We made fuzz interactive with your playing dynamics so you can focus on grooving and not on tweaking knobs. Most music effects are made for just one type of gear, like synths or guitar pedals. The connectivity between these two is usually limited, but not anymore. Fuzz Fields is a unique effect processor on your audio chain and an inspirational tool. ‚Ä¢ The Oscillation

Fuzz Fields is packed with an internal oscillator that can be accessed by the FREQ footswitch and make the pedal scream, squeal and growl. Turn the GATE knob clockwise to tame the oscillating harmonics and create a gated octaver fuzz with muscles. The high-low gain switch will lead the octaver to lower or higher octaves. Also in this mode, fuzz is at maximum and the FUZZ knob transforms into a FREQUENCY dial affecting the oscillation pitch.
¬†

‚Ä¢ The Dynamic Modulation

Fuzz Fields is loaded with a phase-shifter circuit that can modulate your signal and is enabled by the PHASER footswitch. Turn it on and choose between a phaser or vibrato like effect by the on-board toggle. What's unique about this feature is that the intensity of the modulation can be controlled by the dynamics of your playing, utilizing an envelope follower circuit, the sensitivity of which can be adjusted by the SENSE knob. The modulation can be further modulated by an expression pedal or external CV or whatever you may desire, within reason... or not!¬†
¬†

‚Ä¢ The Gravity Mode

Fuzz Fields' massively dense core produces a gravitational field that can be enabled by the GRAVITY footswitch. This field is exerted by your dynamics to the gain of the fuzz, pulling it down the harder you play depending on SENSE threshold, creating an effect we describe as 'gain tremolo'. But that's not the end of it.¬†

By turning both FREQUENCY and GRAVITY modes on, your dynamics affect the oscillation pitch, creating a bombs-falling-from-the-sky or a ferocious-screaming-parrot-with-murderous-intents like effect. With the use of an expression pedal, you can create downward/upward swells and tune the pitch by foot, while with an external CV signal you can modulate the oscillator's pitch and simulate a firetruck siren, robotic speech or hyper-galactic drone noises.¬†


Furthermore, by turning FREQUENCY & GRAVITY & PHASER modes on, while plugging in an external CV and an expression pedal, you can open a cosmic portal to unknown dimensions and jam with extraterrestrial beings from beyond.

Thus, the birthing of the bi-function switch. A robust microcontroller+relay+firmware switching system that ensures the smooth operation of the pedal.

Crash - The Predecessor

It all began with the Crash pedal. A simple yet very playful oscillated fuzz we sold quite a few of, between 2015 and 2022. Crash suffered one worrying issue though. No one could really assert dominance over the uncontrollable screaming oscillations and the fluffy slugs upon this volcanic pedal.

¬† ¬† ¬† ¬† ¬† Crash was featured in the "MEET: a friendly introduction" series by Knobs

The Development Process

‚Ä¢ The Electronics: To address its playability issue, we started in 2023 to play around with Crash's inner circuitry. We managed to tame the oscillations and not only control it but also take advantage of the CV and expression inputs to use the Fuzz Fields (ex. Crash) in more creative ways and produce interesting sound synthesis. We added modulation features on strategically selected parts of the circuits that transforms this fuzz core into a Field of Effects. You can now control it and create your really personal sounds.

‚Ä¢ First Cardboard Prototype: As our electronics design expanded and consumed more space on the breadboard, we were able to make clearer design decisions. We then created our earliest interface prototype using cardboard, with all the cables positioned next to the breadboard. That was also our proof of concept, as it bore almost all the features we had imagined!

‚Ä¢ Interface Design: We designed everything ourselves using KiCad software. The PCB interface is made from 1.6mm thick PCB material, which is sturdy enough to step on without breaking. Another advantage of using PCB material for the user interface is that illuminated elements can shine under and through the resin of the PCB. The gold elements also add a nice aesthetic touch.

‚Ä¢ Printed Circuit Board Design: ¬†The PCB is designed for assembly on an SMT (Surface Mount Technology) line, which reduces errors, lowers costs, and maintains consistent quality. Due to the high number of components, it would be nearly impossible to keep the design compact using only through-hole components.

‚Ä¢ Sheet Metal Enclosure:We have designed a sheet metal enclosure made of 1.5mm aluminium. It's both lightweight and strong, so it doesn't add unnecessary weight to your pedalboard while remaining durable enough that you don't have to worry about it getting damaged.

‚Ä¢ Final 3D Models: We tested the tolerances between the electronics board and the metal enclosure using 3D models to identify any discrepancies before moving to Design for Manufacturing (DFM).

‚Ä¢ First Batch of Prototypes: On our first batch of prototypes, called REV.A1, we discovered and fixed a few mistakes. After debugging these issues, we successfully turned the device into a fully functional effect pedal, exactly as we had imagined it.

Fuzz Fields prototype is in fully operational state and in our hands! However, some design changes had to be implemented to accompany the latest updates in the circuitry control and user interface on the REV.A2.

‚Ä¢ HOLD - GRAVITY Footswitch: the now 'GRAVITY' footswitch was originally purposed to engage the external CV, if one was inserted. It was named 'HOLD'... not anymore.

‚Ä¢ PHASER - FAZER Footswitch: 'PHASER' is now called 'FAZER' because of the bleep bloop and pew pew. Basically, the switch activates the phase shifter circuit which can sound like a phaser or vibrato, but a CV or expression pedal can make it sound differently. It can sound like a phaser but it ain't strictly one.

‚Ä¢ DIP Switch Print: graphic notations were added for the DIP switches.¬†

‚Ä¢ 'HOLD IN' is now called 'CV IN' cause that's what it is.
