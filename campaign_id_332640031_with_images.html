Story

Praxitron, with its advanced dual-head design, is the next evolutionary step in additive manufacturing. Engineered for superior performance, it delivers unparalleled speed, precision, and versatility, significantly outpacing current single-head 3D printers.

The IDEX, independent dual extruder, design enables simultaneous printing with multiple materials or colors, which streamlines complex projects and reduces production times. Praxitron will empower makers with efficiency and flexibility they’ve never experienced before, setting a new standard in the 3D printing industry.

Stronger Structural Integrity

One of the most overlooked advantages of printing with dual extruders is creating stronger overall prints. For example, by using a thinner nozzle (0.4mm) to print walls and a thicker nozzle (0.8mm) to print infills, you can maintain the details of a finer print and the strength of a courser print. The enhanced structural integrity to your prints will allow you to construct truly functional creations. 

Complex Design

Praxitron’s dual extrusion allows you to print intricate designs that may require varying properties, like heat resistance, durability, or flexibility, in different parts of the same object.

Combining two materials also allows you to make functional parts, such as printing with a flexible filament for certain areas and rigid material for others or you can print a a single object with different textures, such as rubber grips on a plastic handle.

Streamlined Support Structures

Praxitron helps you streamline support structures for all your prints, by printing with a combination of materials, such as a strong structural material, like PLA or ABS, and a support material like PVA or HIPS, which dissolves in water or limonene. The ability to print easily removable supports make creating complex geometries and overhangs extremely easy.

You can create visually striking multi-colored projects with Praxitron, as it supports different-colored filaments. There is no need for manual filament swaps during printing.

95% Waste Less: Time & Materials

With two print heads, the Praxitron can switch seamlessly between materials or colors without pausing for manual intervention, speeding up the process significantly. And at the same time, you won’t waste materials like traditional automatic material systems.

Praxitron’s single-piece chassis enhances sturdiness, reducing vibrations for higher print accuracy. It simplifies maintenance and there is no assembly required, making it super user-friendly. The compact design even improves portability, reliability, and calibration precision.

Increased Stability & Precision

The one-piece chassis design boosts structural integrity and minimizes vibrations during printing, significantly reducing mechanical stress. This enhances Praxitron's durability and extends its lifespan. The custom designed belt also enhances precision of every print. 

Praxitron comes as is without any set-up needed, there's no assembly required. Just remove it from the box, plug it in (don't forget the filaments), and you're ready to go. 

Auto Bed Leveling

Praxitron's Auto Bed Leveling feature, powered by piezo sensors, improves print reliability by automatically adjusting for any unevenness on the print bed. It ensures a consistent first layer, which is key to preventing print failures and improving overall quality. Plus, it removes the hassle of manual calibration, making it a truly plug-and-print machine. 

The Built-in camera lets you monitor your prints in real time, even when you're away from the printer. 

The filament feed box features filament detection, which helps with smooth filament feeding and also ensures uninterrupted printing. It monitors filament flow and prevents issues, such as clogging, before they can affect your prints. 

Praxitron is designed for easy routine maintenance. The extruders are easy to remove and replace in whenever you need to change nozzle sizes or refill filaments.

Long-lasting design

Reusability and recyclability

Conventional multi-material and multi-color printers relying on tool change and material change systems waste too much material while performing the changing action. Praxitron, with its two print heads, uses minimal materials when alternating print heads, keeping waste out of landfills.
