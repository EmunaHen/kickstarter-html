Story

THRASH
The Ultimate Travel Tool

A tool created for traveling. I wanted a tool that I could bring on my bike anywhere. Often only the first few miles of a trail is properly maintained, tools get awkward and heavy. No one wants to carry a shovel or rake for miles when they're not even sure if they will need them.

Meet the THRASH. It is a full-size shovel, rake and axe. It is twice as large as a folding army shovel but still fits in your hydration pack. It can be mounted to your 4x4, thrown under your car seat, or stored in a drawer. I designed this tool to do it all. But I need a handle that can do it all too.  The next step is to make a handle that is strong and flexible. The best choice for this is aluminum extrusion. Wood and fiberglass handles both require complicated adapters to prevent fracturing. Fiberglass handles even have a wooden core at the high stress sections for reinforcement. An aluminum handle is perfect for a compact shovel but a new tool die is expensive. This tool is based on the 3/4" ACME American handle threaded connector, so any hardware store handle can attach in an emergency or retro-fit.

     Details:

     ⦿    Dimensions: 17" x 8.5" 
     ⦿    Blade: Heat hardened 14 gauge steel
     ⦿    Connectors: Steel, 3/4" ACME Thread
     ⦿    Handle Aerospace Aluminum

ABOUT ME

I made all the prototypes for this project myself, it required a lot of bandsaw cutting and angle grinding. I will get the production plates water jet cut and spine CNC machined. I still want to have hands on time making these production quality tools. I am super excited to set up an assembly line and everything for a brand new product.
I am a mechanical engineer and have been working in the sporting goods industry for ten years. I love bikes and making my own stuff. I grew up in Medford Oregon then went to college in Klamath Falls, about 2 hours away. In college I was president of the Mars Reach Space Club, where we made a freeze dryer and high-altitude balloon for fun. To graduate everyone had to be part of a year long project, my team built a carbon fiber - composite drift boat for $4000. I moved to Los Angles for a bit where I got a job in a hot tub factory and made an electric bike to commute the 10 miles to work, this was back in 2015 when e-bikes were rare and expensive. At that time I had a "real" YouTube channel for my projects and it was pretty great.
That e-bike got me my dream job at a premium bike company, Rolf Prima, when they were just setting up a new factory. I learned everything about making wheels. I started by designing the production line and getting hands on experience rolling and machining aluminum rims. Then I moved into testing designs and finally designing my own rims. Rims start out as straight aluminum extrusion and a good design has to be flexible and strong just like a shovel handle. 

Risks and challenges

The main risk is getting the extrusion in time for next spring. I need to have new dies made and the extrusion scheduling and shipping process can take 8 months. If I meet the project goal quick I am going to order the extrusion before the kick starter is even done. The second large risk is the Axe blade. It is working great on 2" staubs and dead branches but I need to get a production quality tool made before I can see if it works for cutting firewood. Lastly, the threaded connections needs to be super robust. I applied for a patent that has several locking mechanism options to make sure the handle doesn't un-tighten while in use. I am going to have to be super careful with testing to make sure the handle connection is perfect.
