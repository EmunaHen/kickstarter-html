Risks and challenges

Bringing our innovative cycling helmet to market comes with some challenges, but we have a strong plan to mitigate them: - Product functionality: We've designed wÿnd to use standard components and longstanding technologies to avoid inventing something that is beyond what off-the-shelf parts can provide. The helmet part of the helmet has been conservatively designed by our partners in China to pass certification with ease. The electronics leverage circuit and components used in dynamo-torches with reduced LED brightness, still suitable for night-riding, to hit our goal of 1:60 generation-time:usage-time. Whilst we've done the maths and tested the parts in isolation, there is a risk that putting it all together at scale will be more complicated then anticipated. - Manufacturing & Supply Chain: We have extensive experience working with manufacturers in China, having visited multiple times and collaborated closely on past projects. Our helmet factory produces for leading brands like POC & Trek, ensuring top-tier quality and safety. The electronics factory is ready to produce using industry-standard components, similar to widely used hand-crank dynamo torches. - Certification & Compliance: Our founders have strong connections with certification bodies and already run a software company that supports certification testers. Working alongside our experienced manufacturer, we know what it takes to meet safety standards. The helmet design is a conservative commuter style, not pushing certification limits, making compliance more straightforward. - Durability & User Experience: The hand-crank system is based on proven dynamo torch designs but reinforced for frequent use. Components are designed to detach before breaking if hit hard, improving durability. Extensive real-world testing will refine the design for longevity and ease of use. - Production & Delivery Timeline: We’ve built in 50% timeline redundancy to account for unexpected delays. While logistics can always pose risks, our experience and existing supplier relationships help minimise them. - Market Adoption & Education: As a new type of helmet, some user education may be needed. We will provide clear instructional materials and marketing to highlight the benefits of the built-in generator. We’re confident that our experience, strong partnerships, and well-thought-out risk management will ensure a smooth path to delivering a high-quality product to our backers.

Long-lasting design

The rechargeable batteries in wÿnd are nickel–metal hydride that offer up to 2000 charge cycles, allowing each wÿnd light to be used for years. At the end of life, only the lights need be replaced and are sold separately. All moving parts are mechanically attached and will be sold separately as spares so can be replaced by the user over purchasing a new helmet entirely should it break.
