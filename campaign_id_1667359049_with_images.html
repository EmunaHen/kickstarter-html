The All-in-One Invention Lab: Meet InnoBoard

InnoBoard is the ultimate STEAM education platform that lets kids and creators build, code, and control anything—from AI-powered robots to smart farms and voice assistants. Whether you're a teacher, parent, student, or tech tinkerer, InnoBoard brings invention to your fingertips Code with drag-and-drop. Build AI projects. Automate the world With InnoBoard, the only limit is your imagination.

Why InnoBoard?

Learning tech shouldn’t require expensive tools or expert-level skills. But in most classrooms, access to Robotics, AI, and IoT is fragmented, complicated, and expensive That’s where InnoBoard comes in.

One device. 100+ ideas.

With its built-in screen, sensors, motors, and WiFi + AI support, InnoBoard lets you go from idea to invention in minutes—without soldering or complicated setup.

Learn by doing

Our low-code platform, InnoBoard IDE, lets anyone start coding via blocks—and upgrade to Python or Arduino anytime.

You can also build machine learning models visually using AI Playground, our no-code tool that supports object detection, voice commands, and more.

“How I Code?” — Just Drag and Drop!

InnoBoard’s coding interface is designed to make programming accessible for all ages. Whether you're a student, teacher, or total beginner, you can start coding right away with a visual, block-based system—no prior experience needed.

  • Easily connect to WiFi
  • Read sensor data like temperature in seconds
  • Transition to full Arduino or Python coding anytime

Perfect for classrooms, workshops, and individual learners who want to turn ideas into interactive tech projects—fast.

“Playground” — Build AI Visually

The InnoBoard Playground is a drag-and-drop AI creation tool that lets users train machine learning models and connect IoT devices with zero code.

  • In just a few clicks, learners can:
  • Upload data (like images or voice)
  • Choose an AI model (e.g. YOLO for object detection)
  • Build logical flows and see real-time results

Ideal for teaching AI, computer vision, and edge intelligence in a way that’s simple, hands-on, and classroom-ready.

Risks and challenges

Risks and Challenges Like any hardware and education-based project, InnoBoard faces several risks, particularly in budgeting, manufacturing, and timelines. Production Delays: We are working with trusted manufacturers in Thailand, but unexpected issues in sourcing components or logistics may cause delays. We have built in time buffers to minimize impact and will communicate transparently if delays arise. Shipping and Logistics: International shipping from Thailand may face customs or regional delays. We are working with experienced partners and have researched shipping rates and methods to provide the best options for our backers. Software Development: Our IDE and AI Playground platforms are under active development. While basic versions are already tested and usable, advanced features are in ongoing refinement. We are committed to releasing updates regularly and providing excellent support. Educational Content Readiness: We aim to provide engaging course materials and teacher guides. Some of this content may evolve based on feedback or translation progress, especially for international users. Scale of Demand: If this campaign exceeds expectations, we may face fulfillment challenges. However, we have a scalable plan in place, including flexible production and team expansion options. We are passionate educators and developers, and this is not our first time building learning tools. We're confident in our ability to deliver, and we will keep our backers informed every step of the way.

Use of AI

I plan to use AI-generated content in my project.

What parts of your project will use AI generated content? Please be as specific as possible.

Our project includes a drag-and-drop AI creation tool called InnoBoard Playground, where users can train and deploy simple AI models like object detection and voice classification. These models are used for educational purposes and are generated through the platform’s built-in AI functions. Additionally, text-to-speech (TTS) and image classification outputs are generated as part of the learning kits and demos.

Do you have the consent of owners of the works that were (or will be) used to produce the AI generated portion of your projects? Please explain.

Yes. All AI-generated content in our project is produced using tools and datasets that are either open-source, publicly licensed for educational use, or developed in-house. For example, our AI Playground utilizes pre-trained models and datasets that are publicly available under open licenses, and we do not use any proprietary third-party content without consent. We also educate users on ethical AI usage and dataset transparency as part of our curriculum.
