Story

TheBinaryClock is here!

A minimalist, modern clock that turns time into glowing binary - made to captivate tech lovers and creative minds alike.

Our concept:

Made specifically for those who want more than just the time: TheBinaryClock is tech that makes a statement and illuminates the room. Logic meets elegance in a blend of innovation and aesthetics  -made for tech nerds, creatives, and design lovers who want their space to shine.

Say goodbye to analog and digital clocks that are just the same thing over and over - and to binary clocks that fall flat: TheBinaryClock stands out with a unique design, precise craftsmanship and attention to detail.

A new layout

Three horizontal rows show hours, minutes and seconds. Glowing circles display each as a binary number - simple and functional.


You’ll find a step-by-step guide on our website that shows you how to read the clock - plus an easy intro to the world of binary: https://fedanilabs.de/anleitung

But there´s more:

TheBinaryClock is nerdy-at-heart, stylish in design, and built to last. At 40x25x3.6 cm, it’s a bold statement, whether on your wall or the desk.

With every detail refined again and again by people who are simply never satisfied, it has become a design piece with a serious wow factor:

  • Birchwood casing, built to golden ratio proportions
  • Matte acrylic glass - elegant when off, radiant when lit
  • RGB LEDs with customizable color and brightness
  • Hackable: Arduino Nano with USB-C and open-source code
  • 24h time format, always accurate thanks to a battery-backed RTC module
  • Infrared remote for easy adjustments and custom looks
  • Wall-mounted or freestanding 

Skilled craftsmanship meets smart technology - for a result that truly stands out.

Our path:

Design & Origins
We’re Felix, David, and Nico - tinkerers and friends since 5th grade, passionate about inventing and building. In spring 2024, the Mainz math museum “Ich mach Mathe!” asked us to create a binary clock that vibrantly visualizes the binary system. Our first version, though still rough under the hood, was already a visual magnet - and the feedback we got after it was up was overwhelming. That’s when the idea for TheBinaryClock was born.

The original "V1" Version as it is still on display today in Mainz.

From prototype to product
We refined the design, explored industrial production, sourced suppliers, and tested prototypes. David handled laser-cutting, sanding, and all things wood. Felix built and tested our electronics, while Nico took care of the legal side.

We couldn´t have imagined how many different skills it would take to bring such a product into the world.

Almost a year and a half later, we finally handed out a few clocks to test users, where they were met with enthusiasm and ran flawlessly. Seeing that made us genuinely proud, because it meant: TheBinaryClock is finally ready to be in your hands.

TheBinaryClock makes an impression—whether in a makerspace, office, classroom, or at home.
