Story

Introducing the Gamepad Quest Kit a portable electronics and coding adventure where you don’t just play the game…

What's in the Kit?

The Gamepad Quest Kit is a hands-on educational kit that teaches electronics, coding, and hardware design by guiding you through the ultimate quest: building your own portable video game console from scratch.

It’s not just an electronics kit

It’s a game inspired learning adventure filled with the magic of electronics. 

All kits come with digital instructions, video training, code, and additional supporting files. Depending on your reward level, you can also get all the parts you need, printed versions of our training documents, custom PCB board, tools, poster, and achievement stickers, and controller grips.

We believe the best way to learn electronics is by building something awesome. We want to build upon game design principles to teach learners how to build their own electronics. Whether you're a total beginner or an experienced maker, this kit guides you step by step; teaching essential soldering skills, electrical concepts, and how circuits work! 

A lot of kits stop there, why not? right? Isn’t that enough? No way! Once you build your gamepad, we teach you about coding; even how to code a simple video game! 

How does it work? 

Main Quest

During your electronics adventure you will get to build your own portable video game controller - this is the main quest. However, there is so much more to do with your Gamepad! It is entirely up to you on how much you want to learn and build. You can choose to jump straight to the main quest or go for 100% completion and show off your achievements! 

The main quest of this adventure is to build the console on a our custom PCB board. 

For the main quest and each of the side quests you get to follow our illustrated tutorial instructions and video tutorials to learn how to put it all together and tips and tricks along the way. 

We currently have 7 different games we have adapted to the GamePad and are looking to add more! Please note: we are not developing the games, those are given credit in our github page. Our current list can be seen in this demo video 

Play!

Once you build your portable console you can then play games based on the Atmega 32 architecture. Many of these games are modified versions created by the Arduino community, and you can also learn to create your own game!

But that is just one small part of the adventure! In electronics lore, you learn about each component. In sidequests, you learn how to build control circuits and play music. And once you build your controller, we teach you the basics of coding your own game, such as how to control a pixel on a screen and how to create a timer. The kit is designed to be - Open World - all quests and lore are optional, but certain side quests and lore help you understand and prepare you for the main quest, and you are also free to go straight to the end.

Build the game console then build the game! 

Learn how to build simple video games in code. We start you off by learning the basics such as controlling a pixel on a screen

Then we teach you how to code the in game timer, states, adding levels, and more!

Explore Electronics  

The kit, its organization and structure is all based off video game design. For example, our instructions are based on open world game design principles. 

Side Quests

Are optional electronics activities. Some are designed to be completed before the main quest and others must be completed after the main quest. Learn electronics piece-by-piece (buttons, displays, LEDs, microcontrollers). There will be a lot of optional side quests you can go on as you use the kit. Examples of side quests that you can do as a part of your kit include:  Build an LED circuit, Discharge a capacitor, Build a 555 timer circuit, Build a stopwatch, Control a pixel on a screen. And we are developing more and more! There are different types of side quests:

  • Tutorial - learn the basics with a tutorial quest
  • Level up - a more challenging application of skills learned

Build on the skills learned in tutorial quests with more challenging level up quests!

  • Modding - a technical challenge that requires multiple skills

Put multiple skills together to modify the hardware and software of the GamePad. For example turn your GamePad into a music player!

or learn how to design your own GamePad printed circuit board

Electronics Lore

Is where you learn what components do, how they work, and tips for how to use them. You can dive in and learn as much or as little of the lore as you want! Don’t want to learn about the lore of the components. That’s fine – just like in a video game, you can skip the lore and go to what you find interesting.

Fulfillments, Shipments and Taxes

Packaging: To ensure a smooth fulfillment, our team will start final product packaging as soon as all elements are ready. Contents will be sealed to protect them from scratching and moisture; the kits will be lined inside the box to ensure they stay secure and undamaged during shipping. The shipment mailer or box will also be sealed for a further layer of protection. 

Typical shipping time:

Shipment may take up to 4 weeks depending on the distance and customs clearance speed of your country (but most of the backers should expect less than this time). Due to the volume of work and limitations of the Kickstarter platform, we usually DO NOT explicitly attach the tracking order for everyone, to save a bit of our labor (a small team with a limited workforce that must concentrate on product development), but if you are seeing an exceptionally long delay, feel free to send us a message and we will look into your reward. 

For European backers concerning VAT: Since Kickstarter does not handle VAT charges, EU backers may incur VAT on their orders. We will declare a reasonable value on shipments to help minimize unnecessary costs.

Risks and challenges

The biggest challenge is not getting the word out! I have already developed the kit, tested with multiple people, now I just need to keep perfecting it. There is some uncertainty with global tariffs and trade - we have pre-purchased most of the electronics items needed for the kit. But the printed items may still see impacts from tariffs, most of our print suppliers are from the US, however as their costs for raw materials go up they may pass those on to others. We do not anticipate issues but there are always risks. This may delay printing or delivery of some kits materials.
