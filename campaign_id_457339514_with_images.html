Risks and challenges

• Solo Founder Reality — I design, code, and handle support myself. Plan: weekly build logs; GPT-auto-drafted replies; hire a Fiverr VA (10 h/week) once we top 500 subscribers. • Sudden Traffic Spikes — A viral TikTok could dump 50 k users overnight. Plan: Vercel + Cloudflare auto-scale; 95 % of content pre-cached; load-tested at 20× funding goal. • AI Cost & Latency — Live GPT calls can be pricey or stall. Plan: pre-generate daily wisdom; cache premium replies; fallback to a lighter model if OpenAI hiccups. • Retention After the First Wow — One cool reading doesn’t guarantee a subscription. Plan: daily-echo loop, streak badge, and monthly “Moon-Code” perks ship within 30 days. • Timeline Drift — Feature creep kills Kickstarters. Plan: hard milestones—Alpha (30 days), Garden Chat (60), Mobile PWA (90). If I slip, backers see it in the public log and get immediate fixes. If I miss a deadline: you still access the pre-generated well of wisdom while I patch the leak—no shipping delays, no customs excuses.

What parts of your project will use AI generated content? Please be as specific as possible.

Where AI is used Visuals – All concept art, background images, and the reward-tier “Lantern Path” graphic were created with Midjourney (v6). Demo text & captions – Drafted with OpenAI GPT-4o, then copy-edited by me. In-product engine – The live website delivers real-time “Lantern wisdom” via the OpenAI GPT-4o API; I cache and curate final replies. Where humans take over Game & UX design, website code, map logic, and database caching – 100 % hand-written by me (TypeScript / React / Supabase). Final copy polish, video editing, voice-over, and soundtrack – human-crafted. Symbol deck & elemental glyphs – sketched, vectorized, and colour-graded manually after AI concept passes.

Do you have the consent of owners of the works that were (or will be) used to produce the AI generated portion of your projects? Please explain.

Image Generation (Midjourney v6, Pro plan) Input prompts: I only use original text prompts or references that are either (a) my own photographs from temple trips or (b) public-domain imagery (pre-1928 scroll textures, Qing-era lantern illustrations). License: My Midjourney Pro subscription grants full commercial rights to all generated outputs, per their Terms of Service (§ 4 Commercial Rights). No direct style cloning: I don’t name living artists or copyrighted media in prompts, so the outputs aren’t derivative of anyone’s protected work. Text Generation (OpenAI GPT-4o) Inputs: I supply project-specific prompts; no third-party copyrighted text is fed in. Outputs: Under OpenAI Terms, I own the content produced. I review and edit every line before publishing.
