Story

Fathomdeep offers a world first opportunity: to send personal or research objects on a round-trip descent to 10,000 meters (33,000 feet or 6 miles) to the bottom of the Mariana Trench beneath the surface of the Pacific Ocean and back.

Learn more about the mission, project, and press inquiries at¬†fathomdeep.co

Launching from Guam in late 2025, this expedition utilizes a modular descent locker system, which is essentially a repurposed shark cage outfitted with individual containers for your items. Each object will experience its own interaction with the deep, ranging from raw seawater immersion to a controlled atmospheric pressure environment.

This is not tourism. This is not mining. This is contactless passage to ocean depths, powered by marine engineering and a conscious reverence for the natural deep.

How the Descent Works

Technical Specifications


This project offers backers an accessible method to transport objects of their choosing from their location to 6 miles under the Mariana Trench 33,000ft or 10,000M down - and return shipping back from there.¬†

You are reading this correctly.¬†

This is a round trip descent and ascent of your objects. Individual items are organized by volume and will be secured in place using a system of smaller lockers secured into a larger frame called the descent locker. This arrangement allows us to arrange items as needed to deliver a target buoyancy to achieve the necessary tension to maintain our target descent speed range.¬†

Different objects will be handled differently by type. There are four key categorizations that are your choice as part of the expedition:

Raw immersion - these are items that will experience full pressure and interaction with sea water. They would not dissipate and filter outside of the locker or mesh bags under pressure. Examples: foam cups, surfboards, watches, jewelry, clothing, leather

Pressurized contact - these objects will be sealed in a seawater containing bladder. They will be exposed to the full pressure of the voyage, but the objects themselves will not make direct seawater contact with the water available at 10,000M depth. Examples: rice, lemons, camera film, soda can, tea, coffee, rum. These can be prepared in seawater in sealed formats to force high pressure seawater exposure, but will not be directly exposed to the sub sea water column.

Isolated descent - these are objects that will remain at constant 1 atm pressure throughout the entirety of the descent using a spherical pressure chamber. Examples: watches that are going down exclusively as mementos or delicate sensors that require deep-sea isolation placement to function properly and would otherwise be destroyed by the pressure of the depths. The specific cost of depth-only drops will need to be customized to your item particulars, so please reach out if this format is of interest.

Cremated remains - our mission is to respect the deep by leaving no trace, but cremated remains are the sole exception to this. These ashes are primarily calcium phosphates with trace other elements that do not contain organic matter which could disrupt benthic ecosystems through microbial activity. Cremated remains are screened to be dry, granular, and chemically inert such that they do not decompose further or introduce toxins into marine environments. To minimize radius further, the remains will be released with a specialized urn designed to release contents at 10,000M depth. These contents cannot contain objects other than ash and require specific permitting which we will arrange as part of this offering.¬†

Where

The specific target of this expedition will be the Sirena Deep located at 12¬∞3.924‚Ä≤N 144¬∞34.868‚Ä≤E, which has a maximum depth of 35,151ft or 10,714M. When


This expedition is targeted to run to completion by the end of October 2025 across multiple days until all descents are completed. The weather across the Mariana Trench is often very challenging for expeditions. As with everything in the open ocean, weather conditions will dictate reality. Typhoon season runs June to November, but weather in the trench region becomes more challenging by October - hence why the expedition is slated to be completed by that time.¬†

Travel to the Sirena Deep from Guam will take 2-4 hours under clear conditions.

Once there, each descent is expected to take 2-4 hours down, hold 1 hour at depth, then take 2-4 hours for the return ascent. This means that each descent takes one day in real terms. The expedition will continue descents daily while weather permits with a potential need to return to port if weather conditions become a risk.¬†

Risks and challenges

This project has been mapped out by a marine and mechanical engineer team and will be captained by an experienced boat captain sailed from Guam. We have minimized avoidable risks without over-indexing on investments before demand is successfully proven out. That's where Kickstarter comes in. If this project is funded, it means there is more than sufficient interest in universalizing access to the bottom of the ocean with a substantial margin of safety. Core risks: 1. Weather. This is both a risk for timing and a risk for realization. Safety of crew and cargo is paramount in this expedition and will be the decisive factor in all questions throughout the project. Typical challenging weather has been built into the project schedule but will absolutely delay the project if this becomes atypical. We will post regular updates if challenges are known or encountered. 2. The Descent Locker will be descending blind using bathymetry maps in a known location. The Sirena Deep is kilometers wide, which allows plenty of range but no adjustments are possible throughout the descent / ascent period. 3. Tangled line. The winch design for this application is not complex nor unknown, but requires testing and reliable operation. All 6 miles of line will be extended under tension, but ocean currents and twist in the line can compound coiling risks. This is a risk that is expected and can be managed through proper winch design. 4. Item loss. This project is operating in profoundly new territory, which presents a real possibility of total item loss. Items of great sentimental value are not recommended to be sent in the first iteration of descents in 2025 unless you are personally accompanying the voyage and are comfortable with the risk real time. Risk will be staged by performing test descents before performing the primary descents of the project. 5. Marine Cargo Insurance. Loss and damage to items is a meaningful risk and not possible to accurately account for in a blanket format for all backer offerings. Sub-sea transport is not included in standard marine cargo insurance, but is available at cost for items of value. While we manage for all known risks, this will need to be accounted for on a case by case basis until insurance carriers have an understanding of project operating risk level. Peripheral managed risks: 1. The Sirena deep portion of the Mariana Trench lies in US Marine Monument waters. This is a good thing for clarity of permitting authority, which allows the project to operate in the water column of a US Marine Monument. Additional permitting is in process, but still needed for release of cremated remains. 2. Physical reality. This descent strategy has not yet been executed nor implemented. While we have outlined and created a resolution plan for all known challenges, there are always unexpected issues that arise from the physical world. The Sirena Deep has been visited one time by a single human in all of known time. Its bathymetry has been mapped thanks to this expedition and it is known to be a particularly favorable destination for this project. 3. Weather. Typhoon season intersects with good weather season in Guam, which means a strong weather event could delay the ultimate implementation schedule.

Something else

Fathomdeep is committed to leaving no trace behind in the Mariana Trench. The descent vessel is designed to be fully self-contained and returned to the surface in the same condition. All objects included in descents will be securely housed in lockers or mesh bags to be exposed to the deep sea environment, but without the ability to exit the descent vessel. A different solution will be provided for objects that contain liquids or material that may exude into the water column, a sealed pressure bladder containing sea water will be arranged at surface level before descent such that the pressure at depth can be expressed without a loss of material. There will be one exception to this: a burial at sea. The delivery of ashes to the ocean‚Äôs deepest region represents a profound act of remembrance. When the full depth of the descent is reached, ashes will be released from a depth triggered container latch while a video feed is recorded of this release.
