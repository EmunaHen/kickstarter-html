Story

From Battlefield to Backyard.

The design is finished, the prototype has been built and tested, and an order of five units has been filled. Now, it's ready for you to enjoy!

I'm a blacksmith and metalworker with over a decade of experience. I've set out to create the ultimate outdoor cooking, camping, and survival tool. You'll want to carry this tool in the bed of your truck, under the driver's seat, or in your "bug-out" bag. It's solid AR-500 steel, measures 13.5" long, weighs about 2 pounds 4 ounces, and is just a bit over 8" at the widest. 

Since it resembles a Medieval berserker weapon, I've dubbed it the Crowsbeak Multi-Tool. Dual-wield for twice the enjoyment.

What makes such a simple design a "Multi-Tool?" Use it to:

  • Pick up pan and pot lids.
  • Lift grill grates, griddles, woks, and discadas.
  • Carry pots and pans while employing an extra handle.
  • Pick up bailed pots like coffee pots and Dutch ovens.
  • Safely move firewood without splinters.
  • Raid castles.
  • You can even use the handle to open bottles!

 Just ask this guy.

I'm a small, one-man shop. I'm here to tell you: manufacturing is not just a money game, it's a volume game. The larger that the first full-scale production run is, the less it will cost you (the customer) to purchase one. 

Our fundraising goal will produce 100 finished units while offering a reasonably priced, made-in-the-U.S.A. tool. I employ every ounce of my metalworking expertise to bring this project to life; CAD design, plasma cutting, sandblasting, grinding, and final finishing, which includes a food-safe oil-seasoned coating.

The Crowsbeak Multi-Tool. Tough as an axe, handy as a pocket knife.

Design Evolution.

The image above showcases the evolution from hand-forged concept to production-ready tool. The trickiest part was getting the angle and thickness of the pickaroon just right.

The head of the original pickaroon was forged by hand from mild steel, with a welded A2 steel bit, and adjusted to get the correct angle.

The prototype and production models involved reimagining some of the existing multi-tools that I make to create the final design. This was done with the help of CAD software.

The result is a lightweight, foolproof, and versatile design that combines centuries of metalworking techniques and time-tested tools.

Risks and challenges

Our biggest hurdle is material prices. Steel can be a volatile market at times, but I've calculated some of that risk into our final figure. In terms of producing a consistent product across hundreds or thousands of units, I'm fortunate to have a shortlist of suppliers that I work with on a regular basis who can handle large jobs. I'll also be here for you. If you need support or have a question about the tool once you receive it, reach out to me via Kickstarter or on Facebook at Z P L & Co. I've been in the business for a while and will always provide assistance and feedback to keep customers coming back!

Long-lasting design

It's made from one piece of solid, American steel. AR-500 is an alloy commonly found on heavy earthmoving equipment.

Reusability and recyclability

This tool will last a lifetime or longer! Occasional maintenance includes sharpening the picaroon end and periodic oiling to prevent rust.

Sustainable materials

Steel is one of the few materials that is completely recyclable. In fact, 92% of steel is recycled in North America each year, more than paper, aluminum, plastic, and glass combined.

Environmentally friendly factories

Designed, manufactured, and shipped from American soil. Our process follows all Federal and State regulations regarding safety and environmental protection.
