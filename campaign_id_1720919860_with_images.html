Risks and challenges

Athena is currently under active development, with a working MVP (Minimum Viable Product) already being built. However, as with any tech project, there are some potential risks and challenges: - Technical Scalability: As Athena grows, managing a large number of simultaneous users could stress the system. To address this, we are building the app on scalable cloud infrastructure (AWS) and conducting regular stress tests to ensure reliability. - AI Model Limitations: At launch, Athena will rely on a single AI model for academic support and index generation, which could initially limit some personalization features. Future updates are planned to integrate multiple AI models to increase flexibility and accuracy. - Time Constraints: Some advanced features, like full Word/Pages document integration within the roadmap, will not be available at launch due to development complexity. We are prioritizing the essential core features and planning phased updates based on user feedback and funding milestones. - Market Competition: The academic support tool sector is competitive, but Athena differentiates itself through its fully integrated, thesis-focused workflow, combining research, structuring, and time management in one platform ‚Äî something that current competitors do not offer. - User Adoption: Students may initially prefer traditional methods. To encourage adoption, Athena is being designed with an intuitive, user-friendly interface and clear onboarding processes to make it easy for students of all tech skill levels to use it effectively. We are committed to transparency, rapid iteration, and maintaining a user-first approach. With your support, we are confident Athena will become an essential companion for thousands of students writing their thesis.
