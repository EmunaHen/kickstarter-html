MindOne also comes with our custom iKKO AI OS — a lightweight companion to Android 15, designed to enhance focus and productivity.

After real-world testing, we realized most users are deeply familiar with Android and still rely on its app ecosystem. Instead of developing two fully isolated systems, which would require a reboot each time you switch and consume more memory, we chose a primary/secondary structure. This allows you to enjoy the best of both worlds.

In this setup, iKKO OS acts as a dedicated focus workspace, ideal for work and study. It’s completely under your control, protected by a separate password, and designed to be distraction-free. You can even drag your favorite Android apps into this space, creating a personalized environment tailored to your needs. With a long press of a physical button, you can switch seamlessly between the iKKO OS and Android, ensuring flexibility and ease of use.

It includes a full set of built-in AI tools: translation, voice notes, summaries, podcasts, and more — all ready to use out of the box with no subscriptions or setup required. Each tool automatically connects to the right AI model for your task, giving you smart, instant answers without juggling multiple apps or subscriptions.

MindOne runs on the MediaTek MT8781

A high-end 4G+ platform made for low-power, always-connected devices.

In a product as compact and integrated as MindOne, it’s the perfect fit. We didn’t set out to build an entertainment phone. We wanted something more stable, more efficient, more focused — a digital assistant you can carry anywhere, and rely on every day.

MT8781 is a platform specifically built by MediaTek for IoT and connectivity-first applications. It’s part of MediaTek’s new vSIM initiative in AI-driven environments, and we’ve developed MindOne on this platform with their direct technical support. The chip is optimized for low power consumption, stable connectivity, and global roaming — exactly what our “always-connected” MindOne experience is designed for.

It runs cool, sips power, and fits perfectly into a compact phone like this. Even with a 2200mAh battery, you still get all-day uptime. It stays quietly online in the background, and handles multitasking without breaking a sweat.

As the designer and creator of MindOne, we take full responsibility for making sure it’s built to last — and a joy to use every day, not just something that looks good on a spec sheet.

We know 5G sounds fast — but in a device this compact, it comes with trade-offs: more heat, faster battery drain, and reduced global stability. That’s not what we want for a phone that needs to stay reliably connected all day.

Here’s the truth: 5G still runs on 4G infrastructure with different base stations. And globally, 4G remains the mainstream. 4G+ (with carrier aggregation) offers stronger coverage, smoother roaming, and greater power efficiency.

That’s why we went with 4G+, it’s faster, saves power, and just works better around the world.

For what MindOne is made to do — smart, efficient, always-on connectivity — 4G+ is the right choice. In fact, in many places, it’s still the most reliable network available.

One common question we get: 

How does MindOne offer global connectivity — even for free?

The answer lies in the chip itself. With MT8781’s built-in vSIM support, we’ve pre-configured a global virtual network at the hardware level. So when you're traveling or working remotely, there's no SIM card to insert — just power it on and go online.

We didn’t choose this chip to chase benchmarks or headlines. We chose it because it helps us strike a rare balance: compact size, all-day battery, seamless connectivity, and reliable performance. It’s not about raw specs — it’s about building something that simply works, everywhere.
